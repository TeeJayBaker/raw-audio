# Architecture

**Purpose:** what network shapes have actually been used to produce raw audio at music sample rates, and what does each one bake in? Includes architectures that *emit* waveform but compute internally in a transformed domain — what matters is the I/O. Image-domain architectures are tracked when their structural pattern (patching, asymmetric enc/dec scaling, latent interfaces) ports to audio.

## Families

Three architecture families in the index:

### 1D conv U-Net at sample rate

CRASH, DiffWave, WaveGrad, WaveFM. Local features → downsample → bottleneck → upsample, FiLM-style time/noise conditioning. Receptive field is the design knob. Sequence length = `T_audio` (21 000 for 0.48 s @ 44.1 kHz; 24 000 for 1 s @ 24 kHz). Two sub-variants:

- **σ-FiLM symmetric** (CRASH). FiLM(σ) injection at every D/U block from an RFF(32)→512 noise-level embedding; channels 128/128/256/512/512; aggressive downsampling (2-2-3-5-5 = 300× on 0.48 s drums). Symmetric encoder/decoder.
- **HiFi-GAN-MRF asymmetric** (WaveFM). Thin downsampling encoder (4×1 ResBlock matrices, kernel-3) acts as a mel-conditioning controller; most capacity on the upsampling side (3×3 MRF ResBlock matrices with kernel-list `[3, 7, 11]` and dilation-list `[1, 3, 5]`-style — HiFi-GAN MRF block ported into a U-Net). Snake-beta activations from BigVGAN for periodic inductive bias. Sinusoidal-time embedding of `100·t` additively injected into downsampling hidden layers.

WaveFM has the first CPU number in this family: **xRT 0.64 @ 1-NFE, 19.5 M params** (Flow2GAN Tab. 7 re-benchmark, Xeon Platinum 8457C, batch 16, 1 s @ 24 kHz) — sub-real-time, consistent with the rest of the sample-rate-waveform family (RFWave 0.37, PeriodWave-Turbo 0.12 @ 4-NFE).

### Patched ViT

EPG, JiT, pMF on image; **[LinDiff](../../papers/2306.05708.md) and [WavFlow](../../papers/2605.18749.md) on raw audio** — two published instances spanning four orders of magnitude in scale (4-layer / 256-d / vocoder vs 30+ layer / 896-d / multimodal V2A+T2A). Linear patch embedding → stacked transformer blocks → linear unembedding. Patch size is the cost knob; token count drives FLOPs. Sequence length = `T_audio / patch_len`.

**Audio specifics from LinDiff** (22.05 kHz mel-vocoder, 64-sample patches = 2.90 ms, 4-layer × 4-head × 256-dim trunk, MLP via Conv1d):

- **Mel cross-attention is the conditioning channel.** Audio is query, mel hidden features (linearly projected to trunk dim) are K/V. Sits *between* self-attention and the FFN inside each block, with its own LN before. This is the patched-ViT-on-audio analogue of FiLM-mel on a U-Net.
- **TA-LN (Time-Adaptive LN)** = AdaLN's affine `(γ, β)` driven by the diffusion-step embedding, with **no zero-init residual gate**. Distinguishes from AdaLN-zero (which scales the *residual branch* by `α(t)` init=0). Trade: TA-LN is fine at depth 4 but at deeper trunks AdaLN-zero is the safer default. Mel goes through cross-attention, not the AdaLN.
- **Linear unpatchify produces patch-boundary artefacts**. Spectrogram shows horizontal-line harmonic noise at the patch period when no smoother follows — load-bearing motivation for a Post-Conv head (see below).
- **Patch size is monotonically quality-versus-cost** on audio (LinDiff Tab. 4 MOS at 100 steps: patch 64 → 4.18, patch 128 → 3.65, patch 256 → 3.34). No saturation; smaller patches strictly win on quality.

**Audio specifics from WavFlow** (16 / 44.1 kHz multimodal V2A+T2A, 200-sample patches = 12.5 ms / 4.5 ms, 11–21-layer MMDiT, 896-d, 14 heads, 624 M – 1.03 B params, 5 M video-text-audio pairs):

- **Conditioning is AdaLN-style on a multi-stream joint transformer** (MMDiT) — global `c_g` = mean-pooled CLIP-text + mean-pooled CLIP-visual + timestep embedding (semantic, *what*); frame-aligned `c_c` = Synchformer per-segment features upsampled to length `C` plus `c_g` (temporal, *when*). LinDiff uses cross-attention(mel); WavFlow uses AdaLN(`c_g`) + AdaLN(`c_c`) on joint+fused blocks. Different conditioning idioms for different modalities — when conditioning is at frame-rate (mel), cross-attention works; when it's at sub-frame-rate (CLIP/Synchformer at ~8 Hz), upsample-then-AdaLN is the working pattern.
- **Multi-rate RoPE alignment.** Audio and visual streams run at different frame rates (`C = 640` audio tokens vs `N_clip = 64` visual). Multiplying the visual stream's RoPE base frequency by `C/N_clip` (10× here) makes equal real-time moments receive matching rotary phases across streams — the audio-domain instance of the multi-rate-RoPE problem.
- **Thin output head: AdaLN + single kernel-7 1-D Conv** before unpatchify (cf. LinDiff's 3-Conv1d + TA-LVC Post-Conv smoother). No ablation on whether this is load-bearing at WavFlow's scale or whether the very high token count (640 / 1764) makes patch-boundary artefacts negligible. Open whether the smoother head can be thinner at large scale.
- **`D = 200` saturation point at 16 kHz** (12.5 ms per token, well below the ~25 ms human auditory resolution threshold). Patch-dim sweep `D ∈ {160, 200, 224, 256, 512}` at 1 M / 3 M data; D = 200 is the empirical sweet spot. Data scale and patch granularity interact: at 200 K, finer patches strictly help; at 3 M, `D = 512` hits a **capacity bottleneck** (degrades) — the only non-monotonic point in the sweep. Wider patches need more data to fill the per-token information density; narrower patches always cost more FLOPs.
- **Sync-aligned patch counts (`C = 576` or `768` = 3 × / 4 × Synchformer's 192) gave no measurable benefit** over generic fine-grained `D ≤ 256`. The multi-rate-RoPE fix above is sufficient — exact token-count alignment between conditioning and audio streams is not needed.

**Post-Conv smoother head** (LinDiff §3.2; project's `ConvSmootherHead`). Three plain Conv1d layers + 1 **Time-Aware Location-Variable Conv** (TA-LVC: a kernel-predictor MLP takes `(c, t)` and outputs per-time-step conv weights — position-varying kernels, the dynamic-convolution audio analogue), 32 hidden channels, Leaky ReLU activations. Explicit purpose: restore high-frequency detail and smooth patch boundaries after the linear unpatchify. Ablation: removing it drops MOS 4.12 → 3.84 at 3 steps and reintroduces the harmonic-line artefact. The Post-Conv hyperparameters (kernel sizes, dilations) are not specified in LinDiff and refer back to FastDiff / LVCNet.

### STFT-domain ConvNeXt

Vocos, RFWave, Flow2GAN, ComVo, WaveNeXt. STFT-derived input (mel or complex spectrogram) → ConvNeXt stack → readout to waveform. Sequence length = `T_audio / hop` per branch — an order of magnitude shorter than the sample-rate sequence. Trunk and readout vary along orthogonal axes (compose):

**Trunk axes:**

- **Single-branch + frequency-axis multi-band batching** (RFWave): slice the complex spectrogram into K freq subbands with small overlaps, stack into the batch dim, one shared backbone, AdaLN(subband-index), overlap loss for seam consistency. Subbands at inference decoded in parallel, independently — no AR low→high cascade.
- **Multi-branch at different `(N-FFT, hop)`** (Flow2GAN): K parallel ConvNeXt stacks at different time-frequency resolutions, each does its own iSTFT, outputs summed in waveform domain.
- **Complex-valued layers throughout** (ComVo): every layer is genuinely complex-valued — complex Conv (real filter `h = a + ib`, four real multiplies fused into one block-matrix multiply), complex LayerNorm (centre + decorrelate by 2×2 real/imag covariance), complex split-GELU, complex pointwise convs. Discriminator follows suit — cMRD (hinge applied independently to real and imag of each sub-discriminator output) replaces standard RVNN MRD; MPD stays real. Matched-memory ablation shows the win is from complex-domain modelling, not doubled per-parameter memory. Cost: ~5.7× slower on GPU at matched params; CPU not reported.

**Readout axis:**

- **iSTFT** (Vocos, RFWave, Flow2GAN, ComVo): predict (magnitude, phase) or equivalent complex spectrum from the final ConvNeXt output, iSTFT analytically to waveform.
- **Learned linear upsampler** (WaveNeXt): drop the iSTFT, predict waveform samples directly via `Linear(d → n_fft) → Linear(n_fft → hop_length, bias=False) → Reshape` — a 256× pixel-shuffle at 24 kHz, 512× at 48 kHz. Trunk byte-for-byte identical to Vocos; CPU cost indistinguishable. The diagnostic: time-domain GAN loss is degenerate over (mag, phase) under overlap-add — Vocos's predicted (mag, phase) don't match ground truth but re-analyze from the synthesized waveform as if they did. Direct waveform prediction matches the loss space. Higher MOS at every WaveNeXt-vs-Vocos condition tested; the gap widens at 48 kHz where Vocos collapses.

This family has the project's only published >real-time CPU benchmark (Flow2GAN xRT 4.85 @ 1-NFE, 78.9 M; WaveNeXt independently confirms xRT 10 for Vocos-class trunks at single-core, batch 1).

**The CPU axis runs cleanly along this taxonomy.** Sample-rate-waveform architectures (CRASH family + WaveFM + PeriodWave-Turbo + RFWave) sit at ~0.1–0.7× RT on CPU regardless of parameter count or NFE; STFT-domain ConvNeXt (Vocos, Flow2GAN) sits at 4–400× RT. The operating-sequence-length axis dominates — Flow2GAN at 78.9 M (hop-rate ~94/s @ 24 kHz) is ~8× faster on CPU than WaveFM at 19.5 M (sample-rate 24 000/s @ 24 kHz) despite 4× the parameters.

## Design axes

### 1D conv U-Net

- **Downsampling chain.** Sets receptive field, FLOPs per sample, input-length quantization. CRASH gets 300× downsample on 0.48 s drums; WaveFM uses 1024× cumulative upsampling for mel→24 kHz.
- **Conditioning injection.** FiLM at every block (CRASH σ-FiLM), additive into downsampling (WaveFM time), concatenation, cross-attention.
- **Encoder/decoder symmetry.** Symmetric (CRASH); asymmetric thin-encoder + heavy-decoder (WaveFM, HiFi-GAN-style). When the input is a *conditioning controller* (mel) rather than a noisy version of the output, asymmetry is justified — the decoder synthesises audio from the controller. Echoes EPG's image-side decoder-asymmetry lesson. Open whether asymmetry holds for unconditional waveform U-Nets (no controller-vs-synthesiser split).
- **σ vs t encoding.** Embed σ directly (RFF — CRASH) or t (sinusoidal/positional scaled — WaveFM scales `t × 100`). CRASH argues σ is better because the network skips learning σ(t); WaveFM uses t because FM's linear interpolant makes σ(t) trivial. All three currently-published choices (CRASH σ-FiLM, WaveFM additive t, RFWave sinusoidal-t + AdaLN subband) work.
- **Activation choice.** ReLU/SiLU vs **snake / snake-beta** (BigVGAN, WaveFM). Snake-beta `x + (1/(e^β + ε))·sin²(e^α·x)` gives periodic inductive bias for audio. WaveFM Tab. 4: small consistent SMOS gain. **Periodic activations + random-derivative-FM target are incompatible** (random `dx_t/dt` disrupts learned periodic patterns) — WaveFM's stated motivation for `x₁`-reparameterisation.
- **Dilated vs downsampling.** WaveNet/DiffWave-style stays at full resolution with dilated stacks; U-Net trades some receptive-field locality for smaller bottleneck compute. WaveFM uses dilated convs *within* upsampling ResBlocks (kernels 3/7/11, dilations 1/3/5).
- **MRF ResBlock structure** (HiFi-GAN family). ResBlock as a matrix indexed by `(kernel_list, dilation_list)`; multi-receptive-field coverage per block without changing spatial resolution. Survives across FM-vocoder generations (inherited by WaveFM verbatim).

### Patched ViT

- **Patch size** sets token count = `T/patch_len`. Compute is `O(tokens²)` for attention, `O(tokens · width²)` for the FFN. Picking patch length to hold token count at 256–1024 across (sample rate × clip length) keeps cost predictable.
- **Encoder/decoder split.** EPG: growing the decoder helps more than growing the encoder at fixed compute. **JiT skips the split entirely** (plain ViT, no asymmetry) and reaches competitive FID at 2 B params — asymmetric structure is an optimisation, not a necessity.
- **Conditioning.** adaLN-Zero on the decoder for t / class (DiT default); encoder-to-decoder residuals (SimpleDiffusion); **in-context class conditioning** (append `K = 32` repeated class tokens, MAR-style) gives ~1.2 FID on JiT-B/16 over adaLN-Zero alone. iMF replaces adaLN-zero with in-context (−⅓ params); pMF / TVM inherit. Family-wide default: in-context class tokens + adaLN-Zero(t) together.
- **Bottleneck linear patch embedding** (JiT). Two-stage linear: raw patch (`P²·C`-d) → `d'` → hidden_dim. `d' ≈ 64` improves JiT-B/16 by ~1 FID. Load-bearing at high resolution (JiT-B/64 on 1024² has raw patch dim 12 288 fed through 768-d hidden via 128-d bottleneck). For audio: needed only when `P·C > hidden_dim` (e.g. 10 s × 44.1 kHz stereo at 256-token budget = 6892-d/patch); usually unnecessary.
- **General-purpose Transformer ingredients** (JiT Tab. 4). SwiGLU + RMSNorm + RoPE + QK-norm + in-context class. Vanilla DiT → −42% FID on JiT-B/16 200ep. All five are LM-grade, dimension-agnostic, port to 1-D verbatim.
- **Latent interface (ELIT).** Insert `K` latent tokens between a short spatial head and tail; Read cross-attention pulls patch tokens into latents, most blocks operate only on `K`, Write cross-attention broadcasts back. Attention cost drops from `O(N²)` to `O(K²)` once `K ≪ N`; grouped to `O(N·K/G)` for Read/Write.

### STFT-domain ConvNeXt

- **(N-FFT, hop) per branch.** Sets time-frequency tradeoff and sequence length. Flow2GAN: `{(512,256), (256,128), (128,64)}`. ComVo: single `(1024, 256)`, Hann-1024, max-freq 12 kHz. WaveNeXt: `(1024, 256)` @ 24 kHz, `(2048, 512)` @ 48 kHz.
- **Branch count.** Vocos, ComVo, WaveNeXt: 1; Flow2GAN: 3. Multi-resolution split adds expressive power at fixed params.
- **Branch embedding dims.** Larger width on lower-frame-rate branches (Flow2GAN: 768 / 512 / 384) since they carry more spectral content per frame.
- **Four orthogonal axes** — frequency-axis subband batching (RFWave), multi-resolution branches (Flow2GAN), complex-valued layers throughout (ComVo), iSTFT vs learned-linear readout (WaveNeXt) — should compose; no paper tests the combinations. Trivially-combinable pairs: Flow2GAN + learned readout (each resolution branch emits waveform directly via `Linear(d → n_fft_k) → Linear(n_fft_k → hop_k)` and per-branch outputs summed); ComVo + learned readout (complex trunk feeds a real linear upsampler after a `real-imag → real` projection).
- **Real-channel-concat vs complex-valued layers.** Vocos/RFWave/Flow2GAN stack real and imag along channels and apply standard real Conv — complex algebra is not baked in. ComVo's CVNN bakes it in via complex Conv. Same number of real multiplies per output channel (four); cost difference is implementation overhead from PyTorch's `complex64` autograd graph, fixable via block-matrix scheme. Memory ~2× per parameter (each weight is a real-imag pair).
- **Phase quantization** (ComVo). One-line structured nonlinearity on complex features: `z_q = |z| · e^{i · (2π/N_q) · round(N_q θ / 2π)}` with `N_q ≈ 128` and STE on the round. Architecture-agnostic in principle; validated only inside ComVo's CVNN ConvNeXt; porting to a multi-branch RVNN (treat phase as `atan2(imag, real)` of channel-concatenated tensors) is untested. Conceptually incompatible with WaveNeXt's readout — there's no phase representation in the head to quantize.
- **Readout.** Either differentiable iSTFT (Vocos/RFWave/Flow2GAN/ComVo) or learned `Linear → Linear → Reshape` pixel-shuffler (WaveNeXt). The iSTFT bakes in the overlap-add prior; the learned linear is one extra `n_fft × hop_length` weight matrix (no bias, no activation, no overlap-add) at ~256 k params per branch — negligible vs the ConvNeXt trunk.
- **Condition encoder reuse.** Flow2GAN runs a separate ConvNeXt over the mel/codec input *once* and reuses across all FM sampling steps. Per-step cost = only the per-branch generative ConvNeXt; matters when N=4 must stay under a CPU budget. ComVo and WaveNeXt are 1-NFE so this trick doesn't apply.

## What we've learned

- **Operating sequence length dominates CPU cost** more than layer type, parameter count, or NFE. Flow2GAN at 78.9 M (hop-rate ~94/s) is ~8× faster on CPU than WaveFM at 19.5 M (sample-rate 24 000/s) at the same 1-NFE / 24 kHz / batch 16. Once you commit to STFT-frame operation, the NFE penalty is affordable; staying at sample-rate locks in sub-RT CPU regardless of NFE.
- **Aggressive downsampling works on short clips.** CRASH gets away with 300× on 0.48 s drums; WaveFM uses 1024× upsampling on the conditioning side. Strong prior — fine for percussive one-shots and vocoders, possibly limiting for sustained tones where long-range structure matters.
- **Asymmetric 1-D U-Nets work for conditional waveform.** WaveFM puts ~80% of params on the upsampling side and treats the downsampling side as a thin mel-conditioning encoder. Echoes EPG's image-side decoder-asymmetry lesson. Open whether this holds for unconditional U-Nets.
- **HiFi-GAN MRF ResBlocks survive the move from pure GAN to FM + post-training** (WaveFM inherits verbatim).
- **Snake-beta activation gives a small consistent audio gain** on U-Nets (WaveFM Tab. 4) but is incompatible with raw velocity prediction (random derivative disrupts periodic patterns).
- **Patching is the ViT analogue of downsampling.** Patch length of 64 samples / 22.05 kHz = 344 tokens/s (LinDiff sweet spot); patch=512 / 48 kHz = 94 tokens/s. LinDiff's Tab. 4 shows monotonic MOS loss as patch grows 64 → 128 → 256 at fixed trunk (4.18 → 3.65 → 3.34) — no saturation, smaller patches always win on quality at the cost of compute.
- **Linear unpatchify on raw waveform leaves patch-boundary artefacts** (LinDiff §3.2, Fig. 3). Horizontal-line harmonic noise at the patch period appears in the spectrogram when no post-trunk smoother is present. The Post-Conv head (3 Conv1d + 1 Time-Aware LVC, 32 ch in LinDiff) restores high-frequency detail and removes the artefact; ablation drops MOS 4.12 → 3.84. This is the load-bearing motivation for any conv-smoother head on a patched-waveform transformer. **Project's `ConvSmootherHead` is the direct lineage** — same role, simpler structure (3 Conv1d at k=13, no LVC). WavFlow uses a single AdaLN + Conv1d(k=7) at much higher token count and 1 B params with no ablation; the smoother-head requirement may relax at scale, or the artefact may persist below FD_PaSST resolution.
- **Patched-waveform-ViT scales from 4-layer vocoder to 1 B-param multimodal V2A+T2A** (LinDiff → WavFlow). Same skeleton (linear patch embed → transformer blocks → linear unembed + thin smoother). The architecture family is now validated end-to-end against latent-space competitors at production scale. Patch dim sits at the low end at low capacity / vocoder data (LinDiff `D = 64`, 4 layers) and at the middle of the sweep at high capacity / 5 M data (WavFlow `D = 200` saturation point, 11–21 layers). At fixed capacity, **patch-dim sweet spot moves with training-data scale** — sparse data wants finer patches; abundant data can absorb coarser ones until a hard capacity bottleneck appears (WavFlow `D = 512` at 3 M data degrades despite scaling improvements at `D ≤ 256`).
- **Time-Aware Location-Variable Conv (TA-LVC)** — small kernel-predictor MLP takes `(c, t)` and emits **per-time-step** conv weights, conditional on local mel and step. Audio's dynamic-convolution analogue; goes beyond FiLM (which only scales channels) by varying receptive-field weights along time. Originates in LVCNet / FastDiff; LinDiff's contribution is the time-conditioning.
- **Patch-size scaling at fixed FLOPs** (originated in JiT, inherited by pMF). Token count fixed at 256 across 256² / 512² / 1024² → GFLOPs essentially flat. **Audio analogue: hold token count constant across 1 s / 5 s / 10 s clips by growing patch size** — a hard requirement for usable interactive audio gen.
- **Prediction-target choice is architectural-grade in pixel space** (JiT). Predicting `x` vs `ε` flips JiT-B/16 from 10.14 to 379.21 FID at the same architecture and loss. Catastrophic failure is specific to **per-patch dim ≳ network hidden dim**. Whether the failure triggers on 1-D audio is open — audio patch dims (P·C ≤ 1000 at typical configs) sit well below image-domain failure thresholds.
- **Latent-interface gives a runtime budget knob** (ELIT). Patch-size scaling sets one token count per checkpoint; ELIT's `J̄` is chosen per inference call. ~3% overhead at max budget vs equivalent DiT.
- **Frequency-axis multi-band batching is essentially free** (RFWave). Works whenever the spectrogram is wide (high SR, mono or stereo) and time axis is short.
- **STFT-domain operation makes ConvNeXt the natural backbone** — short sequence, no need to downsample further; ConvNeXt's depthwise+pointwise structure fits under tens of M params.
- **Four orthogonal axes inside STFT-domain ConvNeXt now mapped, none combined** — freq-axis batching, multi-resolution branches, complex-valued layers, iSTFT vs learned-linear readout. Each adds expressive power along its own axis; should compose.
- **Complex-valued layers + complex discriminator beat real-valued matched-memory baseline** (ComVo). Win is structural (complex algebra baked in), not capacity. Cost: ~5.7× GPU slowdown, ~2× memory; CPU unknown. **Phase quantization** is a one-line, architecture-agnostic addition.
- **iSTFT readout is replaceable** (WaveNeXt). Drop it for `Linear → Linear → Reshape` with no change to trunk, training loss, or CPU cost — and MOS improves on every Hi-Fi-CAPTAIN condition tested (analysis-synthesis female/male, JETS-E2E-TTS female/male at 24 kHz, JETS-E2E-TTS at 48 kHz). The diagnostic that justifies it: time-domain GAN loss is degenerate over (mag, phase) under overlap-add — Vocos's predicted spectra don't match ground truth but re-analyze cleanly from the synthesized waveform. The gap widens at 48 kHz where the readout does 512× upsampling and Vocos collapses (MOS 2.39 vs WaveNeXt 3.82). **Generalises beyond vocoding**: any model with `representation → invertible-transform → loss` where the transform is loss-redundant can predict in the loss space directly.

## Open questions

- Does ELIT's Read attention concentrate on transients/onsets the way it concentrates on faces on images? If yes, audio gets automatic transient-vs-silence compute allocation for free.
- Smallest U-Net (channels, depth) that still hits competitive FAD on a drum-one-shot benchmark? No published small-net ablation.
- Does CRASH-style architecture extend to 1–10 s inputs cleanly, or does the 300× downsample need an extra level?
- **Stereo.** Every existing arch is mono. Naive channel-doubling has obvious phase-coherence pitfalls.
- Is a hybrid (small downsample chain + transformer in the bottleneck) worth trying at these sequence lengths, or is conv-only compute-optimal at music sample rates?
- **TA-LVC vs plain Conv1d in the smoother head.** LinDiff's Post-Conv uses 3 Conv1d + 1 TA-LVC; the project's `ConvSmootherHead` is plain Conv1d only. Does the location-variable kernel add measurable quality at the project's 48 kHz / patch=512 / depth-12 trunk, or is it structurally redundant with deeper attention?
- **Patch-density vs trunk-capacity vs data-scale tradeoff on raw waveform.** Three published audio data points sit at very different corners: LinDiff (4-layer / 256-d / `D = 64` / ~5 K speech-vocoder hours), WavFlow (11–21-layer / 896-d / `D = 200` / 5 M paired multimodal clips), project's `waveform_transformer.yaml` (12-layer / 512-d / `D = 512` / small drum-style corpus). WavFlow's sweep shows `D = 200` saturates at 1–3 M data and `D = 512` hits a capacity bottleneck at 3 M but is *better* than `D = 200` at 200 K. At the project's small-data corner, the expected sweet spot is `D` much smaller than 512 — likely closer to LinDiff's 64. Empirical sweep needed.
- **Does the complex-spectrogram-target trick survive without conditioning?** All STFT-frame architectures (Vocos, RFWave, Flow2GAN, ComVo) are conditional vocoders. For unconditional generation the model has to learn coherent phase from noise. ComVo's matched-memory ablation suggests the CVNN advantage may *widen* in the unconditional regime.
- **CPU cost of CVNN-everywhere** (ComVo). GPU is 5.7× slower than Vocos at matched params; CPU likely worse because complex kernels in BLAS are less optimised than float32.
- **Phase quantization on non-CVNN backbones** (ComVo's structured nonlinearity on `atan2(imag, real)` of channel-concatenated tensors). One-line layer; untested on Flow2GAN's RVNN.
- **Learned readout on multi-branch / subband-batched / complex-valued trunks** (WaveNeXt's `Linear → Linear → Reshape` head behind Flow2GAN / RFWave / ComVo trunks). Compositionally clean; untested. The 48 kHz Vocos collapse → WaveNeXt survival result predicts the readout swap matters more as sequence length / sample rate grow.
- Time-axis multi-band batching for short clips (chunk time into K segments the way RFWave chunks frequency). Useful if the time dim is the long one. Untested.
- Does pMF's patch-size scaling hold for variable audio clip length? Image: 256 tokens across 256²/512²/1024² → GFLOPs flat. Audio: 256 tokens across 1/5/10 s @ 22 kHz → patches of 86/430/860 samples.
