## TL;DR (project-relevant)

JiT — "Just image Transformers" — is the cleanest possible argument that **pixel-space diffusion can be done with a plain ViT, no extra losses, no tokenizer, no SSL pretrain, no perceptual loss, no GAN, no representation alignment** — provided you make one specific choice: **predict the clean image (`x`-pred), not the noise or the velocity**. Under the manifold assumption, clean data lies on a low-dimensional submanifold of the high-dim observation space while noise / velocity fill the full ambient space; a limited-capacity network can output a manifold-valued quantity but cannot output a full-dim noise tensor. That single decision flips JiT-B/16 at ImageNet-256 from **`ε`-pred FID 379.21 (catastrophic) → `x`-pred FID 10.14**, with the same loss, optimizer, schedule, and architecture. **JiT scales: B/16 4.37 → G/16 1.82 FID at ImageNet-256 (600 ep, CFG interval), and B/64 4.82 FID at 1024×1024** using a 12 288-dim raw patch fed through a 768-d Transformer with a 128-d bottleneck embedding — no SSL, no perceptual loss, no tokenizer. The paper's core lesson for raw-audio: when the network output is in the data space (the audio manifold) and the data manifold is low-dim relative to the ambient signal, **`x`-prediction is what unlocks high-dimensional pixel/sample-space generation without auxiliary machinery**. JiT is the *minimal* end-to-end self-contained pixel-space transformer — the structural baseline that pMF (2601.22158) and EPG (2510.12586) build on. It is *superseded by pMF for 1-NFE*, but for **multi-step pixel-space at low FLOPs / small params, JiT-B/16 (131 M, 25 GFLOPs, FID 3.66 at 600 ep) is still the cheapest competitive ImageNet-256 number in the wiki.**

## Why this paper matters

JiT is the **structural progenitor** of two papers that already anchor this project's design space:

- **pMF (2601.22158)** explicitly reuses JiT's `x`-prediction: pMF predicts `x_θ` and converts to velocity-space for the loss. **pMF without JiT's `x`-pred collapses to 164 FID at patch-dim 768** (paper's Tab. 2 of pMF). JiT is the dependency that makes pMF viable in pixel space.
- **EPG (2510.12586)** uses a ViT enc/dec on pixels; JiT shows the simpler "single plain ViT, large patches, no enc/dec split, no SSL" baseline that EPG's asymmetric structure improves on.

Reading JiT clarifies which structural moves in pMF / EPG are doing real work and which are inherited from JiT. **The bare ViT on raw pixels with `x`-prediction is the audio-translatable substrate**; everything pMF/EPG add (perceptual loss, SSL pre-train, encoder/decoder asymmetry, MeanFlow identity) sits on top of that substrate.

## The core argument

### Manifold assumption recast as a prediction-target question

The paper's thesis is one slide:

> Clean data `x ~ p_data` lies on a low-dimensional manifold `M ⊂ ℝ^D` (`d ≪ D`). Noise `ε ~ N(0, I)` and velocity `v = x − ε` (under linear interpolant `z_t = t·x + (1−t)·ε`) fill the *full* `ℝ^D`. A **finite-capacity network** can plausibly output a manifold-valued quantity (it only needs to encode `d` numbers' worth of information per token) but **cannot output a full-dim Gaussian** (would need `D` numbers' worth of information per token, exceeding network capacity).

Therefore: **predict `x`, not `ε` or `v`**. The loss can still live in whatever space; what matters is **what the network's direct output is**.

### Decoupling prediction space and loss space (Table 1 / Sec 3.2)

The nine combinations of {prediction space} × {loss space} (Tab. 1):

|  | `x`-pred | `ε`-pred | `v`-pred |
|---|---|---|---|
| `x`-loss | direct `x_θ` | `x` from `ε_θ` | `x` from `v_θ` |
| `ε`-loss | `ε` from `x_θ` | direct `ε_θ` | `ε` from `v_θ` |
| `v`-loss | `v` from `x_θ` | `v` from `ε_θ` | direct `v_θ` |

**JiT's recommended cell: `x`-pred with `v`-loss** (Eq. 6). Predicting on the manifold + loss-weighted by `1/(1−t)²` is empirically the strongest combination. The choice of *prediction* space matters; the choice of *loss* space is a re-weighting.

### Empirical signature (JiT-B/16, ImageNet-256, Tab. 2(a))

| | `x`-pred | `ε`-pred | `v`-pred |
|---|---|---|---|
| `x`-loss | **10.14** | 379.21 | 107.55 |
| `ε`-loss | 10.45 | 394.58 | 126.88 |
| `v`-loss | **8.62** | 372.38 | 96.53 |

`x`-pred works across **all three loss spaces**; `ε`- and `v`-pred fail catastrophically across all three loss spaces. **The catastrophic failure is a property of the *prediction* space**, not the loss.

Tab. 2(b) — JiT-B/4 at 64×64 (low-dim regime, 48-d patches well below 768-d hidden): all nine combinations work reasonably (3.46 – 6.20 FID). The catastrophic failure of `ε`/`v`-pred is **specific to the high-dim patch regime** — when the per-patch dim approaches or exceeds the network width, the network can no longer faithfully output `D`-dim Gaussian noise but *can* still output the manifold-valued `x`.

### Toy experiment (Sec 3.3, Fig 2)

`d=2` swiss roll projected through a random column-orthogonal `P ∈ ℝ^{D×d}` into `D ∈ {2, 8, 16, 512}`-dim space. A 256-dim 5-layer MLP trained with `v`-loss:
- At `D=2`: all three predictions work.
- At `D=8, 16`: `ε`-pred and `v`-pred degrade.
- At `D=512` (under-complete MLP relative to ambient dim): **only `x`-pred recovers the spiral**; `ε`/`v`-pred produce blob noise.

**The manifold story is mechanistic, not just empirical:** an under-complete network cannot retain enough information to output `D`-dim Gaussian samples but can produce a `d`-dim manifold-valued output.

## Architecture (Sec 4.1, Fig 3, Tab 9)

**Just a plain ViT on raw pixel patches.** Linear embed → N Transformer blocks → linear unembed. The diffusion transformer (DiT) directly applied to patches of pixels with `x`-prediction.

```
Input: H×W×3 image → (H/p × W/p) patches of (p²×3) pixels each
  └─ Linear embed (p²·3) → hidden_dim
  └─ + positional embed (RoPE), + class tokens (in-context, 32 of them)
  └─ N × { adaLN-Zero(t, class) → Attn (RoPE, qk-norm) → SwiGLU FFN, RMSNorm }
  └─ Linear unembed hidden_dim → (p²·3)
Output: x-prediction (denoised patches), reshape to H×W×3
```

**Patch sizes / configurations (Tab. 9 / Tab. 5):**

| | JiT-B | JiT-L | JiT-H | JiT-G |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Depth | 12 | 24 | 32 | 40 |
| Hidden dim | 768 | 1024 | 1280 | 1664 |
| Heads | 12 | 16 | 16 | 16 |
| Patch dim (raw, P²×3) | 768 (16²×3 @ 256) | 768 | 768 | 768 |
| Bottleneck dim `d'` | 128 (B/L), 256 (H/G) | | | |
| Params | 131 M | 459 M | 953 M | 2 B (est) |

**Patch / resolution scaling.** Fix sequence length = `16×16 = 256` across resolutions, grow patch size:
- 256² → patch=16, patch-dim 768, ~25 GFLOPs (B/16)
- 512² → patch=32, patch-dim 3072, ~26 GFLOPs (B/32) — **same params and compute as 256²**
- 1024² → patch=64, patch-dim 12288, ~30 GFLOPs (B/64)

**Token count constant across resolutions ⇒ compute roughly constant across resolutions.** This is the cost-flat-across-resolution property that pMF inherits and extends with perceptual loss; **the structural lever pMF cites for variable-clip-length audio is JiT's**.

### "Just Advanced" general-purpose Transformer ingredients (Tab. 4)

JiT incorporates language-model-grade Transformer best practices, **all general-purpose, none audio- or vision-specific**:
- **SwiGLU** FFN (vs vanilla MLP)
- **RMSNorm** (vs LayerNorm)
- **RoPE** positional embedding (vs learned absolute)
- **QK-norm** in attention
- **In-context class conditioning** — append 32 repeated class tokens to the sequence (vs DiT's adaLN-zero alone). Tab. 4: SwiGLU+RMSNorm baseline 7.48 → +RoPE+QK-norm 5.44 → +in-context-class-tokens **4.37** (B/16, 200 ep, FID-50K).

**TVM (2511.19797) and iMF (2512.02012) also use in-context conditioning** — this is the family-wide default now.

### Bottleneck linear embedding (Fig 4, Sec 4.4)

**Counter-intuitive design choice.** Rather than scaling hidden dim to match raw patch dim (768 at 256² → 3072 at 512² → 12288 at 1024²), JiT keeps hidden_dim modest and uses a **low-rank linear patch embedding**: raw patch (3072-d at 512²) → linear(`d' = 128`) → linear(768) → Transformer.

Even an aggressive bottleneck (`d' = 16` on a 768-d raw patch) does **not** cause catastrophic failure (Fig 4: 9.40 → 7.35 FID across `d' ∈ {16, 32, 64, 128, 512}`, vs 8.15 at no-bottleneck). **Reduction *helps*** by ~1 FID at `d' ≈ 64`, echoing the classical denoising-autoencoder / IB-method bottleneck argument: the manifold is low-d, so a low-d intermediate representation is sufficient and slightly regularising.

**Audio analogue:** 1-D raw audio patches at 22 kHz × 1 s / 256 tokens = 86 samples/patch × 1 channel = 86-d. *Below* JiT's smallest 768-d. Bottleneck embedding is a *natural fit at long clips* — 10 s clip at 22 kHz = 860 samples/patch, still well below image patch dims, no bottleneck needed at all. **JiT's bottleneck construction matters only when patch_dim ≫ hidden_dim** — for audio it may be unnecessary, modulo stereo and higher sample rates.

### Loss form (Sec 4.3)

```
L = E_{t, x, ε} ‖v_θ(z_t, t) − v‖²,    with v = x − ε
v_θ(z_t, t) = (net_θ(z_t, t) − z_t) / (1 − t)
z_t = t·x + (1−t)·ε,    t ∈ [0, 1]    [convention: t=0 noise, t=1 data]
```

Network direct output `net_θ` is `x_θ` (the denoised image); the conversion to `v_θ` happens **after** the network forward. Loss is standard FM `v`-loss. Algorithm 1 is six lines.

**Sampling (Algorithm 2):** Euler step in `v`-space. 50-step Heun by default.

### Noise-schedule shifting + time distribution

- Time sampler: **logit-normal** `logit(t) ~ N(μ, σ²)` with `μ = −0.8`, `σ = 0.8` (logit-normal pushes mass toward higher noise — Tab. 3 ablation). The schedule shift `μ` matters but does **not** rescue `ε`/`v`-pred from catastrophic failure (Tab. 3) — the manifold story is independent of `t`-sampling.
- For 512²: rescale `ε` magnitude by `2×` (i.e. `ε ~ N(0, 2²·I)`) to approximately maintain SNR across resolutions. For 1024²: rescale by `4×`. **One-line modification across resolutions.**

## What v2 likely changed vs v1

Markers I can read off the v2 PDF (dated 7 Jan 2026):

- **ImageNet 1024×1024 results with JiT-B/64** (Tab. 5) — patch=64, raw patch dim 12288, the most extreme high-dimensional pixel result. **Plausibly v2-added**, since the headline framing "JiT scales to 1024² with same params and compute" is more prominent in v2.
- **Cross-resolution generation experiment (Tab. 12)** — train at one resolution, generate at another by up/downsampling. JiT-G/16@256 → 512 by upsampling: FID@512 = 2.45 (vs 1.78 native 512 model); JiT-G/32@512 → 256 by downsampling: FID@256 = 1.84 (vs 1.82 native). **Downsampling is essentially free; upsampling loses some high-frequency detail.** This is consistent with a v2-added robustness experiment.
- **Classification-loss exploration (Tab. 11)** — adding an auxiliary CE loss on a pooled mid-layer feature improves FID 2.50 (JiT-L/16) vs 2.79 baseline. Section explicitly says *"we do **not** use this loss in the other experiments presented in this paper"* — this is a v2-flagged "future direction" rather than a main result.
- **Pre-conditioner failure analysis (Tab. 10 / Appx. B.2)** — formally shows that EDM-style pre-conditioners (`x_θ = c_skip·z_t + c_out·net_θ`) fail catastrophically in JiT's setting *unless* `c_skip ≡ 0` (which reduces to plain `x`-prediction). Strengthens the v1 "predict `x` directly" thesis with a formal demonstration that *anything* mixing `z_t` into the output (as EDM does) breaks the manifold story.
- **Toy experiment with `D=8, 16` interpolations** (Fig 2) — v2 likely added the intermediate dimensions; v1 may have only shown D=2 and D=512.

(All of the above is informed reading; I don't have v1 side-by-side.)

## Comparison points

### vs patch-based VAR / MaskGIT / MAR

VAR, MaskGIT, MAR all operate on **discrete tokens** from a VQ-VAE/VQ-GAN tokenizer + autoregressive or masked-prediction transformer. **JiT operates on continuous raw pixel patches** — no tokenizer, no quantisation, no codebook. JiT is **the continuous analogue of MAR / MaskGIT**: both keep the Transformer-on-patches structure, but JiT skips the quantisation/tokenization stage entirely.

Performance: JetFormer (autoregressive on raw pixels, no diffusion) sits at FID 6.64 / 2.8B params on ImageNet-256 (Tab. 7). FractalMAR sits at 6.15 / 848M. **JiT-B/16 at 131M beats both at FID 3.66** with diffusion training. The "raw pixels + diffusion + ViT" combination beats "raw pixels + autoregressive + ViT" at the same scale.

### vs latent diffusion (DiT, SiT, REPA)

DiT-XL/2 (675+49M, FID 2.27) operates on SD-VAE latents (8× downsampled, 4-channel). The full pipeline has a frozen VAE encoder + decoder *and* a VGG perceptual loss baked into the VAE training. **JiT replaces all of this with a single network and a single MSE loss.** The cost is FLOPs (JiT-XL-equivalent at 256² would be ~25 GFLOPs for B/16, but L/16 at 88 GFLOPs already matches DiT-XL/2 at 119 GFLOPs).

### vs flow-matching (SiT, RF, etc.)

JiT *is* flow matching — uses linear interpolant `z_t = t·x + (1−t)·ε`, `v`-loss objective, Euler/Heun sampling. The novelty is **not** in the diffusion/flow paradigm but in the **prediction target choice within that paradigm**: predict `x`, not `v` or `ε`. SiT / RF predict `v`; JiT predicts `x` and converts to `v` at the loss. This single choice is what makes pixel-space viable.

### vs other pixel-space efforts (ADM, RIN, VDM++, SimpleDiffusion, SiD2, PixelFlow, PixNerd)

These earlier pixel-space works all use either:
- **U-Net + over-complete channels** (ADM: `H×W×128` in the first layer, 1120 GFLOPs at 256²), or
- **Hierarchical patches / multi-scale** (SiD2, PixelFlow), or
- **External SSL backbones** (PixNerd uses DINOv2).

**JiT removes all of this.** Plain ViT, single resolution, `x`-pred, large patches. JiT-G/16 (383 GFLOPs) beats every non-SSL pixel-space baseline in Tab. 7 except SiD2-UViT/1 (1.38 FID at 653 GFLOPs — but uses dense convolutional structure).

### vs pMF (2601.22158) — what pMF adds to JiT

pMF = JiT + iMF's MeanFlow v-loss + perceptual loss on `x_θ` + (occasional) higher-order time differentiation. **Inherits JiT's `x`-prediction directly** — pMF's central design statement is "we use JiT-style `x`-prediction because predicting on the manifold beats predicting in the ambient space".

- JiT alone, multi-step (50 NFE Heun): FID 1.82 @ 600ep, JiT-G/16, 383 GFLOPs.
- pMF, 1-NFE: FID 2.22 @ pMF-H/16, 956M params, 271 GFLOPs.

**pMF supersedes JiT at 1-NFE; JiT remains the cleaner multi-step pixel-space baseline.** For an audio project that hasn't yet committed to one-step inference, **JiT is the structurally simpler starting point** — only `x`-pred + ViT + Heun, none of pMF's JVP / perceptual-loss machinery.

## How this translates to raw audio

JiT is exceptionally portable. The recipe has **zero image-specific machinery** — no augmentations, no perceptual loss, no SSL pretrain, no representation alignment, no VAE. Translation map:

| JiT component | Audio analogue |
|---|---|
| H×W×3 image | (T, C) waveform — `T` samples, `C ∈ {1, 2}` (mono/stereo) |
| 16×16×3 patch (768-d) | 1-D patch of `P` samples × `C` channels = `P·C`-d |
| `(H/p)×(W/p)` = 256 tokens | `T/P` tokens — pick `P` to land in 256–1024 tokens |
| Linear patch embed | Same — 1-D linear |
| RoPE 2-D positional | RoPE 1-D positional (simpler) |
| Logit-normal `t` sampler | Same |
| `v`-loss + `x`-pred | **Same — verbatim port** |
| 50-step Heun sampling | Same; or Euler for cheaper inference |
| Class tokens (32, in-context) | Genre / instrument / pitch / loop-bpm tokens, in-context, same count |
| adaLN-Zero(t, class) | Same |
| QK-norm, SwiGLU, RMSNorm | All general-purpose, port verbatim |
| EDM noise scaling 2× at 512² | At higher SR or longer clips, rescale `ε` to maintain SNR — same recipe |
| Bottleneck `d'`-embedding | Likely **unnecessary** at audio patch sizes (P·C ≤ hidden_dim in nearly all realistic configurations); apply only if scaling to multi-channel high-SR (e.g. stereo 96 kHz at long clips) |

**Concrete audio configs for short musical samples:**

| Sample length | SR | T (samples) | Patch P | Tokens | Patch dim |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 s | 22.05 kHz mono | 22 050 | 86 | 256 | 86 |
| 1 s | 22.05 kHz stereo | 22 050×2 | 86 | 256 | 172 |
| 5 s | 22.05 kHz mono | 110 250 | 432 | 256 | 432 |
| 10 s | 44.1 kHz stereo | 882 000 | 3 446 | 256 | 6 892 |

**The 10s stereo 44.1 kHz case has a patch dim (6 892) comparable to JiT-B/32's image patch dim (3 072) at 512² — well inside JiT's tested range, and where JiT's bottleneck embedding would actually become useful.** Token count is constant 256 across the full range — **same compute across clip length**, exactly mirroring JiT's image-resolution flatness.

### Audio-specific worries

- **Phase.** JiT predicts `x` — the raw waveform. The network learns waveform structure directly. Phase coherence is the network's responsibility (no codec floor, no STFT discard). This is exactly the project's design bet, but **no audio-domain `x`-pred-on-waveform paper exists** — the prediction-target analysis Section 3 conducts has not been replicated on audio.
- **Stereo.** L/R as two channels of a patch is the obvious move (JiT does R/G/B as 3 channels in a `16×16×3 = 768`-d patch). Whether L-R phase coherence is preserved by the linear patch embedding is empirical — but unlike STFT-domain methods, **there's no discarded phase to reconstruct**; the network outputs the joint (L, R) waveform directly.
- **Snake / periodic activations?** JiT uses SwiGLU + GELU. WaveFM (`x₁`-pred + snake-beta) found periodic activations critical *with* `x₁`-pred. JiT's GELU on `x`-pred works on images; whether snake-beta is necessary on audio `x`-pred is open.
- **No perceptual loss.** JiT explicitly avoids perceptual loss; pMF adds it for ~6 FID points. For audio, **MR-STFT loss** (multi-resolution STFT) is the standard analogue — but JiT's headline result is that **the raw MSE in waveform space is sufficient to reach competitive quality without perceptual loss**. This is testable: train an audio JiT with only `v`-loss in waveform space, no MR-STFT, and see if it matches a JiT + MR-STFT variant.
- **Inference cost.** JiT-B/16 at 25 GFLOPs is ~50× under a 1-second waveform at 22 kHz times 50 NFE Heun = ~1250 GFLOPs/sec audio gen. **For CPU real-time, would need 1-NFE (drop to pMF) or aggressive compression (smaller model, fewer steps).** JiT's natural sweet spot is **multi-step pixel-space**, not 1-NFE — for one-step audio, pMF's recipe is the direct port; for multi-step audio, JiT is.

### What ports verbatim

1. **`x`-prediction + `v`-loss** — the *single most important structural choice*. Untested on audio at scale.
2. **Token-count-constant-across-clip-length via patch-size scaling** — same logic, same constant compute.
3. **Plain ViT with general-purpose Transformer ingredients** (SwiGLU, RMSNorm, RoPE, QK-norm, in-context class conditioning) — language-model standard, dimension-agnostic.
4. **No SSL / no perceptual / no GAN / no representation alignment** — minimal-tooling recipe. The audio JiT baseline can be *more* minimal than the image JiT baseline (no LPIPS, no DINO available for audio at this quality anyway).

### What doesn't port directly

1. **2-D positional embeddings (RoPE 2D)** — trivially replace with RoPE 1-D.
2. **Bottleneck embedding** — probably unnecessary at audio patch dims; safe to omit at first.
3. **EDM noise rescaling rule** — `ε ~ N(0, σ²·I)` with `σ` tuned per (SR × clip length). The image rule (`2×` per resolution doubling) is dimension-specific; audio version is `σ ∝ sqrt(SR · clip_len / reference_dim)`. Likely needs tuning.

## Strengths

- **The most self-contained pixel-space recipe in the wiki.** No tokenizer, no SSL pretrain, no perceptual loss, no GAN. Six-line training algorithm.
- **`x`-prediction is now mechanistically motivated**, not empirical. The manifold-assumption argument explains *why* `x`-pred works where `ε`/`v`-pred fail catastrophically in high-dim pixel space — and matches the observation that low-dim regimes (Tab. 2(b)) don't see this failure.
- **The patch-size-scaling property** (constant compute across image resolution) is the most directly portable structural lever for variable-length audio.
- **JiT-B/16 (131M, 25 GFLOPs) is the cheapest competitive pixel-space ImageNet-256 number.** Strong baseline for CPU-constrained ports.
- **Bottleneck embedding** as a low-rank patch embedding is a small but real improvement (~1 FID on JiT-B/16); architecture-agnostic.
- **General-purpose Transformer ingredients adopted** (SwiGLU/RMSNorm/RoPE/QK-norm/in-context-class) are now family standard — JiT brings the LM toolkit to pixel diffusion.

## Limitations

- **Multi-step only.** JiT is trained as standard FM and inferred with 50-step Heun; 1-NFE collapses without pMF's MeanFlow + perceptual loss machinery. **For 1-NFE pixel audio, you need pMF, not JiT**.
- **No CPU benchmarks.** All GFLOPs measurements; no wall-clock CPU numbers for any of the configurations.
- **No audio data point.** JiT has not been tested on raw audio; the entire "this ports to audio" argument is constructed from the manifold-assumption motivation (which is *more* applicable to audio: the audio data manifold is much lower-d than the ambient `T`-sample space).
- **The Wasserstein-bound / distribution-level guarantee** that TVM (2511.19797) proves does *not* exist for JiT — JiT is a vanilla FM model and inherits whatever guarantees FM has.
- **No `ε`-pred / `v`-pred rescue.** The paper conclusively shows that prediction-space cannot be rescued by loss reweighting or pre-conditioning (Tab. 10) — but doesn't explore *hybrid* outputs (e.g. predict a low-rank `ε` projection plus `x`, or output `x` then add a residual `ε`-prediction head). Open direction the paper itself doesn't take.
- **Tab. 4's three Transformer improvements (SwiGLU/RMSNorm vs vanilla, RoPE+QK-norm, in-context class) are not individually ablated** — they're a stack. Unclear which is the biggest individual contributor.
- **Bottleneck embedding only shown on JiT-B/16** (Fig 4); no scaling test of `d'` on JiT-L/H/G.

## Open questions / things to test in this repo

1. **Audio `x`-pred vs `ε`-pred / `v`-pred catastrophic-failure replication.** Train a tiny 1-D JiT-equivalent at, say, 1 s @ 22 kHz mono, 256 tokens, 86-sample patches, and run JiT's nine-cell prediction × loss matrix. **Predict: `x`-pred succeeds across all losses, `ε`/`v`-pred fail catastrophically — at audio's natural patch sizes (86-d), the failure may *not* trigger** (per JiT-B/4 at 64×64, 48-d patches: all nine combinations work). **If audio patch dim ≪ hidden dim, the manifold argument may not bite the same way.** First-priority replication.
2. **Audio JiT at sample-rate-waveform.** Strip an audio JiT to bare minimum (no MR-STFT, no perceptual loss, no SSL conditioning) — does multi-step waveform generation work at CRASH-class param counts (~20–50M)?
3. **Bottleneck embedding for stereo / high-SR.** At 10 s × 44.1 kHz stereo (P·C = 6892), bottleneck embedding becomes naturally relevant. Test `d' ∈ {64, 128, 256}` analogous to Fig 4.
4. **JiT's monotonic-token-count-across-clip-length on a 1-D backbone.** Confirm GFLOPs flat as P grows with T (image: 25 / 26 / 30 GFLOPs across 256 / 512 / 1024). Audio version: same JiT model serving 1s / 5s / 10s clips at fixed CPU latency.
5. **JiT + MR-STFT loss.** Add MR-STFT as a perceptual loss on `x_θ` (the natural pMF-style addition). Does it help raw-waveform `x`-pred the way LPIPS+ConvNeXt-V2 helps pMF (~6 FID)?
6. **Snake-beta vs GELU on audio JiT.** Periodic activation interaction with `x`-prediction is the obvious test — WaveFM (`x₁`-pred + snake-beta) is the audio precedent, but on a U-Net, not a ViT.

## Position in the project

JiT is the **structural baseline** for raw-waveform Transformer audio gen. It is:

- **More general than pMF** (which adds MeanFlow + perceptual loss) — JiT is the substrate; pMF is JiT + 1-NFE-machinery.
- **Cleaner than EPG** (which adds asymmetric enc/dec + SSL pretrain) — JiT is the simplest viable pixel-space transformer; EPG is JiT + scaling tricks.
- **Less explored than either at audio scales** — neither pMF nor EPG includes a "JiT-only" ablation isolating the `x`-pred contribution.

**Most-directly-usable read:** the audio JiT baseline (`x`-pred + 1-D ViT + RoPE + SwiGLU + in-context-class + `v`-loss + 50-step Heun) is the **first thing to try** for raw-waveform short-clip generation. If it works, pMF (one-step) and EPG (SSL-pretrain) are independent compute-knob upgrades. If it doesn't, the manifold-assumption argument doesn't transfer in 1-D and the project needs an audio-specific prediction-target analysis.
