# Vendi Score — reference-free diversity via kernel entropy

## TL;DR (project-relevant)

A **reference-free, modality-agnostic diversity metric** that turns one user-chosen similarity kernel `k(x,x')` (with `k(x,x)=1`) into an "effective number of distinct samples". Given $n$ samples, build the $n\times n$ kernel matrix $K$, take the eigenvalues $\lambda_i$ of $K/n$, and define

$$\text{Vendi}(x_1,\dots,x_n) = \exp\!\Bigl(-\sum_{i=1}^n \lambda_i \log \lambda_i\Bigr).$$

That's it. No reference dataset, no labels, no probability model. The score is "the population would look this diverse if it had this many completely-dissimilar members". For our raw-audio CPU project, Vendi is the natural **companion to FAD/MIND**: it answers "did the generator collapse?" where FAD answers "do generations look like the training distribution?". The combination **(FAD↓ + Vendi↓)** is the textbook signature of adversarial-induced diversity collapse — a one-step GAN that learns to produce a small set of polished outputs will improve on FAD while Vendi drops sharply, and the pair lets us see it.

## What problem this solves

Existing diversity metrics fall into three traps, all of which hit us harder in audio than in images:

1. **Reference-based** (FID coverage term, Precision/Recall, FAD): require a reference dataset. Fine when one exists, but locks you into measuring "diversity relative to training". You can't ask "how diverse are these 500 generations among themselves?" without a held-out set.
2. **Classifier-based** (Inception Score, num-modes/NoM): require a labelled set and a trained classifier. Audio doesn't have a clean ImageNet equivalent; the labelled-categorical view is wrong for music anyway.
3. **Average-similarity** (IntDiv, n-gram diversity, pairwise-BLEU): the natural reference-free baseline. But average dissimilarity **saturates** as diversity grows (Fig 1a — IntDiv goes 0.5→0.67→0.75 while a sensible "effective number of modes" goes 2→3→4), and **ignores feature correlations** (Fig 1b/c — composing two independent similarities should raise diversity, IntDiv doesn't notice).

Vendi addresses all three: reference-free, no classifier, and the entropy-of-eigenvalues form captures both the *number* of clusters and *within-cluster spread*, which average-similarity flattens.

## The score, formally

**Definition.** Let $k:\mathcal X\times\mathcal X\to\mathbb R$ be positive semidefinite with $k(x,x)=1$. Build $K_{ij}=k(x_i,x_j)$, normalize $K/n$ (eigenvalues sum to 1 because $\text{tr}(K/n)=1$), and

$$\text{VS}_k(x_1,\dots,x_n) = \exp\!\Bigl(-\sum_i \lambda_i \log \lambda_i\Bigr) = \exp\bigl(-\text{tr}(K/n \cdot \log K/n)\bigr).$$

Equivalently: the **exponential of the von Neumann entropy** of $K/n$ (quantum stat-mech connection — $K/n$ plays the density-matrix role), or the **effective rank** of $K$ in Roy & Vetterli's sense.

**Key properties** (Theorem 3.1):

- **Effective-number.** $k(x_i,x_j)=0\ \forall i\ne j\Rightarrow\text{VS}=n$ (all dissimilar). $k(x_i,x_j)=1\ \forall i,j\Rightarrow\text{VS}=1$ (all identical). Linear in the number of modes when modes are equiprobable and orthogonal under $k$.
- **Identical-element collapse.** Duplicates contribute exactly nothing — a sample of 90 copies of A + 10 copies of B has the same VS as the probability-weighted version with $p=(0.9,0.1)$ on two distinct points.
- **Partitioning.** If the sample splits into $S_1,\dots,S_m$ that are mutually $k$-orthogonal, $\text{VS}(\text{union}) = \exp(H(p_1,\dots,p_m))\prod_i \text{VS}(S_i)^{p_i}$ — Shannon-entropy of cluster sizes times geometric mean of within-cluster VS. Clean intuition for "diversity = how many clusters × how diverse within each".
- **Permutation symmetry.**

## Tail-sensitive: why eigenvalues, not average similarity

Average pairwise similarity (IntDiv = $1 - n^{-2}\sum_{ij}k(x_i,x_j)$) is **linear in $K$** — it's just $1 - \mathbf 1^\top K\mathbf 1/n^2$. Vendi is the **entropy of the spectrum**, which weights small $\lambda$'s logarithmically. Concretely:

- A long tail of small modes (many rare-but-real variations) **boosts Vendi** but barely moves IntDiv, because each pairwise entry is still close to its average.
- A few duplicated dominant modes (a generator producing 5 polished outputs repeated 1000 times) **crushes Vendi** to ≈5 but leaves IntDiv near the original.

The paper's Table 4 makes this concrete on molecules: an HMM that produces visible duplicate clusters scores **VS=250.9** vs. real-data **VS=403.9** (a 38% gap), while IntDiv is **0.871 vs. 0.855** — backwards and indistinguishable. This is the diagnostic property we want for adversarial-trained audio models.

## Cost

$O(n^3)$ from the eigendecomposition is the naive cost. When $k(x,x') = \langle\phi(x),\phi(x')\rangle$ for a $d$-dim feature ($d\ll n$), use $K = X X^\top$ and eigendecompose the $d\times d$ Gram $X^\top X$ instead: **$O(d^2 n + d^3) = O(d^2 n)$** — same as FID/FAD covariance computation. With CLAP ($d=512$) or VGGish-style ($d=128$) audio embeddings this is trivial — eval-batch cost dominated by the embedding network, not the metric.

Empirical sample-complexity rate: $1/\sqrt n$ for the kernel-entropy estimator (Bach 2022). For most practical embeddings, a few thousand samples is plenty.

## Mode-collapse diagnostic — the key experiment

Section 4.3 (Table 1) is the paper's most compelling use case for us. On **StackedMNIST** (1000 modes by construction), they evaluate two GANs **explicitly designed to capture all 1000 modes** — Self-conditioned GAN and PresGAN — with the standard number-of-modes (NoM) metric and with Vendi:

| Model | NoM | Mode-Div (entropy of mode dist) | VS |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-cond. GAN | **1000** | 921.0 | 746.7 |
| PresGAN | **1000** | 948.7 | 866.6 |
| Original (real) | 1000 | 950.8 | 943.7 |

NoM saturates at the maximum and is blind. Vendi sees that **neither GAN matches real-data diversity**, and ranks them — PresGAN is more diverse than Self-cond. GAN even though both "capture all modes". Mode-Div (the entropy of the predicted-class distribution) catches most of the gap, but Vendi catches additional within-mode lack-of-diversity that Mode-Div can't see by construction.

**This is the signature pattern we'll see in audio.** A one-step adversarially distilled model can hit excellent FAD (its outputs look in-distribution) while collapsing onto a small library of polished templates. NoM-style metrics don't apply (no labels); FAD doesn't notice (matching first two embedding moments doesn't require diversity); Vendi sees it directly.

## Image-generator results (Table 2)

On CIFAR-10 / ImageNet-64 / LSUN Cat / LSUN Bedroom they evaluate VDVAE, DenseFlow, IDDPM, StyleGAN2, ADM, RQ-VT against the originals using cosine similarity on Inception-2048 features (so VS is on the same representation as FID/Prec/Rec — same eval surface, different metric).

- On lower-resolution datasets VS broadly agrees with FID/Recall — diffusion wins.
- On LSUN, where **ADM has the best FID and best Precision/Recall**, **VS is *lower* than for StyleGAN2 and RQ-VT** — meaning ADM is producing in-distribution but lower-internal-diversity samples than the alternatives. This is exactly the "high fidelity, lower variety" failure mode that FID under-penalizes (precision goes up faster than recall captures the loss).
- **No model matches real-data VS.** All generators are less diverse than their training data on this embedding.

The combined pair (FID, VS) reveals trade-offs that neither alone shows.

## Connections

- **DPP-likelihood.** $|K_X| = \prod_i\lambda_i$ vs. Vendi's $\exp(-\sum\lambda_i\log\lambda_i)$ — both are spectral functions of $K$, but DPP-likelihood is zero whenever any pair is identical (geometric mean), which is too brittle for sampled empirical sets. Vendi's entropy form is well-behaved with near-duplicates.
- **Effective rank** (Roy & Vetterli 2007): identical form on singular values of a feature matrix. Vendi is effective rank applied to a kernel matrix and dressed up with the ecological interpretation.
- **Spectral clustering.** Both use $K$'s eigenvalues. Spectral clustering reads off discrete structure (multiplicity of 0 in graph Laplacian = #components); Vendi gives a continuous diversity score even for fully-connected $K$.
- **Quantum stat-mech.** $K/n$ as density matrix $\rho$, Vendi as $\exp(\text{von Neumann entropy})$. Useful as intuition: orthogonal pure states → max entropy; rank-1 → zero entropy.

## How this translates to raw audio

Vendi is **already modality-agnostic** — the only choice is the kernel. For our project the translation reduces to picking $k$.

**Candidate kernels for audio:**

1. **CLAP embedding cosine.** $k(x,x')=\cos(\psi_\text{CLAP}(x),\psi_\text{CLAP}(x'))$ (clipped to $[0,1]$ or shifted-rescaled so $k(x,x)=1$). Semantic/text-aligned diversity — captures "are these the same kind of sound?". Cheap, $d=512$.
2. **MERT / AudioMAE features.** SSL audio embeddings, more music-aware than CLAP. Same construction. Probably the right default for short musical samples.
3. **VGGish / PANNs.** Older, smaller ($d=128$), legacy baseline — same family as standard FAD's feature space, so a Vendi-on-VGGish pairs naturally with FAD.
4. **Pixel-equivalent: raw-waveform or mel-spectrogram cosine.** Captures low-level structure (timbre, loudness pattern) but not semantic similarity. Useful as a complement — the paper shows in Sec 4.6 that pixel-vs-Inception kernels give very different VS rankings, both informative.

**Practical recipe:**

- Generate $n\approx 2$–$5$k samples from the model.
- Embed under one or two kernels (a "semantic" one like CLAP/MERT and a "perceptual" one like pixel/mel).
- Compute Vendi via the $X^\top X$ trick: trivial.
- Pair with FAD (or MIND, see [2605.06797](2605.06797.md)) for fidelity.

**Reporting convention for this repo:** every model gets `(FAD↓, Vendi_CLAP↑, Vendi_mel↑, CPU-RTF)` as the headline tuple. The reference for Vendi is the dataset's own Vendi on the same kernel — a generator that matches the training-set Vendi is doing well.

**Phase/sample-rate caveats** — same as for MIND: Vendi sees only what the embedding sees, so phase and inaudible micro-structure don't enter. For raw-sample-space work this is fine for diversity (different waveforms get different embeddings), but it means Vendi can't catch "all samples are phase-locked variants of one another" if the embedding is phase-invariant. Mel kernel partially helps; raw-waveform kernel (e.g. normalized cross-correlation) gives the most direct sample-space view but is sensitive to time alignment.

## Limitations (paper's own, plus ours)

- **Cannot distinguish quality from diversity** — random Gaussian noise gets very high Vendi on most kernels. **Must pair with a fidelity metric.** This is non-negotiable in our reports.
- **Kernel choice is everything.** Too-permissive kernel (everything looks similar) → low VS, looks bad. Too-strict kernel (nothing looks similar) → VS=n, looks artificially good. A bad kernel produces a bad metric. The paper is unusually honest about this.
- **Skew biases.** Kernels with unbalanced response (e.g. SimCSE on Amazon reviews scores 5-star reviews as low-diversity because of strong-sentiment regularity) can produce confounded scores. Sanity-check kernels against known cases before adopting.
- **Computational cost rises with sample size** if no embedding exists — Nyström approximation available but introduces error.
