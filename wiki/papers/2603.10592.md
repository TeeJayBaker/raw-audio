# TL;DR — why this matters for raw-audio CPU gen

Theory companion to **Drifting (2602.04770)** and the third entry in the spectral / variational analysis line started by [2603.09936](2603.09936.md) and [2603.12366](2603.12366.md). The single content claim worth taking away:

> **The Drifting field is *literally* the Wasserstein gradient flow of the *forward KL* between *KDE-smoothed* densities. Once you see this, the entire space of `f`-divergences becomes a tunable knob — each one a different drifting recipe with a different built-in failure mode.**

Two practical consequences for this project:

1. **The `V = −∇ δF/δq` template (already in the wiki from 2603.09936) now has a *recipe book*.** Pick a convex `f` with `f(1)=0`; the WGF velocity at the KDE level is `v_f^{kde}(x) = −∇ f'(q_kde(x)/p_kde(x))`. Forward-KL gives the original Drifting drift `∇ log p_kde − ∇ log q_kde`. Reverse-KL up-weights *high-data-density* regions (precision-forcing — avoids blur). `χ²` up-weights *high-generator-density* regions (penalises spurious mass — avoids mode collapse). MMD is the `L²`-density-difference special case. **Every existing drift in the family is one row of Table 1.**
2. **Mixed gradient flows are legal.** Convex combinations of valid divergences yield valid divergences (Theorem 4.12); their WGF velocity is the same convex combination of the individual velocities. The paper's proposed recipe is `V_mix = α · (p_kde/q_kde)(∇ log p_kde − ∇ log q_kde) + β · (q_kde/p_kde)(∇ log p_kde − ∇ log q_kde)` — **reverse-KL + χ²** — which simultaneously avoids mode blurring (reverse-KL's contribution) and mode collapse (χ²'s contribution). Validated on a Swiss-roll toy where the original Drifting model and the L²/MMD variant both blur, while the rev-KL + χ² mixture quickly explores all modes.

For raw audio this is the **divergence-design surface unlocked**. We already have the WGF machinery (W-Flow gave us the Sinkhorn-divergence instantiation that beats everything on ImageNet); this paper gives us:

- The full `f`-divergence menu, with explicit weighting interpretations (when each one fails / succeeds).
- A mode-collapse-vs-blur dial (the `α` vs `β` mix between reverse-KL and χ²) that is the **non-adversarial structural fix complementary to W-Flow's gen→gen self-transport**.
- A clean Riemannian extension that **drops kernel assumption K4 (differentiability)** because compact-manifold boundary terms vanish — relevant if we ever do drift on the unit hypersphere of a normalised audio-φ space (JEPA-style, which the paper itself recommends as the deployment target).

What it does *not* give us: experiments at any scale beyond 2D Swiss roll. Theory contribution; everything else is "future work" in the conclusion. So the value is **recipe-book + framing**, not "this beats W-Flow".

# What problem it solves

Drifting (2602.04770) is empirically strong (1-NFE SOTA at the time) but its identifiability proof needs extra smoothness assumptions, its kernel choice is grid-searched, and **why the recipe works is not derivable from first principles** in the original paper. Subsequent theory papers chip at this:

- 2603.09936 derives the score-difference identity under a *Gaussian* kernel and explains kernel preference via Landau damping.
- 2603.12366 swaps softmax-row normalisation for full Sinkhorn and reframes the drift as the WGF of the Sinkhorn divergence.
- This paper (2603.10592) sits one level higher: **Drifting is one instance of WGF on *any* `f`-divergence approximated by KDE, and choosing the divergence is the design surface.** Forward-KL recovers Drifting; reverse-KL + χ² produces a new mixed drift; MMD is a separate `L²` axis but plugs into the same framework via `L²`-density difference.

Concurrent / overlapping work the paper explicitly distinguishes:

- **Li & Zhu (2602.20463)**: reinterprets Drifting via long-short flow-map semigroup decomposition. Connects to closed-form flow matching. **Does not identify the KDE↔gradient-flow connection.** Same starting paper (Drifting), different conclusion.
- **Belhadji et al. (2502.10600)**: unifies MMD gradient flows with mean shift. **Does not extend to `f`-divergences.** Subsumed by this paper's framework.
- **Yi et al. (MonoFlow 2023)**: connects `f`-GANs to WGFs but *requires a discriminator* to estimate the density ratio. This paper does it via KDE — no adversarial training.

The unification claim is "all four are different angles on the same WGF-on-smoothed-densities object". This paper's distinct angle is the `f`-divergence parameterisation and the mixed-flow theorem.

# Method

## Foundation: KDE smoothing preserves identity, induces regularity

Assumption 4.1 / Appendix A — kernel regularity:

- **K1 Characteristic**: mean embedding `µ ↦ ∫ k(·, y) dµ(y)` is injective on `P(ℝ^d)`. Equivalent to "KDE-matching ⇒ distribution-matching".
- **K2 Uniform gradient bound**: `M_k = sup ‖∇_x k(x, y)‖ < ∞`. Dominating function for Leibniz interchange.
- **K3 Strict positivity**: `k(x, y) > 0` everywhere. Keeps `q_kde > 0`, log-ratios well-defined.
- **K4 Differentiability**: `x ↦ k(x, y) ∈ C¹` for every `y`.

**Gaussian kernel satisfies all four. Laplace kernel violates K4** at `x = y` (Appx J). The Laplace kernel's empirical success in the original Drifting paper is technically outside this framework — gradients are only weakly defined, leading to numerical jittering near data points (paper §5.1). **This is a flag for any audio drift port that picks Laplace** (which 2603.09936's Landau-damping analysis recommends for high-frequency-heavy data).

Under K1–K4, regardless of how irregular `p` and `q` are:

- `p_kde, q_kde ∈ C¹` and strictly positive (Theorem 4.2).
- Log-ratio `log(p_kde/q_kde)` is well-defined and `C¹`. The `f`-divergence machinery applies at the KDE level with **no regularity assumed on `p` or `q`**.
- KDE injectivity (Prop 4.3): `µ_kde = ν_kde` pointwise ⇒ `µ = ν`. **Distribution-matching at the KDE level is sufficient.** This is the load-bearing fact: optimising any divergence between `p_kde` and `q_kde` to zero implies `q = p`.

## Engine: `f`-divergence WGF at the KDE level

`f`-divergence `D_f(ρ‖π) = ∫ π · f(ρ/π) dx` for convex `f` with `f(1) = 0`. First variation `δD_f/δρ = f'(ρ/π)`; WGF velocity is the gradient of the first variation:

```
v_f^{kde}(x) = −∇ f'(q_kde(x) / p_kde(x))                  (Eq. 5)
```

By chain rule this is **always factorisable** as

```
v_f^{kde}(x) = w(x) · (∇ log p_kde(x) − ∇ log q_kde(x))     (Remark 4.6)
```

where the *density-ratio weight* `w(x)` is divergence-specific:

| `f(u)` | Divergence | `f'(u)` | Weight `w(x)` | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `u log u` | forward KL `KL(q‖p)` | `log u + 1` | `1` | uniform attention — original Drifting |
| `−log u` | **reverse KL** `KL(p‖q)` | `−1/u` | `p_kde/q_kde` | **up-weights high-`p` regions → precision-forcing → avoids blur** |
| `½(u−1)²` | **χ²** `χ²(q‖p)` | `u − 1` | `q_kde/p_kde` | **up-weights high-`q` regions → coverage-forcing → avoids mode collapse** |
| `½‖m^q_k − m^p_k‖²_H_k` | MMD² (not an `f`-div) | — | — | `∇(p_kde − q_kde)` — `L²` density gradient |

The common factor `∇ log p_kde − ∇ log q_kde` is *exactly* the original Drifting field divided by `h²` (Theorem 4.9 — the core equivalence). What differs across divergences is the **point-wise reweighting** of that field.

**This is the synthesis the wiki has been missing.** 2603.09936 showed Drifting = forward-KL WGF; this paper says *forward-KL is one row of a 4-row table* and gives the explicit weight pattern for each row.

## Mixed gradient flows (the practical contribution)

Theorem 4.12: if `D_1, D_2` are valid divergences (`D_i(q‖p) ≥ 0`, `= 0 ⇔ q = p`) and `α, β > 0` with `α + β = 1`, then `D_mix = αD_1 + βD_2` is a valid divergence with:

- (a) `D_mix(q‖p) ≥ 0`, `= 0 ⇔ q = p`.
- (b) `v_mix = α v_1 + β v_2` (WGF velocities add linearly under convex combination).
- (c) `d/dt D_mix[q_t] ≤ 0` along the mixed flow.

**Practical mixed drift (Eq. 9):**

```
V_mix(x) = α · (p_kde/q_kde) · (∇ log p_kde − ∇ log q_kde)
        + β · (q_kde/p_kde) · (∇ log p_kde − ∇ log q_kde)
```

corresponding to `D_mix = α · KL_kde(p‖q) + β · χ²_kde(q‖p)`. The qualitative story (their words, rephrased):

- **Reverse-KL term** (factor `p_kde/q_kde`): large when *real* density is high but generator density is low. Drives precision-forcing — particles get pulled hard toward modes where real data sits. Avoids **mode blurring** (the dirty-modes / inflated-variance failure of mean-seeking forward-KL flows).
- **χ² term** (factor `q_kde/p_kde`): large when *generator* density is high but real density is low. Penalises spurious generator mass — particles get pushed *away* from regions the generator over-covers. Avoids **mode collapse** (the all-particles-flock-to-nearest-mode failure of reverse-KL flows on their own).
- Their combination "reconciles mode-seeking and mode-covering behaviours."

This is a **complementary structural fix to W-Flow's gen→gen self-transport** (W-Flow uses two-batch Sinkhorn to enforce a marginal-coverage constraint; this paper achieves a similar end by mixing flows with opposite failure modes). Both are non-adversarial. Both attack mode collapse + blur simultaneously. Composable in principle (Sinkhorn-WGF + reverse-KL term as an extra additive contribution — speculation, untested).

## Riemannian extension (§4.7)

On a compact Riemannian manifold `M` without boundary (canonical example: `S^{d-1}`, used by JEPA-style normalised feature spaces and by Drifting empirically in semantic space):

1. **Boundary terms vanish unconditionally.** The energy-dissipation inequality `d/dt D_f ≤ 0` (Eq. 6) drops the tail-decay assumption it needs on `ℝ^d`. Just connectedness and `f` strictly convex with `f(1)=0` suffice.
2. **Kernel design loosens.** Assumption K4 (differentiability of `x ↦ k(x,y)`) is replaced by manifold analogues K1_S–K4_S; admissible kernels include:
   - **von Mises–Fisher** `k_κ(x, y) = exp(κ x^T y)` — spherical Gaussian, characteristic.
   - **Spherical logarithmic kernel** (Prop J.5) — polynomial (inverse-distance) weighting, analogous to Euclidean IMQ kernel. **Heavier tails, better global coverage** than vMF.

For audio: **only directly relevant once we operate in a normalised audio-φ space** (`‖φ(x)‖ = 1`). JEPA / SimCLR-style audio encoders produce exactly this; AudioMAE / EnCodec do not by default. The Riemannian extension is the natural target if we end up porting Drifting onto a JEPA-style audio backbone — the paper explicitly cites JEPA as the deployment target in its future-work conclusion.

## Identifiability (Theorem 4.7)

Cleanest statement to date: `v_f^{kde} ≡ 0 ⇒ q = p`, with no smoothness assumed on `p` or `q`. Two-step proof:

- **Step 1**: `v_f^{kde} = 0` ⇒ `∇ f'(q_kde/p_kde) = 0` ⇒ (by strict convexity of `f`, hence injectivity of `f'`) `q_kde/p_kde = λ` (constant) everywhere.
- **Step 2**: Integrating both sides ⇒ `λ = 1` ⇒ `q_kde = p_kde` ⇒ (by K1 / Prop 4.3 KDE injectivity) `q = p`.

This **subsumes the identifiability arguments of both 2603.09936 (Gaussian-kernel-specific Fourier-injectivity argument) and 2603.12366 (Sinkhorn-divergence-specific argument)** into one chain of two reductions — KDE injectivity (kernel property) + first-variation injectivity (`f`-divergence property). The mixed-divergence drift `V_mix` inherits identifiability by the same argument applied to `D_mix` (which is itself a valid divergence by Theorem 4.12).

# Architecture and training

Algorithm 1 — minimal modification to Drifting training loop:

```
1. Pick divergence (or mixture).
2. Derive WGF velocity v_f^{kde} from chosen f via Eq. 5 / Table 1.
3. Pick kernel k satisfying K1–K4 (or K1_S–K4_S on a manifold).
4. For each iteration:
   ε ~ p_ref;  x = f_θ(ε)       (generated samples)
   y+ ~ p_data                   (target samples)
   v_kde(x) = mini-batch estimate of Eq. 5 using KDE score formula (Appx E)
   x_target = sg(x + η · v_kde(x))
   L = (1/N) Σ ‖f_θ(ε) − x_target‖²
   θ ← θ − η_θ ∇_θ L
```

Mini-batch KDE-score estimator (Appx E): `∇ log µ_kde(x) = (∫ ∇_x k(x, y) dµ(y)) / (∫ k(x, y) dµ(y))`. For Gaussian kernel, simplifies to `(1/h²) · (E_{µ, k_h}[y|x] − x)` — the mean-shift toward kernel-weighted neighbours. For divergences other than forward-KL, multiply by the appropriate density-ratio weight `(p_kde/q_kde)` or `(q_kde/p_kde)`, estimated from kernel sums on the two batches.

**Same backbone, same stop-grad structure, same training loop as Drifting.** The only change is the inner velocity formula. **No new inference cost.**

# Experiments — what the paper actually validates

**Synthetic 2D Swiss roll, 4 mode-collapse / blur ablation.** Figure 1 of the paper:

- (a) **Reverse-KL + χ² mixture (RBF kernel)**: hits all modes by step 500, sharp by step 2000. No blur, no missed modes.
- (b) **`L²` distance (= MMD WGF, RBF kernel)**: blurs continuously; the cloud stays diffuse and doesn't sharpen to the spiral.
- (c) **Forward-KL (Laplace kernel, original Drifting Model)**: hits the spiral by step 2000 but with visible particle jittering near the centre — the K4 violation of Laplace kernel is visible as numerical instability at the data manifold.
- (d) **Forward-KL (RBF kernel)**: similar to (c) but more diffuse — confirming the "blur" failure mode of forward-KL flows even with a regular kernel.

**That is the entire experimental section.** No ImageNet, no audio, no CPU number, no scaling. The contribution is the framework + the qualitative validation that mixed flows fix what forward-KL alone can't.

Future work explicitly lists: large-scale image, conditional generation, multi-modal generation, ablation of kernel + bandwidth choices, mini-batch / acceleration techniques, ViT backbones, JEPA-style hyperspherical semantic space.

# Inference cost

**Unchanged from Drifting** — 1 forward pass through `f_θ`. All gradient-flow / mixed-divergence machinery is training-time only. The mixed flow adds *one* extra kernel sum + one extra ratio computation per training step (for the `p_kde/q_kde` or `q_kde/p_kde` weight); negligible vs the backward pass.

For audio CPU inference: **no change**. The headline 1-NFE CPU latency target is untouched whether we use forward-KL Drifting, reverse-KL Drifting, χ² Drifting, or the mixed drift.

# How this translates to raw audio

The Drifting / Sinkhorn-Drifting / W-Flow audio-translation discussions (in `2602.04770.md`, `2603.12366.md`, `2605.11755.md`) carry over unchanged. What *this* paper adds for audio:

## 1. An explicit divergence recipe for the audio-perceptual drift program

The audio-translation story so far: drift training needs an audio-φ encoder; the right energy `F[q]` is open; W-Flow picked `S_ε` (debiased Sinkhorn) and beat MMD-WGF and KL-WGF on ImageNet (Tab 2a). This paper says **forward-KL is one of those underperforming options precisely because of mode-blurring; the audio-relevant fix is reverse-KL + χ² mixing.**

Direct ports:

- **Sinkhorn-on-Mel + reverse-KL/χ² mix.** Compute KDE of generator and real batches in Mel feature space; build the mixed WGF velocity with `α ≈ β ≈ 0.5`; train with stop-grad as in Drifting. **Sidesteps full Sinkhorn iteration** (no `L=10` Sinkhorn cost on N×M kernel matrices that W-Flow needs); pays per-step cost of one kernel sum + one ratio per particle.
- **AudioMAE-φ + reverse-KL/χ²-mix drift.** Same template with frozen AudioMAE features instead of Mel.
- **Spherical-feature drift on a JEPA-style audio encoder.** If we end up with a normalised audio-φ space (L2-normalised at the encoder output, JEPA-style), the Riemannian extension applies directly. **K4 differentiability drops** — Laplace-on-sphere becomes a *legal* kernel choice (instead of weakly-defined as on `ℝ^d`), removing the Drifting Laplace-kernel jittering observed in §5.1.

## 2. The mode-blur / mode-collapse dial is now exposed as a hyperparameter

In the Drifting/W-Flow training stack we add to our project, the `(α, β)` weighting between reverse-KL and χ² becomes an explicit dial we can sweep. For an audio corpus that's:

- **Concentrated** (e.g. drum-only, single-instrument): forward-KL or Sinkhorn-WGF suffices; mixing buys little.
- **Multimodal but balanced** (multi-instrument, multi-genre but with comparable sample sizes): `α = β = 0.5` baseline.
- **Severely imbalanced** (95% pop / 5% jazz, our analogue of W-Flow's 95% senior / 5% child FFHQ): bias `β` (the χ²-coverage term) higher.

**This is a structural anti-mode-collapse fix that doesn't depend on a discriminator** — same property as W-Flow's two-batch self-transport, achieved via the divergence-choice axis rather than the OT-coupling axis. Both should compose; both should be tried.

## 3. Kernel-choice warning explicit at the framework level

Laplace kernel violates K4 (differentiability). The original Drifting paper used Laplace empirically; 2603.09936 justified it via Landau damping; this paper says **Laplace's velocity field is only weakly-defined and produces numerical jittering at convergence** (Fig 1c). Possible fixes:

- Mollified Laplace `e^{−√(‖x−y‖² + δ²)/τ}` — `C¹` everywhere, recovers Laplace as `δ → 0`.
- Move to a normalised feature space and use vMF (the Riemannian story above).
- Use a Gaussian kernel and pay the Landau-damping cost (Gaussian + exponential bandwidth annealing of 2603.09936 buys back most of it).

For raw waveform with high-frequency content, this is a non-trivial trade-off. The cleanest engineering solution is **move to a JEPA-style normalised audio-φ and use vMF or spherical-log kernel** — both K4-legal on the sphere, both supported by the framework's Riemannian extension.

## 4. CPU inference is unchanged

1 NFE forward pass; nothing in this paper touches that. All contributions are training-time.

# Strengths

- **The unifying framework that 2603.09936 hinted at, made concrete.** Drifting, MMD, KL-flow, χ²-flow, reverse-KL-flow are all rows of one table (Tab 1).
- **Mixed gradient flows as a theorem, not a heuristic** (Thm 4.12). Convex-combination divergences yield convex-combination velocities and inherit identifiability + energy dissipation. This is the load-bearing legitimisation of the rev-KL + χ² recipe.
- **Cleanest identifiability proof yet** (Thm 4.7). Two-step: KDE injectivity (kernel property) + first-variation injectivity (`f`-divergence property). No smoothness assumed on `p` or `q`.
- **Riemannian extension drops K4** on compact manifolds without boundary. Opens the door to vMF / spherical-log kernels on normalised audio-φ spaces.
- **Explicit failure-mode map.** Forward-KL blurs; reverse-KL collapses; χ² over-covers; mixing reconciles. Every row of Table 1 has a known structural failure mode, and the mixed flow is justified by combining their opposites.
- **Diagnostic for Drifting's Laplace-kernel choice.** Kernel violates K4 — the empirical Laplace preference is technically outside the framework, and the numerical jittering at convergence (Fig 1c) is now an *explained* artefact rather than a "feature".

# Limitations

- **No experiments at scale.** 2D Swiss roll is it. The framework's predictions on ImageNet / audio / anywhere realistic are pure conjecture.
- **No comparison to W-Flow.** Same era, same problem, same starting point (Drifting). W-Flow's Sinkhorn-WGF beats MMD-WGF and KL-WGF on ImageNet at scale (Tab 2a) — does the mixed rev-KL + χ² flow beat Sinkhorn-WGF? **Open empirical question that the framework strongly suggests is testable.**
- **Mini-batch variance in high-d.** The paper acknowledges this explicitly in Limitations: as dimension grows, KDE estimates from mini-batches degrade and "seriously affect stability". Audio dimension (`d ≈ 10^4` for 1 s @ 22 kHz, `d ≈ 512` for a typical audio-φ) is non-trivial. **The high-d behaviour is *the* open question for the framework's applicability.** This is also W-Flow's `τ^{1−d/2}` curse in another form.
- **No proposed mini-batch acceleration.** Listed as future work; nothing concrete given. Direct estimation of the density-ratio weights from `N × N` kernel sums is `O(N²)` per particle per step.
- **Kernel choice still empirical.** Framework says any `K1`–`K4` kernel is admissible, but offers no theory for which kernel converges fastest on which divergence on which data. The Landau-damping analysis of 2603.09936 is the closest thing we have, and it's specific to forward-KL + Gaussian.
- **The `(α, β)` weighting is hand-tuned.** No principled schedule, no theoretical optimum. Likely problem-dependent.

# Open questions for this project

1. **Mixed-flow vs Sinkhorn-WGF head-to-head on imbalanced toy.** W-Flow demonstrated mode-coverage rescue on 95% senior / 5% child FFHQ. Run the same experiment with rev-KL + χ² Drifting and compare. If they match: framework's promise is real and we have a much cheaper alternative to Sinkhorn-WGF (no L=10 Sinkhorn iterations per step).
2. **Mini-batch variance on audio-`d`.** At audio dimensionality (`d ≈ 512` for AudioMAE-φ, `d ≈ 10^4` for raw waveform), does the KDE-ratio estimator from `N = 64` particles work, or does it collapse? Cheap sanity check — small toy at intermediate `d` (256, 512, 1024) on Gaussian mixture targets.
3. **Composability with W-Flow's Sinkhorn-WGF.** Add a `χ²-KDE` term to W-Flow's `S_ε` loss (additive on the velocity field). Does the mode-coverage further improve on imbalanced FFHQ?
4. **Riemannian drift on a JEPA-style audio-φ.** If/when we have a JEPA audio encoder with L2-normalised output, deploy the spherical-log kernel + mixed-flow drift directly. Tests the framework at the *only* point where its theoretical extensions are uniquely active.
5. **Empirical-K4 sensitivity on raw waveform.** Compare (a) Laplace kernel (K4-violating, jittery), (b) mollified Laplace, (c) Gaussian + exponential bandwidth, (d) vMF on normalised-features. The framework predicts (a) jitters, (b)/(c)/(d) don't. Tiny experiment with clear pass/fail.
6. **Audio analogue of Fig 1.** Reverse-KL + χ² on a 1-D audio corpus with deliberately imbalanced modes (5% bass-heavy / 95% mid-range). Does the mixture preserve the minority mode where forward-KL Drifting doesn't?
