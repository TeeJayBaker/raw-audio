## TL;DR (project-relevant)

R3GAN ("Re-GAN") is the **canonical 2025 minimal GAN recipe**: an RpGAN (relativistic pairing) loss with **both R1 and R2 zero-centered gradient penalties**, on top of a modernized ResNet/ConvNeXt-style backbone, with **none of StyleGAN2's tricks** (no PL reg, no minibatch stddev, no mapping network, no style injection, no weight modulation, no equalized LR, no normalization layers, no z-normalization, no lazy reg, no mixing reg). One forward pass to sample; FID 2.75 on FFHQ-256 (beating StyleGAN2's 3.78 at fewer params), FID 1.27 on ImageNet-32 (beating DDPM-IP and ADM), and **the first GAN to hit full 1000/1000 mode coverage on Stacked-MNIST with a clean theoretical justification rather than a stddev hack**. For this repo: it's the **reference for "what a defensible vocoder/raw-audio GAN should look like under R1+R2" — both because the audio-GAN literature (HiFi-GAN, BigVGAN) has been adding R1-only ad-hoc, and because R3GAN's whole thesis is "if your loss is sound, you can throw away the tricks", which is the same diagnosis the audio-GAN bag-of-tricks (anti-aliased upsamplers, snake activations, MPD+MRD discriminator stacks) deserves**.

## Why we care

Three reasons R3GAN is load-bearing for this project:

1. **One-step inference.** A 1-NFE generative model with FID competitive against diffusion (200–1000 NFE) is exactly the cost profile we need for CPU. R3GAN's 25 M-param FFHQ-256 generator runs in a single forward pass; on CPU that's the only way short of distillation we plausibly hit faster-than-real-time at 44.1 kHz.
2. **The loss generalizes.** RpGAN + R1 + R2 is architecture-agnostic — nothing in the convergence argument depends on the discriminator being 2D conv. It transports directly to 1D waveform GANs, STFT-domain GANs, and multi-discriminator vocoder setups (MPD + MRD).
3. **It calls out tricks-as-symptoms.** The paper's framing — *"GAN tricks compensate for an ill-posed loss; fix the loss and the tricks become unnecessary"* — is a stance the raw-audio GAN literature has not been forced to take. HiFi-GAN's mel loss + feature matching + MPD + MRD bag should be re-examined under R1+R2 RpGAN.

## The loss — written out

Traditional GAN (Goodfellow), with `f(t) = −log(1 + e^{−t})`:

```
L(θ, ψ) = E_{z∼p_z} [ f( D_ψ(G_θ(z)) ) ] + E_{x∼p_D} [ f(−D_ψ(x)) ]
```

Relativistic pairing GAN (RpGAN, Jolicoeur-Martineau 2018):

```
L(θ, ψ) = E_{z∼p_z, x∼p_D} [ f( D_ψ(G_θ(z)) − D_ψ(x) ) ]
```

The pairing is the central trick. Where the classic GAN only requires `D` to separate real from fake on aggregate (so one decision boundary suffices, and `G` can solve it by pushing all fakes *barely* across one side — collapse), RpGAN forces `D` to maintain a margin **per real sample**: every fake is critiqued *relative* to a specific real. Sun et al. (2023) showed RpGAN's loss landscape contains **no bad local minima** corresponding to mode-dropping; every basin is a global minimum.

But RpGAN alone is not enough. The authors' Proposition I (proven in Appendix B) shows **unregularized RpGAN does not converge under gradient descent** for general `p_D` (e.g. delta-like distributions). Empirically, both classic GAN + R1 and RpGAN + R1 alone diverge.

Their fix: add **both** zero-centered gradient penalties:

```
R1(ψ) = (γ/2) E_{x∼p_D}  [ ‖∇_x D_ψ(x)‖² ]      # gradient norm on real
R2(θ, ψ) = (γ/2) E_{x∼p_θ} [ ‖∇_x D_ψ(x)‖² ]    # gradient norm on fake
```

Proposition II (Appendix C) extends Mescheder et al.'s local-convergence analysis to RpGAN: with R1+R2, all eigenvalues of the Jacobian of the regularized gradient vector field at the equilibrium have negative real parts, so the system is **locally convergent** for sufficiently small learning rates.

The intuition for "why R2 not just R1":

- R1 penalizes `D`'s gradient on real samples. Near equilibrium when `G` already matches `p_D`, R1 keeps `∇_x D` small there, so `D` stops oscillating on the real manifold.
- But early in training (when `p_θ` is far from `p_D`), if you regularize `D` *only* on real samples, `D` is free to have huge gradients on fakes — and the discriminator's gradient on fakes is exactly the signal `G` uses to update. Gradient explosion on fakes → training diverges.
- R2 closes that loop: penalizing `∇_x D` on fakes too keeps both branches well-behaved throughout training, not only at equilibrium.

Empirically (Fig. 1, Table 1 in the paper): GAN+R1 and RpGAN+R1 both diverge on Stacked-MNIST; GAN+R1+R2 trains stably but mode-drops (693/1000 modes, KL 0.927); **RpGAN+R1+R2 trains stably and recovers all 1000/1000 modes (KL 0.0781)**. The pairing handles diversity, the R1+R2 pair handles stability — they're orthogonal and both needed.

## Stacked-MNIST — the diversity benchmark

Stacked-MNIST = three random MNIST digits concatenated as the three RGB channels, giving 1000 uniformly distributed modes. A pretrained classifier scores each generated image, and we report (a) number of modes the generator ever produces, out of 1000, and (b) the reverse KL `D_KL(p_θ ‖ p_D)` between the categorical distribution of generated mode-labels and the true uniform.

R3GAN's Table 3 numbers vs. all prior GANs:

| Model | # modes ↑ | D_KL ↓ |
|---|---|---|
| DCGAN | 99 | 3.40 |
| VEEGAN | 150 | 2.95 |
| WGAN-GP | 959 | 0.73 |
| PacGAN | 992 | 0.28 |
| StyleGAN2 | 940 | 0.42 |
| PresGAN | 1000 | 0.12 |
| VAEBM | 1000 | 0.087 |
| DDGAN | 1000 | 0.071 |
| MEG | 1000 | 0.031 |
| **R3GAN (Config E)** | **1000** | **0.029** |

R3GAN is the **first standard GAN** (no diffusion-style noise, no EBM, no VAE prior) to reach full coverage *and* the lowest KL of the bunch. The relativistic pairing is doing the work — StyleGAN2 at 940/1000 even with minibatch-stddev illustrates how brittle the trick-based approach to diversity is.

## The roadmap — what they dropped, what they kept

The roadmap (Configs A → E, Table 2) is the actually-useful part of the paper. Starting from StyleGAN2 (Config A, FID 7.52 on FFHQ-256):

**Config B — strip every StyleGAN2 trick.** Removed in this step:

- *Style-based generation:* mapping network (z → W), style injection at every layer, weight modulation/demodulation, additive noise injection.
- *Image-manipulation enhancements:* mixing regularization, path-length regularization.
- *Misc tricks:* z normalization, minibatch stddev, equalized learning rate, lazy regularization.

FID worsens to 12.46 but training is stable on the original loss. This is the *evidence* that none of these tricks were essential — they were patches over an unstable foundation.

**Config C — swap loss to RpGAN + R1 + R2.** FID 11.65. The well-behaved loss alone, without architecture changes, recovers some of the loss from removing the tricks and (more importantly) lets training proceed without divergence.

**Config D — ResNet-ify with ConvNeXt principles, part 1.**

- 1-3-1 bottleneck ResNet blocks for both G and D
- Bilinear resampling instead of strided/transposed conv
- Fix-up initialization (zero-init last conv in each block, scale others by L^{-0.25})
- Drop output skips (no longer needed with this backbone)
- Leaky ReLU throughout, no tanh on G output

FID 9.95.

**Config E — bottleneck modernization, part 2.**

- Increase channel width 1.5×, depthwise → grouped conv with group size 16 (the GPU-efficient compromise: depthwise is bandwidth-bound)
- Inverted bottleneck (ConvNeXt-style)

FID 7.05 — **beats StyleGAN2 (7.52) at 23 M / 23 M params vs 24.8 M / 24.0 M.**

What they explicitly **rejected** from the ConvNeXt recipe: large-kernel depthwise conv (worse than 3×3 grouped), GELU (worse than leaky ReLU for GAN training), layer norm (not needed and arguably harmful for the loss they use, which assumes independent per-sample handling), `patchify` stem, `4-stage` ratio. The principle is **don't blindly import ViT-mimicry tricks into a CNN that already works**.

What they kept that turned out to matter (vs. early GAN failure modes per DCGAN): no normalization in either G or D; no momentum (Adam β₁ = 0); bilinear upsampling not transposed conv; 4×4 constant input for G; leaky ReLU both sides.

## Architecture details — final Config E

- Fully symmetric ResNet G and D, ~25 M params each
- Per resolution stage: one transition layer (bilinear resample + optional 1×1 conv for channel change) + two residual blocks
- Residual block: Conv1×1 → LeakyReLU → Conv3×3 (grouped, group=16) → LeakyReLU → Conv1×1 (no bias). Bottleneck width 0.75× the stage width; inverted-bottleneck means the 3×3 grouped conv is the wide one
- G's stem: 4×4 learnable feature maps modulated by `z` via a single linear layer
- D's head: 4×4 depthwise conv to global pool spatial extent → linear to logit
- No normalization, no skip-cogging-output-to-RGB, no mapping network

This is a **minimal symmetric CNN**, not a style-modulated branching structure. The simplification is real and the saved complexity is real.

A few honest caveats the paper itself raises:

- **No pretrained-ImageNet feature leakage.** StyleGAN-XL and StyleSAN-XL get lower FID on FFHQ-256 (2.19, 1.68) but use Projected-GAN-style ImageNet-pretrained discriminators, which Kynkäänniemi et al. (2023) showed leak features into the FID metric. R3GAN's numbers are without that trick.
- **No image-editing affordances.** Dropping the mapping network kills GAN-inversion-style editing. That's fine for our use case (sample generation), explicitly bad if you want StyleGAN-style controllable synthesis.
- **Recall (sample diversity, Kynkäänniemi et al. 2019)** is similar to or marginally worse than diffusion counterparts, better than other GANs. FFHQ-256 recall 0.49 (R3GAN) vs 0.43 (StyleGAN2). ImageNet-32 recall 0.63 vs ADM's ≈0.63.

## Translates to raw audio as

This is the section that matters most for the repo. R3GAN is an **image** paper, but its core thesis is loss-not-tricks and that thesis is architecture-agnostic.

**Direct port: R1 + R2 on existing audio-GAN discriminators.** HiFi-GAN, BigVGAN, MelGAN all use multi-period discriminators (MPD) and multi-resolution STFT discriminators (MRD). The R1 penalty has occasionally been used in audio-GAN training (Karras-style); R2 has not, to our knowledge, been added. Adding R2 to both MPD and MRD branches is a one-line training change that R3GAN's theory predicts should improve stability — particularly for the well-known issue that audio-GAN training is sensitive to learning rate and learning rate scheduling, with periodic divergence/recovery cycles. **This is a concrete experiment we can run.**

**The RpGAN relativistic pairing in audio.** Audio-GAN losses are usually hinge or non-saturating, with a feature-matching loss bolted on. The RpGAN pairing requires comparing `D(real)` and `D(fake)` for paired samples — for vocoders this is natural (a mel-spectrogram conditions a real waveform and a generated waveform of the same content). Whether the pairing produces the same mode-coverage benefit on continuous audio distributions as on Stacked-MNIST is an empirical question, but the theoretical argument (no bad local minima → diversity) carries over: vocoders are known to over-smooth and lose timbral variation; that's a mode-collapse symptom.

**Does "drop the bag of tricks" apply to audio?** The audio-GAN bag includes:

- *Multi-period discriminator* (MPD; HiFi-GAN): catches periodic patterns at different prime periods.
- *Multi-resolution STFT discriminator* (MRD; UnivNet, BigVGAN): catches spectral artifacts at multiple FFT sizes.
- *Mel-spectrogram L1 / L2 reconstruction loss:* anchors `G` against ground truth in the perceptual domain.
- *Feature-matching loss:* matches intermediate D activations between real and fake to stabilize.
- *Anti-aliased upsamplers + snake activations* (BigVGAN): explicitly handle the Nyquist / aliasing problem that raw conv stacks have at 24-48 kHz.

R3GAN's argument is that **the loss should be well-behaved enough that you don't need stabilizers**. The audio bag splits into two categories under that lens:

1. *Stabilizers / regularizers* (feature matching, the mel L1, lazy regularization analogs): these are exactly the kind of patch R3GAN says should become unnecessary under a sound loss. **Worth ablating: train an MPD+MRD vocoder under RpGAN + R1 + R2 with no feature matching and no mel loss. If R3GAN's thesis is right, FAD shouldn't collapse; if it does, the audio domain has a structural mismatch with the image case.**
2. *Inductive-bias / physics encoders* (MPD's prime-period split, MRD's STFT scales, anti-aliased upsamplers, snake activations): these aren't stabilizers — they encode actual properties of audio signals (periodicity, time-frequency duality, Nyquist) that 2D images don't have. R3GAN's "modernize the backbone" argument doesn't say these should go; it says they should be free to be evaluated *on their merits* once the loss isn't covering for instability. Snake activations in particular were introduced because alias-injected high-frequency content breaks training stability — under R1+R2, does it still help, or is it solving a problem the gradient penalty already solved? Unknown, worth testing.

The interesting hypothesis: **R1+R2 RpGAN with a clean 1D-conv backbone (no snake, no anti-aliased upsampler) could match HiFi-GAN-with-tricks at lower CPU inference cost.** Snake activations are not cheap on CPU; anti-aliased upsamplers replace strided transposed convs with longer filter banks. Both add nontrivial latency. If R3GAN's lesson holds, those costs may be optional.

**What does NOT transport.** ConvNeXt's specific inverted bottleneck was tuned for 2D images at 256² resolution; 1D audio at 50 k samples has completely different aspect ratios and channel/width tradeoffs. The roadmap structure (strip → fix loss → modernize backbone → modernize block) is the lesson, not the specific Config E geometry.

## What we should test in this repo

1. **Reproduce R1+R2 on a small 1D conv GAN at 44.1 kHz drum hits** (using the CRASH dataset structure, ~0.5 s samples). Compare R1 alone, R1+R2, and R1+R2+RpGAN on FAD and recall. Baseline: a hinge-loss 1D conv GAN of the same architecture.
2. **Stacked-Audio diversity benchmark.** Construct an audio analog of Stacked-MNIST: e.g. mixtures of N kicks × M snares × P hats with known categorical labels, train a classifier, measure mode coverage. We don't have a published version of this — would be a small but novel contribution.
3. **CPU inference cost.** R3GAN's 23 M-param FFHQ-256 generator is a 1-NFE forward pass. Measure equivalent: 1D conv generator at 21 000 samples, ~25 M params, single-batch CPU, leaky ReLU + grouped conv 16. Compare to HiFi-GAN-v1 (also ~14 M params, 1 NFE) on the same hardware.
4. **Ablate the audio bag.** Train MPD+MRD vocoder under RpGAN+R1+R2 with feature matching OFF, mel loss OFF. Sweep snake vs leaky ReLU. Sweep anti-aliased vs bilinear upsampler. Report FAD and CPU latency.

## Strengths

- Clean theory: RpGAN landscape (no bad minima) + Mescheder local convergence (R1+R2) compose, with explicit proofs in appendix.
- The roadmap (Config A → E) is **reproducible engineering work** — every step has a delta-FID and a deletion list. This is unusually honest by ML-paper standards.
- Beats StyleGAN2 with fewer params *and* fewer features. No "we trained 4× longer" caveat.
- One-step inference at FID competitive with diffusion's hundreds-of-NFE.

## Limitations

- **No CPU benchmark, no inference-cost discussion.** Standard for an image GAN paper but the gap we care about.
- Scalability above ImageNet-64 is unverified. The authors flag this.
- Removing the mapping network kills latent-space affordances. Not relevant to us but worth flagging.
- The local convergence guarantee is *local* — it says "if you're close enough to the equilibrium, you'll converge"; it doesn't say global convergence. Empirically this seems fine; theoretically there's still a gap.
- All architecture choices are tuned on FFHQ-256. The "inverted bottleneck" wins might not survive on 1D audio with different aspect ratios.

## Open questions for the repo

- Does R1+R2 stabilize MPD+MRD vocoder training enough to drop the feature-matching loss?
- Is RpGAN's mode-coverage benefit real on continuous audio distributions, or is it a property of multimodal categorical setups like Stacked-MNIST?
- What does the audio analog of "Config E" look like — i.e. what's the minimal stable 1D-conv GAN once R1+R2 is in place?
- Can the same one-step recipe match diffusion FAD on raw waveforms with no distillation?
- Are snake activations and anti-aliased upsamplers solving problems R1+R2 already solves, or solving genuinely different problems (aliasing)?
