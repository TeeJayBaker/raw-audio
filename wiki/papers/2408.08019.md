# PeriodWave-Turbo

## TL;DR (project lens)

**The literal recipe this project is following: FM-pretrain an audio generator, then adversarial-finetune it to a fixed 2-/4-step generator.** PeriodWave-Turbo takes the pre-trained PeriodWave (a multi-period flow-matching vocoder, 7.6 M / 29.8 M / 70.2 M params) and runs ~1k–300k steps of fine-tuning with multi-scale mel L1 + LSGAN (MPD + MS-SB-CQTD) + feature-matching loss, generating its training samples by 4-step Euler unrolling of the pre-trained FM ODE. PESQ jumps from 4.29 (16-step teacher) to **4.454** on LibriTTS — SOTA at publication time, beating 112 M-param BigVGAN-v2. **1 000 fine-tune steps** is enough to surpass every prior vocoder on objective metrics. CPU inference at batch 16 on Xeon: **xRT 0.12 at 4 NFE for the 70 M model** — i.e. ~8× slower than real-time. The same FM+GAN-finetune recipe, on a waveform-domain multi-period U-Net backbone, runs **40× slower on CPU than Flow2GAN's STFT-domain ConvNeXt** at comparable quality. The recipe is right; the backbone is wrong for CPU.

## Problem and framing

CFM vocoders (PeriodWave, FlowVC) match GAN-vocoder quality but need 16+ ODE steps. GAN vocoders (HiFi-GAN, BigVGAN-v2) are 1-step but training is unstable, expensive (2.5M–10M steps), and they degrade on synthesised mels (two-stage TTS mismatch). The paper asks: can you take a pre-trained CFM vocoder and cheaply convert it to a fixed-few-step generator that retains the FM quality advantage while collapsing inference to 2 or 4 NFE?

## Method — adversarial flow matching optimization

**Few-step generator modification.** Initialize from pre-trained PeriodWave (FM objective). Fix the sampler to Euler at 2 or 4 steps, with prescribed `t ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75}` for 4-step. The model's parameters are the only thing being trained — sampler shape is now part of the architecture, not a hyperparameter. The paper notes this is the audio analog of [ADD/LADD/UFOGen/DMD2](../wiki/concepts/methods.md): pre-trained diffusion/FM teacher → adversarial fine-tune of a fixed-step student.

**Loss form (Eq. 4):**

  L_final = L_adv(G) + 2·L_fm + 45·L_mel

- `L_mel` is **multi-scale mel-spectrogram L1** (not multi-scale STFT — see ablation below). Hop sizes `{8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512}`, windows = 4·hop, mel-filterbank applied (DAC-style recipe from Kumar et al. 2024).
- `L_adv` is **LSGAN** form: `E[(D(x)−1)² + D(G(x_t,c,t))²]` for D, `E[(D(G)−1)²]` for G. D is MPD + MS-SB-CQTD.
- `L_fm` is L1 feature-matching between discriminator activations on real and generated audio.
- No warmup, no loss-weight schedule — just constant `(λ_fm, λ_mel) = (2, 45)` from step 1.

**Discriminators.**
- **MPD** (HiFi-GAN): reshape 1-D waveform into 2-D with prime periods `{2, 3, 5, 7, 11}`, run small 2-D conv stacks. Captures different periodic patterns of the signal.
- **MS-SB-CQTD** (Gu et al. 2024, also used in BigVGAN-v2): multi-scale constant-Q transform discriminator with per-octave sub-band processing — explicit pitch / harmonic modelling.
- No MSD, no MRD. The (MPD, MS-SB-CQTD) combination is the BigVGAN-v2 recipe.

**Reconstruction loss ablation (Table 9, critical).** Three options compared:
- *Multi-scale mel L1 (chosen):* stable, best PESQ.
- *Single-scale mel L1:* lower PESQ, model survives.
- *Multi-scale STFT L1 (no mel filter):* "generates a high electric noise" — fails. The paper's footnote 1 attributes this to **phase-level reconstruction error being non-informative**: STFT magnitude+phase L1 punishes the model on phase mismatch that doesn't correspond to perceptual error, so the network learns to produce noise instead. **Frequency-domain reconstruction losses must be mel-weighted (perceptual), not raw STFT.**

**Without adversarial loss, training collapses after ~10k steps for all reconstruction-only variants except multi-scale mel** (which is stable but artefacted). The adversarial term is the actual quality driver; the mel-L1 is a stabiliser.

**Distillation Method comparison.** The authors implemented DMD-style distillation (fake-score from pre-trained PeriodWave teacher, à la Yin et al. 2024b) and found it does **not** help. Footnote 2 attributes this to "the time-aligned strong condition Mel-spectrogram" already pinning the output distribution — DMD's role of supplying a target distribution is redundant when the conditioning is this strong. **Speculative implication for unconditional audio:** DMD might actually matter when conditioning is weak or absent (text-to-audio, raw unconditional waveform).

## Why 1 000 fine-tune steps suffice

The paper trains for 0.3M steps on LibriTTS but reports that **after 1k fine-tune steps the model already beats every prior vocoder on objective metrics on LJSpeech** (Table 2: PESQ 4.349 at 1k steps, vs BigVGAN-v2 at 4.262 after 3M steps). Why so cheap:

1. The FM-pretrain has already solved the hard part — placing mass in the right region of waveform space.
2. The student only needs to learn to *compensate for ODE truncation error* of 4-step Euler (vs the 16-step Midpoint teacher) plus pick up the high-frequency detail that vector-field MSE under-fits. Both are local corrections to an already-good generator.
3. Adversarial training is being used as a *post-hoc sharpener* on a near-final model, not as the primary optimisation. Stability problems of "fully adversarial" training don't appear.

Ablation (Table 9): same model trained from scratch with the same adversarial-FM-optimization objective reaches only PESQ 3.935 at 0.5M steps — confirming the FM pre-train, not the GAN finetune, is doing most of the work.

## Architecture — period-aware FM estimator

PeriodWave's backbone (taken whole-cloth from arXiv 2408.07547):

- A U-Net of D/M/U blocks (down / middle / up) where the input 1-D waveform `x_t` is **periodified** by reshaping it into 2-D with prime period `p ∈ {2, 3, 5, 7, 11}`. The model is conditioned on the period index `p` and runs a 2-D conv stack on the reshaped representation, then reshapes back to 1-D and sums over periods.
- This is the **audio analog of multi-resolution image generators** (cf. multi-resolution ConvNeXt / Vocos / Flow2GAN, but operating in *waveform-domain* with periodic reshaping instead of STFT-domain with multi-window STFT). The "resolution" axis is replaced by a "period prime" axis.
- Three sizes: S (7.57 M, hidden 256), B (29.80 M, hidden 512), L (70.24 M, hidden 768).
- Crucially: **convolutions run at sample rate** (post-periodify reshape, but still 24 kHz × 1/p hop). This is the root cause of poor CPU throughput.

## Inference cost

CPU benchmark on AMD EPYC 7313, batch 16, LibriTTS test set:

| Model         | NFE | Params | CPU xRT | GPU xRT |
|---------------|-----|--------|---------|---------|
| PeriodWave-B (teacher) | 16  | 29.8 M | 0.21    | 4.62    |
| PeriodWave-L (teacher) | 16  | 70.2 M | 0.09    | 2.77    |
| Turbo-S       | 4   | 7.6 M  | 2.58    | 42.26   |
| Turbo-B       | 4   | 29.8 M | 1.12    | 35.01   |
| Turbo-L       | 4   | 70.2 M | **0.12**| 24.77   |

Adversarial fine-tuning gives a ~7.5× CPU speedup over the teacher at matched params (mostly the 16→4 NFE drop). The 70 M model is still **8× slower than real-time** on CPU, batch 16. The 7.6 M Turbo-S is above real-time (2.58×) but its PESQ (4.324) is already SOTA-tier — there is a useful Pareto point at small backbone × few NFE.

GPU is fine across all sizes — this paper is not bottlenecked on GPU.

## Headline results

- **LibriTTS PESQ 4.454** (Turbo-L, 4 NFE, 0.3M fine-tune steps) — SOTA at publication. Outperforms BigVGAN-v2 (112 M, 3M GAN steps).
- LJSpeech: PESQ 4.418 at 0.1M fine-tune steps.
- MUSDB18-HQ (OOD music): PeriodWave-Turbo-L beats every baseline except BigVGAN-v2 (which was trained on music). Higher pitch and periodicity fidelity than waveform-domain GAN competitors.
- Two-stage TTS (ARDiT-TTS + vocoder): Turbo-L gets CER 0.50, WER 1.49, MOS 4.32 — best across the board. The FM-init recipe survives the synthesised-mel domain shift better than from-scratch GANs.
- **2-step variant also works**: Turbo-B at 2 NFE hits PESQ 4.345 (Table 9). 1-step is not reported — the iterative-refinement framing breaks down there.

## Translates-to-audio takeaways for this project

This is the canonical FM-pretrain + GAN-finetune audio pipeline. Comparing PeriodWave-Turbo against [Flow2GAN](2512.23278.md) on identical training-recipe DNA isolates the architectural variable:

| Aspect                | PeriodWave-Turbo                | Flow2GAN                          |
|-----------------------|---------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Backbone              | Multi-period 1-D U-Net (waveform-domain) | Multi-resolution ConvNeXt over STFT |
| Domain of computation | Sample-rate (24 kHz, periodified) | Hop-rate (factor ~256 lower) on complex STFT coefficients |
| Params                | 70.2 M                          | 78.9 M                            |
| FM loss               | Velocity prediction, no spectral weighting | x₁-prediction, spectral-energy-inverse weighting |
| GAN loss              | LSGAN + FM + multi-scale mel L1 (λ=45) | HingeGAN + FM + multi-scale mel L1 |
| Discriminators        | MPD + MS-SB-CQTD                | MPD + MRD                         |
| NFE                   | 4                               | 4                                 |
| PESQ on LibriTTS      | 4.454                           | 4.48                              |
| **CPU xRT (Xeon, batch 16)** | **0.12 (8× slower than RT)** | **1.35 (1.35× faster than RT)**   |

**Same recipe, same NFE, same quality tier, ~11× CPU throughput gap.** The architectural choice — running convs at sample-rate (waveform domain) vs at hop-rate (STFT domain) — dominates everything else in the inference budget. This is the single most important comparison in the corpus for the project's CPU target.

Subsidiary lessons:

- **FM pre-train + cheap GAN finetune (~1k steps) works for audio.** This is reproducible and doesn't require the BigVGAN-style 5M-step adversarial run.
- **Use mel-perceptual reconstruction, not raw STFT.** Multi-scale mel L1 is the only reconstruction loss that stabilises waveform-output models without GAN supervision; raw STFT L1 collapses to electric noise (phase-error gradient is misleading).
- **MPD + MS-SB-CQTD is a strong discriminator pair.** Worth keeping for waveform-output models; for STFT-domain models the MRD/CQT axis may be redundant with the model's own STFT structure.
- **DMD-style distribution-matching distillation didn't help.** When mel conditioning is this strong, the conditional distribution is already pinned by the input. For *unconditional* raw-audio gen, the calculus changes — DMD may matter there.
- **Fixed-NFE specialist beats variable-NFE generalist** at the target NFE. Trade flexibility for quality at the chosen step count. (Cf. Shortcut Models / IMM for the opposite design choice.)
- **Few-step (2-4) is the sweet spot, not 1-step.** Iterative refinement keeps the train-inference distribution aligned and lets the model correct ODE truncation error in the loss landscape. 1-step generation is left unattempted here.

## Strengths

- Concrete, reproducible recipe with code release.
- Honest ablations (Table 9 directly tests reconstruction-loss choice, distillation, pre-train ablation).
- Multiple model scales (S/B/L) — scaling behaviour is monotonic and modest.
- Strong OOD result on MUSDB18-HQ (music) without music in training — suggests the FM pre-train provides distributional robustness.

## Limitations / open questions

- **CPU performance is sub-real-time at quality-competitive sizes.** Authors themselves flag this in the conclusion and propose "multiple STFT-based down-sampling" to replace the waveform-domain U-Net downsamplers — i.e. they recognise the architectural problem (and Flow2GAN later solves it more directly).
- 1-step generation is not attempted. Whether the recipe extends to 1 NFE is open.
- Mel-conditioned only. The DMD-ineffectiveness result is consistent with strong conditioning; behaviour without mel is unknown.
- Multi-period reshape is hardware-unfriendly on CPU — strided reshapes and per-period 2-D convs at sample rate hurt cache locality.

## Things to test in this repo

- Does the *recipe* (FM pre-train → 1k–10k step adversarial finetune with multi-scale mel L1 + MPD + MS-SB-CQTD) transfer to an **STFT-domain ConvNeXt backbone** at smaller scale? If yes, we get the recipe simplicity of PeriodWave-Turbo *and* the CPU throughput of Flow2GAN — basically reproduce Flow2GAN from PeriodWave-Turbo's loss prescription.
- Does **DMD** start helping when the conditioning is weakened (e.g. only coarse mel, or class-conditional, or unconditional)? PeriodWave-Turbo's null result is conditional on strong mel input.
- Does multi-scale mel L1 stay stable as a sole reconstruction loss for *unconditional* generation? PeriodWave-Turbo's stability claim assumes mel-conditioning.
