# TL;DR вҖ” why this matters for raw-audio CPU gen

A new from-scratch 1-NFE generator that **sets the strongest 1-step FID anywhere** (1.29 on ImageNet 256ВІ) by making the Drifting recipe principled. The recipe is:

1. **Pick an energy functional `F[q]` with `F=0 вҮ” q=p`.** They pick the *debiased Sinkhorn divergence* `S_Оө(q, p)`.
2. **Write down its Wasserstein gradient flow** `вҲӮ_t q_t = вҲҮВ·(q_t V_t)` with `V_t = вҲ’вҲҮ ОҙF/Оҙq`. For Sinkhorn this gives a closed form: `V^Оө_{q,p}(x) = T^Оө_{q,p}(x) вҲ’ T^Оө_{q,q}(x)` вҖ” barycentric projection from a **genвҶ’real** OT plan, minus the barycentric projection from a **genвҶ’gen self-transport** plan. Both plans are computed by Sinkhorn iteration on the batch.
3. **Explicit Euler-discretise** the WGF in distribution space: `q^{(k+1)} = (id + О· V^{(k)})_# q^{(k)}`. This is a **training-time** sequence of distributional updates, not an inference-time chain.
4. **Compress** the whole sequence into a single static generator `f_Оё` by regressing `f_Оё(z) вүҲ sg(f_Оё^{prev}(z) + О· V(f_Оё^{prev}(z)))`. Inference is **one forward pass**.

This is exactly the `V = вҲ’вҲҮ ОҙF/Оҙq` template that the Drifting / Sinkhorn-Drifting theory companions [(2603.09936)](2603.09936.md), [(2603.12366)](2603.12366.md) called out as the right design surface for one-step generation. W-Flow is the first instance to (a) deliver SOTA on ImageNet, (b) be globally batch-coordinated rather than per-sample-local, (c) fix Drifting's mode-collapse failure mode demonstrably, and (d) ground convergence in a real continuous-time PDE result rather than a `V=0` argument. For us: drop-in replacement for Drifting-style audio-ПҶ training; same inference cost (1 NFE) so the CPU target is unchanged; **the self-transport term is the load-bearing new piece** and the one most likely to matter on multimodal audio corpora.

The catch: **two-stage training** (no end-to-end shortcut) and it inherits Drifting's dependency on a strong feature encoder ПҶ вҖ” same `latent-MAE` setup ported from Drifting. Two-stage compute vs single-stage iMF/IMM is the cost question this method has to answer.

# What problem it solves

Existing 1-NFE recipes split into three loose camps, each with a structural weakness:

- **Diffusion/flow distillation** вҖ” the training signal inherits a transport path originally designed for many-step inference. Distillation-shaped, teacher-bound, no principled link to "one-step ought to mean".
- **MeanFlow / IMM / consistency** вҖ” self-consistency identities along the diffusion ODE. Same inheritance, just collapsed by definition into a single step.
- **Drifting** вҖ” a hand-designed mean-shift drift `V^+ вҲ’ V^вҲ’` in ПҶ-space. **No principled energy underneath**, identifiability fails (`V=0 вҮҺ q=p` rigorously without assumptions), the kernel-sum construction is *local* (each particle's update depends only on its kernel-weighted neighbours), and the diagonal of `V^вҲ’` has to be hand-masked to prevent self-attraction collapse at low П„.

The unifying question the paper poses: *what principles should govern the training dynamics of one-step generators?* Their answer: define the dynamics in **distribution space** as the steepest-descent flow (in Wasserstein metric) of an explicit energy functional, then compress that flow into a static map. Drifting was already trying to do this; W-Flow makes it rigorous and shows the right energy is **Sinkhorn divergence** (not MMD, not KL вҖ” both ablate worse, see Tab 2a).

# Method, the part that distinguishes it from neighbours

## Wasserstein gradient flow as the design surface

Choose `F: P(в„қ^n) вҶ’ в„қ` with `F=0 вҮ” q=p`. The Wasserstein gradient flow of `F` is

```
вҲӮ_t q_t + вҲҮВ·(q_t V_t) = 0,    V_t(x) = вҲ’вҲҮ ОҙF/Оҙq (q_t)(x).
```

In words: at every time `t`, the distribution `q_t` slides in the direction of *steepest descent of F under the Wasserstein metric*. The velocity field `V_t` is the spatial gradient of the first variation of `F`. Discretise time with explicit Euler:

```
g^{(k)}(x) = x + О· V^{(k)}(x),    q^{(k+1)} = g^{(k)}_# q^{(k)}.
```

This is the training-time stepwise update. The composition `g^{(K-1)} вҲҳ В·В·В· вҲҳ g^{(0)} вҲҳ f^{(0)}` is the desired one-step map; the generator network `f_Оё` is trained to *be* this composition (without ever evaluating it as a chain at inference). Table 1 of the paper enumerates the velocity fields induced by three candidate energies:

| Energy `F[q_t]` | Velocity field `V_t(x)` |
|---|---|
| ВҪ MMDВІ(q_t, p) | `вҲ«вҲҮ_x k(x,y) p(dy) вҲ’ вҲ«вҲҮ_x k(x,y) q_t(dy)` вҖ” mean-shift, local kernel-sum |
| KL(q_t вҖ– p) | `вҲҮ log p(x) вҲ’ вҲҮ log q_t(x)` вҖ” score difference (intractable q_t score) |
| **S_Оө(q_t, p)** | **`T^Оө_{q_t,p}(x) вҲ’ T^Оө_{q_t,q_t}(x)`** вҖ” barycentric-projection difference |

MMD is what GMMN and Drifting (under a Gaussian kernel, after [2603.09936](2603.09936.md)'s identification) effectively use. KL is what diffusion uses (and is intractable in this form). Sinkhorn is what W-Flow picks. Their ablation:

| Divergence | 1-NFE FID @ 100 epochs B/2 ablation |
|---|---|
| Drifting (heuristic Drifting Model) | 8.46 |
| MMD | 10.40 |
| KL | 10.17 |
| **Sinkhorn (W-Flow)** | **7.29** |

So Sinkhorn-WGF cleanly beats the MMD WGF (and beats heuristic Drifting). Note that two of these вҖ” MMD and Sinkhorn вҖ” share the `V_t = (genвҶ’real) вҲ’ (genвҶ’gen)` skeleton; the difference is only how the coupling is built.

## The Sinkhorn velocity field in closed form

For the entropic OT problem

```
OT_Оө(q, p) := inf_{ПҖ вҲҲ О (q,p)} вҲ« ВҪвҖ–xвҲ’yвҖ–ВІ ПҖ(dx, dy) + Оө D_KL(ПҖ вҖ– qвҠ—p),
```

with debiased Sinkhorn divergence

```
S_Оө(q, p) := OT_Оө(q, p) вҲ’ ВҪ OT_Оө(q, q) вҲ’ ВҪ OT_Оө(p, p).
```

The induced velocity field is

```
V^Оө_{q,p}(x) = T^Оө_{q,p}(x) вҲ’ T^Оө_{q,q}(x),
```

where `T^Оө_{q,p}(x) = вҲ« y ПҖ^Оө_{q,p}(dy | x)` is the **barycentric projection** of the optimal coupling `ПҖ^Оө_{q,p}` evaluated at `x` (mean of the conditional given `x`). Two crucial properties (Prop 3.5 / 3.7):

- `V^Оө_{q,p} вүЎ 0  вҮ”  q = p`. **No spurious stationary points.** Drifting's identifiability hole closes.
- `S_Оө` has no local minima other than `q=p` under Gaussian assumptions (Hardion-Lacombe 2026, ref [22] in the paper).

The two-term structure is structurally significant: `T^Оө_{q,p}` is the **attraction** to data; `T^Оө_{q,q}` is a **self-consistency correction** that prevents the update from collapsing onto local clusters and enforces global mass conservation. This is the new ingredient over plain Drifting (where `V^вҲ’` is built from kernel-weighted negatives, often reusing the same batch and requiring a diagonal mask) and over MMD-WGF (where the second term uses kernel gradients on the model distribution itself, with no marginal coupling constraint).

Figure 9 of the paper makes the contrast pictorial: Drifting computes `K_row` and `K_col` softmax-normalised kernel matrices separately, takes their geometric mean `KМғ = вҲҡ(K_row вҠҷ K_col)`, masks the diagonal of the self-term, and combines. W-Flow runs Sinkhorn-Knopp on the full Gibbs kernel until convergence (10 iterations sufficient, Tab 7), yielding two doubly-stochastic transport plans `О ^{qМӮ_Оё, pМӮ}` (genвҶ’real) and `О ^{qМӮ_Оё, qМӮ'_Оё}` (genвҶ’gen', see below). The Drifting heuristic is now seen as a **single-iteration approximation** of full Sinkhorn вҖ” extending it to multiple iterations is what closes the OT gap (consistent with [Sinkhorn-Drifting (2603.12366)](2603.12366.md)).

## Two-batch self-transport (the load-bearing trick)

Naively estimating `T^Оө_{q,q}(x_i)` from a single batch `{x_i}` introduces a **self-matching artefact**: the diagonal of the OT plan gets all the weight at zero transport cost. The estimator overemphasises `ПҖ_ii` and the correction term collapses. Sinkhorn-Drifting [(2603.12366)](2603.12366.md) sees the same issue and patches via the marginal constraint; Drifting [(2602.04770)](2602.04770.md) patches it with a `+1e6 В· I` diagonal-distance mask. W-Flow's fix is different and theoretically cleaner: **draw an independent second batch from the current generator** `{x'_l}_{l=1}^N` (i.i.d. resample from `q_t`), and compute the self-transport coupling between `{x_i}` and `{x'_l}`. Empirically (Tab 2c):

| OT cost | Mask | 2-batch | 1-NFE FID |
|---|---|---|---|
| `вҖ–В·вҖ–` (в„“вӮӮ) | вҖ” | вң“ | 7.16 |
| `ВҪвҖ–В·вҖ–ВІ` (quadratic) | вҖ” | вң“ | **7.08** |
| `ВҪвҖ–В·вҖ–ВІ` | вҖ” | вҖ” (1-batch self-transport) | **17.57** вҶҗ catastrophic |
| `ВҪвҖ–В·вҖ–ВІ` | вң“ (diagonal mask) | вҖ” | 7.45 |

The 1-batch self-estimator nearly triples FID; the diagonal mask is a partial fix; the two-batch is the right answer. The cost is **doubling the number of generator forwards per training step** (and one extra Sinkhorn run). Cheap relative to backward pass.

## Algorithm 1 (training)

```
repeat
    z, z' ~ p_ref                         # two reference batches (size N)
    y    ~ p_data                          # target batch (size M)
    x  вҶҗ f_Оё(z),  x' вҶҗ sg(f_Оё(z'))        # second batch stop-grad'd
    О ^Оө_{x, y}   вҶҗ Sinkhorn(x, y;  Оө, L)
    О ^Оө_{x, x'}  вҶҗ Sinkhorn(x, x'; Оө, L)
    T^Оө_{q,p}(x_i) = (1/a_i) ОЈ_j (О ^Оө_{x,y })_{ij} y_j
    T^Оө_{q,q}(x_i) = (1/a_i) ОЈ_l (О ^Оө_{x,x'})_{il} x'_l
    V^Оө(x_i) = T^Оө_{q,p}(x_i) вҲ’ T^Оө_{q,q}(x_i)
    xМғ_i = stopgrad(x_i + О· V^Оө(x_i))
    вҲҮ_Оё L = вҲҮ_Оё (1/N) ОЈ вҖ–f_Оё(z_i) вҲ’ xМғ_iвҖ–ВІ
until converged
```

Key choices from Tab 5: `Оө = 0.05`, Sinkhorn iterations `L = 10`, step size `О· = 1.0`, `N = N_neg = 64`, `N_pos = 128` per class, `B = 8192` effective batch. **Stop-grad on the target `xМғ` (and on `x'`) is structural** вҖ” the regression-with-stop-grad shape exactly matches the JKO frozen-field discretisation that [2603.09936](2603.09936.md) identifies. Backprop never goes through the Sinkhorn iteration or the OT plan; the plan is treated as an empirical oracle. This is the property that distinguishes W-Flow from Sinkhorn GANs (which auto-diff through Sinkhorn and pay numerical instability + memory).

## Convergence theorem (Thm 3.1)

The headline theoretical result. Under regularity conditions (Lipschitz `V`, smooth-density limit, see Assumption A.1 of the paper):

- The finite-particle discrete updates `x^{(k+1)} = x^{(k)} + О· V^Оө_{qМӮ^N_k, pМӮ^M}(x^{(k)})` admit a piecewise-linear interpolation with empirical measure `qМӮ^{N,M,О·}_t`.
- As `О· вҶ’ 0`, `N, M вҶ’ вҲһ`, the interpolation converges (in `sup_{t вҲҲ [0,T]} WвӮӮ`) to the unique weak solution of the continuity equation `вҲӮ_t q_t + вҲҮВ·(q_t V^Оө_{q_t, p}) = 0`.

In plain terms: **the batch-level OT updates we run during training are a consistent finite-sample, finite-step approximation of the true continuous-time WGF on `S_Оө`.** No analogous result exists for Drifting's mean-shift drift. The proof uses PicardвҖ“LindelГ¶f for the particle ODE plus a GrГ¶nwall stability estimate (Appendix A). The constants depend on `(L_1, L_2, L_3)` Lipschitz bounds on `V`; for high-d / low-Оө these blow up, mirroring Sinkhorn-Drifting's `П„^{1-d/2}` rate.

## CFG via velocity-level guidance (not distribution mixing)

A side technical contribution worth keeping. Drifting bakes CFG in at training time by *mixing the negative distribution* with unconditionals: `qМғ^w_Оё(x|c) := (1/(w+1)) q_Оё(x|c) + (w/(w+1)) p(x|вҲ…)`. The paper proves this corresponds to a **linearly interpolated** effective target вҖ” *not* the exponentially-tilted target that standard CFG uses. W-Flow instead applies guidance at the **velocity** level:

```
V^{Оө, w}_{q_Оё, p}(x) = [T^Оө_{q_Оё(В·|c), p(В·|c)}(x) вҲ’ T^Оө_{q_Оё(В·|c), q_Оё(В·|c)}(x)]
                    + w В· [T^Оө_{q_Оё(В·|c), p(В·|c)}(x) вҲ’ T^Оө_{q_Оё(В·|c), p(В·|вҲ…)}(x)]
```

Under the KL energy, this recovers exactly the standard exponentially-tilted CFG target (Appx B.3). Velocity guidance (`velo.`) consistently beats distribution guidance (`dist.`): 7.08 vs 7.29 FID at Оө=0.05 (Tab 2b) and 7.68 vs 10.17 under the KL ablation (Tab 8). **CFG is baked at training time, so inference stays 1 forward pass even with guidance** вҖ” same property iMF gets via its `Пү` conditioning. The training-time cost is one extra Sinkhorn call per step (for the genвҶ”unconditional barycentric).

# Architecture and feature space

**Generator:** DiT (Peebles-Xie) at three scales вҖ” B/2 (133 M), L/2 (463 M), XL/2 (679 M). Same arch as Drifting and iMF; no W-Flow-specific architectural change. Patch size 2Г—2, register tokens 16, style-embedding tokens 32.

**Feature space:** identical pipeline to Drifting. Pretrained **latent-MAE ResNet** with 50% masking ratio, multi-scale block widths (640 channels at L/XL scales), `latent-MAE` pretrain for 1280 epochs then class-conditional fine-tune. Trained on the SD-VAE latent space (Г—4 reduction); Sinkhorn divergence is computed in this latent space, not pixel space. **Drifting's failure mode вҖ” pixel-space ImageNet does not converge without a learned ПҶ вҖ” was not retested in W-Flow; the latent setup is inherited wholesale.** This is the load-bearing dependency for any audio port: we need the audio-ПҶ analogue.

**Effective compute:** 8 Г— H100 nodes, ~3 days for L/2 at 1280 epochs.

# Inference cost

**1 NFE.** Identical to Drifting / iMF / pMF / SoFlow. The generator is a static feed-forward DiT; sampling is `x = f_Оё(z, c, w)` with `z ~ N(0, I)`. Throughput (single H100, batch 4, including VAE decode):

# Training cost

The per-step training overhead vs a standard DiT regression is:

1. One extra generator forward (for the second batch `x'`) вҖ” doubles generator FLOPs per step.
2. Two Sinkhorn-Knopp runs of L=10 iterations on NГ—M (genвҶ’real) and NГ—N (genвҶ’gen) Gibbs kernels вҖ” small, no autograd, no backprop through the iterations.
3. Latent-MAE ResNet forward + multiple feature-map extractions per particle for the ПҶ-space loss вҖ” same as Drifting.

L/2: ~3 days on 8Г—H100 at 1280 epochs. Drifting reports similar at 1280 epochs. Tab 4: W-Flow at **640 epochs matches Drifting at 1280 epochs** (FID 1.63 vs 1.75 on B/2; 1.46 vs 1.54 on L/2). The Sinkhorn-WGF dynamics converge ~2Г— faster than Drifting's heuristic dynamics вҖ” counterbalancing the extra forward.

## Mode coverage (the structural argument)

Figure 6 of the paper: a 2D Gaussian mixture with **6 dominant modes + 2 distant minority modes**. Drifting collapses entirely onto the dominant clusters; W-Flow recovers all 8. PCA of generated FFHQ latents under a **95% senior / 5% child** imbalanced training distribution shows Drifting drops the minority-child mode entirely while W-Flow preserves it. **The two-batch self-transport term is doing the work**: by enforcing the genвҶ’gen marginal constraint at every step, particles can't all flock to the nearest mode. This is the structural fix to the known Drifting mode-collapse failure.

For us this is important: any 1-step audio recipe will face *much* more multimodal training distributions than ImageNet-256 (many instruments, many recording conditions, many genres). Mode collapse is the canonical 1-step adversarial failure ([Vendi (2210.02410)](2210.02410.md), [Density & Coverage (2002.09797)](2002.09797.md)) and W-Flow is the first WGF-family method with an explicit *structural* fix for it (vs RpGAN's loss-landscape fix or DMD2's real-data-GAN composition).

## Domain transfer

W-Flow can define a WGF between *any* two distributions вҖ” not just `N(0,I) вҶ’ data`. Set `p_ref = p_source_data` and parameterise `f_Оё(z) = z + N_Оё(z)` (residual, zero-init final layer). Fig 5: one-step **seniorвҶ’young-adult face translation** on FFHQ, 4-layer MLP in the ALAE latent space (512D). W-Flow generates **shorter в„“вӮӮ source-target transports** and better identity preservation than Drifting вҖ” consistent with the OT-induced velocity being nearly straight (Fig 5a: oval-to-circle toy shows W-Flow draws short straight paths; Drifting throws particles far from initial positions).

For audio this is the **timbre-translation / style-transfer / DAC-resynthesis primitive**: one-step source-domain вҶ’ target-domain map with OT-shortest paths. The setup (`p_ref = p_source`, residual generator, zero-init last layer) is dimension-agnostic.

# How this translates to raw audio

**The Sinkhorn-WGF machinery is data-domain-agnostic** and the audio-translation arguments from [Drifting (2602.04770)](2602.04770.md) and [Sinkhorn-Drifting (2603.12366)](2603.12366.md) carry over unchanged, with three additional considerations:

## 1. Sinkhorn on waveforms is well-defined

The Sinkhorn iteration only needs a cost function `c(x, y)` between samples. On waveforms the natural choices are (a) в„“вӮӮ on raw samples (likely poor вҖ” phase-sensitive), (b) в„“вӮӮ in an audio-ПҶ space (AudioMAE / EnCodec / waveform-MAE features), (c) a perceptual cost like MR-STFT distance. The paper's ablation (Tab 2c) prefers **quadratic cost** `ВҪвҖ–xвҲ’yвҖ–ВІ` over в„“вӮӮ вҖ” relevant when picking the audio cost. Lifting via a feature map `ПҶ` is explicitly supported in В§3.4: compute `S_Оө` on `{ПҶ(x_i)}, {ПҶ(y_j)}`; backprop through `ПҶ` to update `Оё` while keeping the OT plan stop-grad'd. **Direct analogue of Drifting's multi-feature setup, only with Sinkhorn-WGF as the per-feature loss.**

## 2. The two-batch self-transport solves a real audio failure mode

Drifting on audio with a low-temperature kernel (the regime audio ПҶ-space sits in, per [Sinkhorn-Drifting (2603.12366)](2603.12366.md)) will hit the self-collapse problem unless masked. **W-Flow's two-batch estimator removes the mask entirely** and is the empirically-best fix on images (1-batch self-FID 17.57 вҶ’ 2-batch 7.08; diagonal-mask 7.45 sits between). For audio, where (a) batches are smaller because of waveform memory, (b) intra-batch self-similarity is higher (more compressible signal), the self-collapse failure should be *worse*; W-Flow's fix is more important here than it is on images.

## 3. Mode coverage on multimodal audio corpora

Real audio corpora are highly multimodal across genre / instrument / pitch / loudness. Drifting's mode-drop failure ([Fig 6 of W-Flow paper](#)) generalises directly. **W-Flow is the only 1-NFE method in the wiki with a structural anti-mode-collapse fix that doesn't depend on a discriminator** (cf RpGAN, ARC-contrastive, DMD2-real-data-GAN вҖ” all adversarial). For an APT-free path this is the strongest candidate.

## What ports cleanly, what needs surgery

| Component | Audio port |
|---|---|
| Sinkhorn iteration | Cheap, no autograd вҖ” direct. L=10 on NГ—M kernel matrices is small relative to a 1-D waveform forward. |
| Two-batch self-transport | Direct. Doubles generator forwards per training step вҖ” same cost shape as MeanFlow. |
| Velocity-level CFG | Audio analogue: text/class conditioning + unconditional samples in the same batch; extra Sinkhorn between conditional gen and unconditional data. Direct. |
| WGF convergence theorem | Survives вҖ” assumptions are on `V` regularity, not on data dimension. The `П„^{1-d/2}` rate in Prop 3.8 (audio is high-d) means we'll need bigger batches at small Оө. |
| Latent-MAE ПҶ pretrain | **Needs audio-ПҶ analogue.** Same blocker as Drifting. Either an audio waveform-MAE (custom) or a pretrained AudioMAE/EnCodec encoder. The paper doesn't revisit whether off-the-shelf encoders work; Drifting found custom > off-the-shelf. |
| DiT generator | If we go 1-D Transformer over patched waveform (Г  la pMF) this ports. Otherwise the conditioning interface needs FiLM. |
| Domain transfer setup | Direct port вҖ” useful for `wavвҶ’wav` style transfer / audio super-resolution. |

## What doesn't translate

- **Classifier-free guidance scale via class labels.** Audio is mostly unconditional or text-conditioned; CFG-as-conditioning is only useful for the text-cond case.
- **ImageNet-style FID** вҖ” replace with FAD on multiple ПҶ's per [evaluation](../wiki/concepts/evaluation.md).
- **VAE-latent training space** вҖ” for raw audio we'd train in waveform / STFT / EnCodec-latent space; the paper's latent-Sinkhorn structure should still work but the ПҶ choice changes.

# Strengths

- **SOTA 1-NFE on ImageNet 256ВІ (1.29)** вҖ” beats Drifting (1.54), iMF (1.72), pMF (2.22), SoFlow (2.96). New ceiling.
- **Principled.** Energy `F` вҶ’ `V = вҲ’вҲҮ ОҙF/Оҙq` вҶ’ discrete training updates вҶ’ static compressed map. Drifting's heuristic shape now has theory underneath.
- **Convergence theorem (Thm 3.1)** вҖ” the only WGF-family 1-NFE method with a real continuous-time particle-convergence result.
- **Self-transport is a real fix to mode collapse**, not just a hand-mask. Empirical evidence on imbalanced FFHQ is striking.
- **Identifiability hole closed**: `V^Оө вүЎ 0 вҮ” q = p` follows from `S_Оө = 0 вҮ” q = p` (Feydy 2019, Hardion-Lacombe 2026).
- **Velocity-level CFG** preserves the standard exponential-tilt target вҖ” Drifting's distribution-mixing CFG approximates it linearly. New, cleaner CFG primitive.
- **Inference identical to Drifting** вҖ” 1 NFE, no extra inference cost vs the baseline this paper supersedes.
- **Sample-efficient**: 640-epoch W-Flow matches 1280-epoch Drifting at every scale.

# Limitations / things to test

- **Two-stage training**: the W-Flow stage compresses a training-time WGF into a static generator. **There's no claim that one can run the WGF inference-time (multi-step) at *better* quality** вҖ” the paper measures only 1-NFE. So unlike EqM, W-Flow doesn't buy any inference-time adaptive-compute property. If we wanted multi-step inference we'd have to redesign.
- **No pixel-space ImageNet result.** All headline numbers are in SD-VAE latent space. The ПҶ-only argument from Drifting (pixel-ImageNet fails without learned ПҶ) is presumably unchanged but untested. **For raw-waveform audio, we'd be in the pixel-equivalent regime by default.**
- **Audio-ПҶ dependency unresolved.** Same as Drifting and Sinkhorn-Drifting вҖ” we need an audio encoder. The paper offers no new evidence on what works as ПҶ.
- **No CPU latency anywhere in the paper.** GPU throughput is reported (Tab 6); CPU isn't measured. Identical to most 1-NFE image papers.
- **Two-batch doubles generator forwards** per training step. Concretely: at 1 NFE inference cost, training cost is ~2Г— a single-pass FM regression. Manageable for B/2 (133 M) on a 4090; potentially painful at XL scale.
- **High-d Г— low-Оө statistical rate.** Prop 3.8-style constants (inherited from Sinkhorn-Drifting Prop 3.8) imply that at audio dimensionality with small Оө we'll need larger batches than at ImageNet-latent dimensionality. Empirical question whether `N=64` suffices.
- **Sinkhorn iterations vs FID** (Tab 7): L=1 вҶ’ 7.92, L=5 вҶ’ 7.45, L=10 вҶ’ 7.29, L=20 вҶ’ 7.33. Plateaus at 10. We'd hope the same holds at audio scale; the paper doesn't characterise the convergence-vs-data-distribution dependence.
- **CIFAR / smaller-scale not reported.** EqM showed FM-vs-EqM relative ranking flips at lower dim; whether W-Flow's gains hold at audio-scale dimensionality (`d вүҲ 22k` for 1 s @ 22 kHz vs ImageNet-latent `d вүҲ 4k`) is an open empirical question.
- **No text-to-image / video / OOD-generalisation experiments.** The convergence theorem is asymptotic; finite-network behaviour outside class-conditional ImageNet untested.
- **No comparison to FD-loss post-training** ([2604.28190](2604.28190.md)), which achieves 0.72 FID @ 1 NFE on a pMF base. W-Flow is the strongest *from-scratch* 1-NFE, but FD-loss post-training of a W-Flow base could plausibly push lower вҖ” untested composition.

# Open questions for this project

1. **What is the audio analogue of the latent-MAE feature encoder?** Train a 1-D waveform-MAE on a music corpus and use it as `ПҶ` for Sinkhorn-on-features. Sanity check: does it stratify pairs of clips by perceptual similarity?
2. **Two-batch self-transport at small N.** On audio we'll likely have `N вүӨ 32` due to memory. At N=32, does the two-batch estimator still beat the diagonal-mask alternative? Sweep N.
3. **Sinkhorn `Оө` on audio.** Paper uses Оө=0.05 in SD-VAE latent space. Audio `ПҶ` will have a different scale; sweep over Оө вҲҲ [0.01, 1.0] on a 1 s drum corpus.
4. **Multi-stage audio benchmark.** Train W-Flow on raw-waveform (1 s @ 22 kHz) with an in-house waveform-MAE-ПҶ; compare to (a) Drifting same ПҶ, (b) iMF (no ПҶ), (c) IMM (no ПҶ), (d) EqM (no ПҶ), on a fixed audio corpus. CPU inference latency for each.
5. **Does the velocity-level CFG primitive matter for text-to-audio?** The CFG-as-conditioning trick from iMF is dimensional-agnostic; W-Flow's velocity-CFG is a strict refinement under KL. Empirical question on T2A.
6. **W-Flow + FD-loss post-training.** Take a trained W-Flow model and apply FD-loss ([2604.28190](2604.28190.md)) for further 1-NFE refinement. iMF+FD-loss is the canonical stack; W-Flow+FD-loss should push lower.
7. **Audio domain transfer via residual W-Flow.** Resynthesise / style-transfer one-step: `p_ref = clean speech`, target = noisy speech, residual generator. Tests whether the OT-shortest-path argument helps audio identity preservation.
8. **Does the structural mode-coverage fix help on imbalanced-genre training corpora?** Synthetic test: 95% pop / 5% jazz training, check whether jazz survives in samples.
