## TL;DR (project-relevant)

**The fourth independent "answer to the JVP problem" — not at the identity level (like SoFlow/SplitMeanFlow) or the regression-target level (like iMF), but at the *objective-decomposition* level.** AlphaFlow algebraically rewrites the **existing** MeanFlow loss as `L_MF = L_TFM + L_TC_c` (trajectory flow matching + trajectory consistency, Eq. 6), then **measures the gradient cosine** between the two terms and finds it is **strongly negative (≲ −0.4) throughout training** — the two terms are in direct optimisation conflict. The α-family (Def. 1, Eq. 8) is a one-parameter interpolant that unifies **flow matching (α=1), Shortcut Models (α=½), MeanFlow (α→0)**, and (for the `z_0`-parameterised network) **discrete-time CT (any α) and continuous-time CT (α→0)** — Theorem 1. Training with a **sigmoid curriculum** on α (Algorithm 2: anneal 1→0 over 150K–250K steps) keeps the early phase on the low-conflict FM manifold and only later transitions to the high-bias MeanFlow objective. New **1-NFE SOTA** on ImageNet-256×256 from-scratch with vanilla DiT-XL/2+: **FID 2.58 / FDD 148.4 (1-NFE), FID 2.15 / FDD 96.8 (2-NFE)** — beats every prior from-scratch fastforward (MeanFlow-XL/2+ 2.20 at 1000 epochs, FACM-XL/2 2.07 with 2× compute per epoch), at 240+60 epochs with no JVP elimination needed and no architecture changes. **The contribution is conceptual + curriculum, not identity-replacement** — both AlphaFlow's α=0 branch (which is just MeanFlow) and its α>0 branch use the same training loop including the standard JVP at the MeanFlow end. The portable take for raw audio: even when JVP is available, training MeanFlow as a curriculum from FM through the α-family is empirically better than training MeanFlow directly. Code: `github.com/snap-research/alphaflow`.

## The problem the paper actually addresses

Three problems sit unsolved in the MeanFlow family circa late 2025:

1. **Why does MeanFlow spend 75 % of training on `r = t` border-case samples?** (Geng 2025 reported this empirically — they sample `r = t` for 75 % of training, even though the inference task is `r < t` trajectory leaps.) **Why does this work?** No theoretical explanation existed prior to this paper.
2. **The MeanFlow loss is opaque.** Squaring `‖u_θ − v_t + (t−r)·du_θ/dt‖²` mixes a "consistency" term with a "flow matching" term in ways that aren't separable in the written form.
3. **What's the design space?** Shortcut Models, MeanFlow, and FM look like three unrelated training recipes — is there a single space that contains all three?

AlphaFlow answers (1) and (2) via the loss decomposition + gradient analysis, and (3) via the α-family.

## The loss decomposition (Eq. 6 / Appx. D.1)

Expanding the squared MeanFlow loss and dropping `θ`-independent terms:

```
L_MF(θ) = E[ ‖u_θ(z_t, r, t) − v_t‖² ]                          ← L_TFM  (Trajectory Flow Matching)
        + E[ 2(t−r) · u_θᵀ(z_t, r, t) · du_θ⁻/dt ]              ← L_TC_c (Trajectory Consistency, continuous)
        + C
```

`u_θ⁻` is `stopgrad(u_θ)`. The two terms have clean independent interpretations:

- **L_TFM** is a flow-matching loss conditional on an extra modelling input `r` — exactly vanilla FM if the network ignores `r`. The optimal manifold for L_TFM is **narrow** (the regression target `v_t = ε − x` is unique).
- **L_TC_c** is a `(t−r)`-reweighted **continuous consistency loss without a boundary condition** — it would normally collapse to a constant (Song et al. 2023's `z_0`-parameterisation trick is what enforces the boundary in standard CT). Here L_TFM implicitly supplies the boundary, which is why MeanFlow training doesn't collapse. The optimal manifold for L_TC_c is **very large** (any constant output minimises it).

**The gradient analysis is the empirical contribution.** Fig. 2a measures cos(∇L_TFM, ∇L_TC_c) on DiT-B/2 throughout 400K training steps — it sits **at or below −0.4 for the entire run**. The two losses are pulling in opposite directions: L_TC_c's broad manifold drags the parameters away from L_TFM's narrow one. This is the structural reason vanilla MeanFlow needs the 75 % `r = t` heuristic — without it, L_TC_c wins and L_TFM never converges.

**Why the heuristic works.** The 75 % `r = t` flow-matching supervision (call it L_FM') is a "slice" of L_TFM at the `p(t, r | r = t)` marginal. Fig. 2c shows: training with 75 % `r = t` supervision lowers L_TFM substantially while barely affecting L_TC_c — L_FM' has **much lower gradient conflict with L_TC_c** (cos(∇L_FM', ∇L_TC_c) ≈ 0 on average, vs −0.4 for L_TFM). So Geng's 75 % heuristic is doing two things simultaneously: directly reducing L_TFM via the `r = t` slice, **and** introducing a low-conflict surrogate that lets L_TC_c train alongside it. But it wastes 75 % of compute on the flow-matching task, not on the actual MeanFlow trajectory-jump problem.

## The α-Flow loss (Def. 1 / Eq. 8) and the unification theorem

```
L_α(θ) ≜ E[ α⁻¹ · ‖u_θ(z_t, r, t) − (α · v̄_{s,t} + (1−α) · u_θ⁻(z_s, r, s))‖² ]
```

where `s = α·r + (1−α)·t` is the intermediate timestep (a convex combination of `r` and `t` parameterised by `α ∈ [0, 1]`), `z_s = z_t + (s−t)·v̄_{s,t}` propagates `z_t` to `s` via the **shift velocity** `v̄_{s,t}`, and `u_θ⁻` is stop-grad. The regression target is a **convex combination of the FM velocity** (`v̄_{s,t}`, weighted α) **and the stop-grad student's own prediction at `s`** (`u_θ⁻(z_s, r, s)`, weighted 1−α).

**Theorem 1 (unification, proven in Appx. D.2).** For the `u_θ`-parameterisation with `v̄_{s,t} = v_t`:

- **α = 1** → **L_TFM = L_{α=1}**. Pure trajectory flow matching.
- **α = ½** → **L_SC = ½ · L_{α=½}** with `v̄_{s,t} = u_θ⁻(z_t, s, t)`. **Shortcut Models** are the α = ½ slice with the specific shift-velocity choice that uses the student's own velocity for the half-step.
- **α → 0** → **∇L_α = ∇L_MF**. **MeanFlow** is the continuous α → 0 limit. The proof unpacks a Taylor expansion `u_θ⁻(z_{t−Δt}, r, t−Δt) = u_θ⁻ − du_θ⁻/dt·Δt + O(Δt²)` and shows the leading-order term recovers the MeanFlow gradient.

For the `z_0`-parameterised network `u_θ(z_t, 0, t) = (z_t − f_θ(z_t, t))/t` (i.e. the "diffusion-prediction" form), Theorem 1 additionally gives:

- **L_α = L_CT_d** at any fixed `α = δ ∈ (0, t)` → **Discrete-time CT** is α-Flow with a fixed α.
- **∇L_α = ∇L_CT_c** as α → 0 → **Continuous-time CT** is the α → 0 limit.

So **the α-knob is the master design parameter** of the whole few-step family. FM, Shortcut, MeanFlow, discrete CT, continuous CT all sit at different points along this single axis.

## The curriculum (Algorithm 2)

Train α as a function of training step `k` via a sigmoid annealing:

```
scale  = 1 / (k_e − k_s)
offset = −(k_s + k_e) / 2 / (k_e − k_s)
α = 1 − sigmoid((scale·k + offset) · γ)
α = 1   if α > 1 − η
α = 0   if α < η
```

with `γ = 25` (temperature), `η = 5×10⁻³` (clamping). Three phases:

1. **α = 1: Trajectory Flow Matching pretraining (0 → k_s steps).** Pure low-variance FM. Establishes the noise-to-data map and provides a good initialisation for the high-variance MeanFlow phase. Sigmoid_{150K → 250K} is best for DiT-B/2.
2. **α ∈ (0, 1): α-Flow transition.** Gradually move from FM toward MeanFlow. Each value of α is a different point in the family; the "high-bias / low-variance" objective at α≈1 (FM) progressively becomes the "low-bias / high-variance" objective at α≈0 (MeanFlow).
3. **α = 0: MeanFlow fine-tuning.** Final phase on the original MeanFlow objective. Because L_TFM is already well-optimised, L_TC_c has less to fight against and few-step quality improves.

**The empirical headline** (Table 2a, DiT-B/2): Constant_{0.0} (= pure MeanFlow) gives 1-NFE FID 44.4 / FDD 844.1. Sigmoid_{0K → 400K} (full curriculum across the whole training run) gives **1-NFE FID 40.0 / FDD 785.4**. Earlier or later transitions are worse. Table 2b shows α-Flow beats vanilla MeanFlow at **every** flow-matching ratio (0/25/50/75 % `r = t`); MeanFlow needs 75 % to reach FID 43.1, α-Flow hits FID 40.0 at 25 % — i.e. **the curriculum reduces the dependence on the `r = t` border-case heuristic**, freeing compute for actual MeanFlow trajectory-jump training.

## Results (Table 1 + Table 6, ImageNet-256, vanilla DiT)

ImageNet-1K 256² class-conditional, SD-VAE latent, vanilla DiT backbones (no architecture changes from MeanFlow paper):

| Method | Params | Epochs | 1-NFE FID / FDD | 2-NFE FID / FDD |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Shortcut-XL/2 (Frans 2025) | 675M | 160 | 10.60 | — |
| IMM-XL/2 (Zhou 2025) | 676M | 3840 | 8.05 / 3.88 | — |
| MeanFlow-XL/2 (Geng 2025a) | 676M | 240 | 3.43 | 2.93 |
| MeanFlow-XL/2+ (Geng 2025a) | 676M | 1000 | — | 2.20 |
| FACM-XL/2 (Peng 2025) | 675M | 800 + 250×2 | — | 2.07† |
| α-Flow-B/2 (ours) | 131M | 240 | 5.40 / 287.1 | 5.01 / 231.8 |
| α-Flow-XL/2 (ours) | 676M | 240 | 2.95 / 164.6 | 2.34 / 105.7 |
| **α-Flow-XL/2+ (ours)** | **676M** | **240 + 60** | **2.58 / 148.4** | **2.15 / 96.8** |

(† FACM requires ~2× training compute per epoch.)

- α-Flow-XL/2 at 240 epochs beats MeanFlow-XL/2 at 240 epochs by **15 % relative (12 % FDD)**: 2.95 vs 3.43 FID.
- α-Flow-XL/2+ at 240+60 epochs (60-epoch large-batch 1024 fine-tune, the "MeanFlow-XL/2+" recipe transplanted) reaches **2.58 FID / 148.4 FDD at 1-NFE and 2.15 FID / 96.8 FDD at 2-NFE** — beats both MeanFlow-XL/2+ (2.20 @ 2-NFE, 1000 epochs) and FACM-XL/2 (2.07 @ 2-NFE) with **23 % of FACM's training epochs**.
- With balanced class sampling (Table 6, an evaluation choice not a method change), α-Flow-XL/2+ reaches **1-NFE FID 2.44 / FDD 147.2 / FCD 3.04, 2-NFE FID 1.95 / FDD 94.6**.

**Architecture-level point:** these numbers are on **vanilla DiT**. No JVP elimination, no interval-aware attention, no decoupled `(t, Δt)` embedding, no in-context conditioning swap. The win is purely from the objective + curriculum.

## Ablation highlights (Table 5, DiT-B/2)

The detailed ablations split which of the α-Flow design choices matter most. With α = 10⁻² fixed, no curriculum:

| Variant | 1-NFE FID / FDD |
|---|---|
| Shortcut Model (v̄ = u_θ⁻(z_t, s, t)) | 59.8 / 1017.3 |
| α-Flow with v̄ = v_t (FM velocity, non-EMA) | 59.2 / 964.6 |
| + adaptive loss `ω = α/(‖Δ‖² + c)` | 49.7 / 845.2 |
| + α = 0.005 (optimal consistency step ratio) | 46.2 / 860.8 |
| MeanFlow (Constant_{0.0}, no curriculum) | 43.3 / 822.3 |
| α-Flow full curriculum, 25 % flow matching | 40.0 / 785.4 |

Take-aways:

- **Shift velocity must be `v_t`, not `u_θ⁻(z_t, s, t)`.** Shortcut Models' choice is structurally worse — and only "works" at α = 0.5 because the network sees a half-step. At small α, the Shortcut choice diverges.
- **No EMA for `u_θ⁻`.** Table 5a: EMA + `u_θ⁻` gives FID 188 / FDD 1762; non-EMA + `v_t` gives 59.2 / 964.6.
- **Adaptive loss `ω = α / (‖Δ‖²_2 + c)`** (Geng et al. derive an adaptive loss `1/(L_MF + c)`; via Eq. 12 the α-Flow equivalent is `α/(L_α + c) ≈ α/(‖Δ‖² + c)` since the L₂ error is the load-bearing term) gives FID 59.2 → 49.7.
- **Consistency step ratio sweep** (Table 5c): α = 5×10⁻³ is optimal. That's why the schedule clamps at η = 5×10⁻³.
- **Curriculum** (Sigmoid_{150K → 250K} on DiT-XL/2): the entire ~20 % FID drop from 49.7 to 40.0 comes from gradually transitioning α rather than holding it fixed.

## Architecture, training cost, JVP

- **Same vanilla DiT** (DiT-B/2: 131M / 23.1 GFLOPs, DiT-XL/2: 676M / 119.0 GFLOPs) as MeanFlow paper. Patch 2×2, depth 12/28, hidden 768/1152.
- **Optimizer:** Adam (β₁=0.9, β₂=0.95), lr 1e-4, constant, no dropout, no weight decay, EMA half-life 6931, grad clip 16. Batch 256 for the main run, 1024 for the +60-epoch fine-tune.
- **JVP is still present** at α = 0 (the algorithm dispatches to `torch.func.jvp(fn, (zt, r, t), (v, 0, 1))` when α == 0; otherwise it uses an extra forward pass at `(zs, r, s)`). **The paper does not eliminate the JVP** — it changes when JVP is invoked (only in the final α = 0 phase) and how training balances the two terms in the meantime. This is the **key contrast** with SoFlow / SplitMeanFlow / TiM-DDE.
- **Sampling:** Algorithm 3 supports both **ODE sampling** (`z ← z − (t_n − t_m)·fn(z, r=t_m, t=t_n)`) and **consistency sampling** (`z ← fn(z, r=0, t=t_n) + t_m·randn`). Fig. 4: consistency sampling is better at larger model scale (XL/2 best 2-NFE FID 2.09 @ intermediate t=0.4); ODE sampling is better for the original MeanFlow-XL/2 (best 2.39 @ t=0.35). They use consistency sampling for XL/2 and ODE for B/2.
- **CFG:** Same as MeanFlow — `v̄_{s,t} = w·v(z_t, t|x) + κ·u_θ⁻(z_t, t, c) + (1 − w − κ)·u_θ⁻(z_t, t, ∅)`. CFG settings differ by scale: w=0.2, κ=0.92, CFG-triggered `t ∈ [0.0, 0.75]` for XL/2.

## How this translates to raw audio

**The α-Flow contribution is architecturally invisible — it's a training-time recipe, not a backbone change.** Any 1-D conv U-Net or Transformer trained with the MeanFlow objective can switch to α-Flow training by:

1. Implementing `L_α` (Eq. 8) — three forward passes through `u_θ` at `(z_t, r, t)`, `(z_s, r, s)`, and the JVP path used at α = 0. The α=0 branch is identical to vanilla MeanFlow training.
2. Adding the sigmoid α-schedule (Algorithm 2) with `γ = 25`, `η = 5×10⁻³`. Recommended schedule on a fresh waveform run: `Sigmoid_{0.4·K → 0.65·K}` over total training steps K.
3. Adopting the adaptive loss weight `ω = α/(‖Δ‖² + c)` with `c = 10⁻³`.
4. Setting `v̄_{s,t} = v_t` (FM velocity), **not** the shortcut choice `u_θ⁻(z_t, s, t)`. No EMA on `u_θ⁻`.
5. Setting `r = t` ratio to **25 %** (vs MeanFlow's 75 %) — α-Flow's reduced reliance on the border-case heuristic is the second-biggest empirical win after the curriculum itself.

**Three audio-relevant structural reads:**

- **The "trajectory consistency manifold is too large" argument is dimension-agnostic.** If anything, raw audio's much-lower intrinsic dimensionality vs ImageNet-latents means L_TC_c's manifold is **even larger** relative to L_TFM's — the gradient-conflict problem should be at least as severe on waveform. The curriculum should help proportionally more.
- **CFG handling is decoupled from the curriculum.** The CFG mechanism is unchanged from MeanFlow. For unconditional waveform generation we can drop the CFG term entirely (set w = 1, κ = 0); for mel-conditioned vocoder use the standard CFG path.
- **The schedule's wall-clock cost is zero** — only a different sampling distribution for α and one extra forward pass during the α ∈ (0, 1) phase. No memory overhead, no FlashAttention-blocking JVP outside the final α = 0 phase. **On a 1-D conv backbone with no attention, the JVP isn't a blocker anyway** — so AlphaFlow's main competitor on raw audio isn't "JVP-free MeanFlow" (SplitMeanFlow, SoFlow) but **"vanilla MeanFlow with a worse training recipe"**.

**What AlphaFlow doesn't buy for audio:**

- No CPU benchmark, no GFLOPs/sample inference numbers — same gap as every other MeanFlow-family paper.
- No 1-D experiments. The dimension-agnostic argument is strong but unvalidated.
- The JVP is still used at α = 0 — if our waveform Transformer needs FlashAttention/FSDP compatibility, AlphaFlow does not solve that. Compose with TiM's DDE (drop-in central FD) or SoFlow / SplitMeanFlow to get the FlashAttention compatibility back.

## How AlphaFlow sits relative to the other unifying frames

Two prior wiki papers (TiM 2509.04394 and IMM 2503.07565) and one being-ingested-next (Shortcut Models 2025) claim "unifying frames" for the few-step family. AlphaFlow is a fourth, **at a different axis from all three**:

- **IMM unifies the MMD-divergence side** of the consistency family (CM, GMMN, IMM, energy-distance as kernel/particle-count/time-pair points in IMM's design space).
- **TiM unifies the regression-target side** via the State Transition Identity over all `(t, r)` pairs — FM, diffusion, MeanFlow, iMF, SoFlow, CM, CTM, PCM, Shortcut are points in TiM's transport+supervision-granularity space.
- **AlphaFlow unifies the loss-decomposition side** — Shortcut, MeanFlow, FM (and discrete/continuous CT via the `z_0`-parameterisation) are points along a **single one-parameter α-knob**. It says less than TiM (it doesn't unify diffusion / iMF / SoFlow directly — those would need separate Taylor expansions or finite-interval extensions) but it makes the FM ↔ Shortcut ↔ MeanFlow trajectory **concrete and trainable** via the α-curriculum.

These three frames are **complementary, not competing**:

- TiM unifies what's supervised and over what time-pair distribution.
- IMM unifies how the distributional match is measured.
- AlphaFlow unifies how the per-sample trajectory-jump objective is interpolated from FM to MeanFlow.

A given concrete method sits in all three frames at different positions. The natural composition surface: **TiM's all-`(t, r)` supervision + IMM's `M ≥ 2` particle kernel + AlphaFlow's α-curriculum** is constructable, untested.

## How AlphaFlow sits relative to the JVP-elimination papers

The "JVP question" now has **four independent answers**:

| Paper | Axis | What it replaces | JVP at training time? |
|---|---|---|---|
| iMF (2512.02012) | regression target | network-dependent target → network-indep `(e − x)` | Kept, stop-gradded |
| SoFlow (2512.15657) | parameterisation + identity | average-velocity → solution-function + Taylor FD | Eliminated |
| SplitMeanFlow (2507.16884) | identity (algebraic) | differential MF identity → algebraic interval-splitting | Eliminated |
| TiM (2509.04394) DDE | local derivative inside target | JVP → central finite difference `(f(t+ε) − f(t−ε))/(2ε)` | Eliminated |
| **AlphaFlow (2510.20771)** | **objective decomposition + curriculum** | **— (JVP still present at α=0)** | **Kept** |

**AlphaFlow is the only one of the five that does not eliminate the JVP.** Its contribution is at a different level: it doesn't ask "how do we avoid the JVP?" but "given that we have it, how should we structure training so the JVP-containing MeanFlow objective is reached only after the optimisation conflict has been resolved by a low-conflict FM warm-up?" This is **orthogonal** to all four JVP-elimination directions — AlphaFlow's curriculum could in principle wrap around a SplitMeanFlow or TiM-DDE inner objective, replacing the α=0 vanilla-MeanFlow phase with a JVP-free one. Untested but structurally clean.

## Strengths

- **The decomposition is exact and the gradient analysis is decisive.** L_MF = L_TFM + L_TC_c is an algebraic identity, not a hypothesis. The cos(∇L_TFM, ∇L_TC_c) ≈ −0.4 measurement is the empirical smoking gun.
- **The α-family is a clean unification** of Shortcut, MeanFlow, FM, and (with `z_0`-parameterisation) discrete/continuous CT. Theorem 1 makes the limits rigorous.
- **The curriculum is parameter-light** (γ, k_s, k_e, η — four scalars).
- **SOTA at the headline metric (1-NFE FID 2.58 on ImageNet-256) on vanilla DiT.** No architecture tricks needed — refutes the implicit claim of TiM that the architecture is load-bearing.
- **Reproducible.** Public code at `github.com/snap-research/alphaflow`, pretrained checkpoints released for ImageNet-1K 256².

## Limitations (paper-honest + project-relevant)

The paper's own "Limitations" (Appx. B) and "Failed Experiments" (Appx. C) are notably candid:

- **The continuous limit `α → 0` is still important** — they cannot get the same quality from a discrete-α α-Flow without anneal-to-zero. Bias-variance trade-off in the consistency objective (Song 2023; Song & Dhariwal 2024).
- **Large-scale instability persists.** They occasionally see unstable training at scale with CFG, both for vanilla MeanFlow and α-Flow. **Not a silver bullet for CM instability.**
- **L_TFM-only pretraining means few-step performance is weak during the first phase**, making progress harder to monitor — relevant if compute is precious.
- **The gradient analysis is empirical, not theoretical.** Why FM matters for consistency is unexplained from first principles.
- **Several attempted refinements failed:** decomposed training with individually-tuned weighting functions (EDM-style) was *worse* than the default adaptive heuristic; LoRA fine-tuning and separate prediction heads for `v` and `u` didn't help; ~50 noise-schedule ablations didn't beat the default; representation-alignment losses (REPA) didn't justify the complexity; EMA schedule sweeps didn't matter.
- **No CPU benchmark, no GFLOPs/sample inference cost.** Standard MeanFlow-family gap.
- **No raw-audio or 1-D experiments.** Standard image-domain gap.
- **No head-to-head against iMF / SoFlow / SplitMeanFlow** at matched scale and epochs. The TiM-vs-AlphaFlow comparison is the highest-value missing experiment for the wiki's 1-NFE leaderboard.

## Open questions for this project

- **α-Flow on 1-D conv U-Net.** Does the FM→MeanFlow curriculum produce the same 15 % relative win on a 1-D backbone trained on raw waveform? Smallest experiment: CRASH-backbone + α-Flow with Sigmoid_{0.4·K → 0.65·K}, 25 % `r = t`, sweep against vanilla MeanFlow at matched compute.
- **AlphaFlow + iMF's v-loss + flexible-CFG conditioning.** The α-curriculum is structurally orthogonal to iMF's regression-target change and CFG-as-conditioning trick. Strict superset of both. Untested.
- **AlphaFlow + DDE (TiM).** Replace the JVP at α = 0 with TiM's central finite difference; keep the curriculum. Gets FlashAttention/FSDP compatibility without changing the loss structure. The most directly portable composition for audio Transformer backbones.
- **AlphaFlow with a SplitMeanFlow-style algebraic identity at α = 0.** Replace the JVP+MF objective at the final phase with SplitMeanFlow's `(t−r)·u(z_t,r,t) = (s−r)·u(z_s,r,s) + (t−s)·u(z_t,s,t)`. Pure α-curriculum with JVP-free terminal phase. Constructable, untested.
- **Audio-specific α-schedule.** The image schedule transitions over 100K–150K steps within a 400K total. Audio training tends to be shorter (drum dataset ~30K clips, smaller model). Does the relative schedule (k_s / K_total ≈ 0.4, k_e / K_total ≈ 0.65) hold, or does waveform need a longer FM phase because of high-frequency components?
- **Does the `r = t` ratio reduction (75 % → 25 %) transfer to waveform?** AlphaFlow's main implicit win is that it works at lower flow-matching ratio. On waveform, where the silent-region "no flow" problem (Flow2GAN's spectral-energy-inverse weighting) means `r = t` flow matching has a different bias-variance trade-off, this number may shift.
