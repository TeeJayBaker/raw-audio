## TL;DR (project-relevant)

Density and Coverage (D&C) are the **outlier-robust scalar pair** that should sit next to FAD whenever we benchmark a raw-audio generator. **Density** answers "is the fake sharp and inside the data manifold?" without being inflated by one weird real-sample blowing up a k-NN ball; **Coverage** answers "did we drop modes?" by checking, for each real point, whether any fake landed in its k-NN ball. The "high Density / low Coverage" signature is exactly the failure mode we expect from 1-NFE adversarial audio distillations (sharp but narrow). Both are drop-in for any audio feature space (CLAP, MERT, EnCodec, random CNN). The paper also empirically demonstrates that **randomly-initialised CNN features ("R64") beat ImageNet features for spectrogram evaluation** — direct evidence that off-the-shelf classifier features mis-rank audio, exactly the concern that motivates FAD-with-domain-models.

## What this paper actually says

It's an evaluation-metrics paper. The claim: Kynkäänniemi et al.'s "Improved Precision and Recall" (P&R, NeurIPS 2019) — which estimates real/fake manifolds as unions of per-sample k-NN hyperspheres — has four practical failures, and a small structural modification fixes all four. The modification is Density and Coverage.

### The P&R primitive (background)

Given embedded samples `{X_i}` real and `{Y_j}` fake, build a "manifold" as a union of per-sample k-NN hyperspheres:

```
manifold(X_1,...,X_N) := ⋃_i B(X_i, NND_k(X_i))
```

where `NND_k(X_i)` is the distance from `X_i` to its k-th nearest neighbour within `{X_i}`. Then:

- **Precision** = fraction of fakes that land in `manifold(reals)`.
- **Recall** = fraction of reals that land in `manifold(fakes)`.

### The four failures of P&R

1. **Doesn't return 1.0 on identical distributions.** When P = Q, precision and recall converge to roughly 0.68 in their R^64 Gaussian experiment — far below the sanity-check value of 1.
2. **Outlier-inflated Precision.** A single real outlier produces a huge k-NN ball, and fakes hugging that outlier all count as "in the real manifold". One outlier in real samples can push precision toward 1 in the wrong regime.
3. **Outlier-inflated Recall.** Symmetric: fake outliers grow the fake manifold; reals enclosed by that overinflated manifold all count. They show recall climbing 11–15% on CelebA/LSUN-Bedroom when 10% StyleGAN outliers are added — by contrast, coverage moves <2%.
4. **Recall misses simultaneous mode collapse.** When all-but-one modes shrink uniformly (rather than disappearing one-at-a-time), recall stays near 1.0 until the dominant mode holds >90% mass, then drops cliff-like. Coverage decays gradually from the start.

There's also a side complaint: P&R's k=3, M=N=50000 are picked without justification. D&C admits a closed-form expected value on P=Q, which lets you derive hyperparameters analytically.

### The fix

**Density** (replaces Precision):

```
density := (1 / kM) · Σ_j Σ_i 1[ Y_j ∈ B(X_i, NND_k(X_i)) ]
```

Instead of asking "is Y_j in *any* real hypersphere" (binary union), it counts **how many real-sample hyperspheres contain Y_j**. The manifold becomes a *superposition* rather than a *union* — conceptually halfway to a Parzen window estimate. One real outlier still produces a big hypersphere, but it only contributes a count of 1 to fakes that fall in it; fakes in dense real regions get counts of k or more, dominating the average. Not bounded by 1 — densely-clustered fakes inside well-populated real regions can score >1.

**Coverage** (replaces Recall):

```
coverage := (1/N) · Σ_i 1[ ∃ j s.t. Y_j ∈ B(X_i, NND_k(X_i)) ]
```

Fraction of *real* points that have at least one fake inside their k-NN hypersphere. **The hypersphere is built around reals, not fakes**, which kills two birds: (i) fake outliers no longer inflate the manifold, and (ii) the k-NN structure is built once per dataset rather than once per model — large speedup for training-curve monitoring (you recompute only the fake-to-real membership test as the model changes).

### Analytic guarantees (the killer feature)

Under P = Q (identical real and fake distributions) D&C admit closed-form expectations:

- `E[density] = 1` (exact, any N, M, k, any distribution, any dim).
- `E[coverage] = 1 − (N-1)(N-2)···(N-k) / [(M+N-1)(M+N-2)···(M+N-k)]`. As M = N → ∞, this approaches `1 − 1/2^k`.

These are distribution-free and dimension-free. Consequence: pick `k` to make `E[coverage]` close enough to 1. They target `> 0.95`, so **k = 5** (giving 1 − 1/32 = 0.969) with **M = N = 10,000** is their recommended default. By contrast, P&R has no analytic handle on P = Q — Equation 8 in the paper involves dependent indicator events `A_i^k` with no closed form.

### Embedding study (the audio-relevant part)

§4.2 of the paper directly tests their metrics on **audio**. They:

- Generate spectrograms with WaveGAN on SC09 (Speech Commands 0–9).
- Compute all four metrics under three embeddings: T4096 (ImageNet-pretrained VGG16 fc2), R4096 (randomly-initialised VGG16 fc2), R64 (randomly-initialised VGG16 with fc20 replaced to 64-d).

Result (Table 1 in the paper, Sound column): under **T4096**, recall and coverage both report ~0.02–0.03 for clearly diverse spoken-digit samples — the ImageNet features grossly underestimate diversity because spectrograms are out-of-distribution for ImageNet. Under **R64** the same generator scores recall 0.54, coverage 0.59 — sensible. Same story on MNIST.

**Their explicit recommendation**: when the target domain differs significantly from ImageNet (MNIST, spectrograms), use randomly-initialised CNN features instead of pretrained ones. Random CNN features still preserve the topology of the input space (it's the Deep Image Prior / Zhang-et-al.-perceptual-metric line of reasoning) but don't inject ImageNet-classifier bias.

### Other small things worth noting

- **Truncation analysis on StyleGAN/FFHQ** (Fig. 7): as ψ rises, precision/density fall and recall/coverage rise, all monotonically. **Density has larger dynamic range than precision** — 0 → ~4 vs precision saturating near 1 — making it the more sensitive fidelity dial.
- **VAE NLL comparison**: they truncate the VAE latent and observe NLL barely moves (41.4 → 40.2) while coverage drops sharply (0.411 → 0.291). D&C disambiguates the fidelity-vs-diversity component of a likelihood-trained model in a way NLL cannot.
- **Sample-size requirements**: N = M = 10,000 with k = 5 is enough to get E[coverage] ≈ 0.97 on P = Q. P&R needs 50,000 with no theoretical basis.

## Translates to raw audio

This paper is unusually friendly to audio: §4.2 already ran the experiment we'd want. Pulling the threads together for this project's regime (raw-sample-space, CPU inference, short clips):

- **Drop-in for any audio feature space.** The metric definitions only require pairwise distances over an embedding. Plug in CLAP, MERT, EnCodec/DAC encoder features, wav2vec2, or a randomly-initialised audio CNN — same formulas. For raw-sample short clips, candidates ordered by how much we trust them: domain-specific (MERT for music, CLAP for general-audio, a randomly-init 1D conv stack à la the paper for "no pretrain bias"). The CLAP backbone is the analogue of T4096; a random 1D CNN over 16kHz audio is the analogue of R64.
- **Coverage is THE scalar to report alongside FAD.** FAD compresses everything to one number and is dominated by mean-shift. Coverage adds the orthogonal "did you drop modes" signal. The expected diagnostic table for any new raw-audio gen we train: FAD ↓, Density (>1.0 ideally), Coverage (target >0.95 vs the val set under matched embeddings).
- **Predicted failure signature for 1-NFE adversarial audio.** ADD/LADD/DMD-style adversarial distillations push fakes toward high-likelihood pockets and lose tails — textbook "high Density / low Coverage". This is the prediction to verify when we benchmark our own one-step audio distillations: if Density rises but Coverage falls vs the teacher, the distillation is sharpening at the cost of diversity. The paper's StyleGAN ψ-truncation experiment is the exact image-domain analogue and shows this dial cleanly (Fig. 7).
- **The "random embedding" finding is directly applicable.** Table 1's spectrogram experiment shows ImageNet features can't evaluate audio meaningfully. For raw waveforms — even further from the ImageNet manifold than spectrograms — the same caveat applies *a fortiori* to any vision-pretrained encoder. Either use domain-specific audio models (CLAP, MERT) or follow the paper's random-CNN recipe with a fresh untrained 1D conv stack.
- **Coverage is per-dataset, not per-model.** This matters for training-time monitoring: the real-side k-NN structure is computed once for the eval set, and every checkpoint just runs an O(MN) membership query (or O(M log N) with a KD-tree). Cheap enough to log every few hundred steps during CPU eval.
- **Hyperparameter recipe carries over.** k = 5, M = N = 10,000 works regardless of dim or distribution (their analytic result). For short audio clips (~10s evaluation set), 10k samples is achievable; for very short clips (~0.1s) we can go larger.

## Relation to neighbouring metrics

- **vs Kynkäänniemi P&R**: D&C *is* P&R with the union → superposition swap on the real side, and the manifold-on-reals swap on the fake side. Strictly preferable: same computational profile, robust to outliers, has analytic guarantees, finer-grained for fidelity (density unbounded).
- **vs FID/FAD**: orthogonal. FAD is mean+covariance Gaussian fit, single scalar. D&C is non-parametric per-sample. Report both. FAD anchors against the global distribution; coverage flags mode-drop; density flags off-manifold sharpening.
- **vs Vendi Score (eigenvalue-based, reference-free)**: Vendi measures intrinsic diversity of *only* the fake set via similarity-matrix eigenvalues, no reals needed. Useful when you have no reference set, but doesn't tell you whether the diversity is the *right* diversity. D&C is the reference-anchored version; pair them.
- **vs Rarity Score (per-sample)**: Rarity scores individual generations by how far they are from real-set neighbours. Per-sample diagnostic. D&C are the summary statistics that pair naturally with rarity for ranking generations within a single eval run.
- **vs MIND / FDr^k / FD-as-loss** (other entries in this wiki's evaluation page): D&C are diagnosis-time tools, not training losses. FD-as-loss enters the optimisation; D&C are read-out only.

## Limitations and open questions

- **Curse of dimensionality on k-NN distances.** All four metrics (P&R and D&C) operate in feature space and inherit k-NN's degraded behaviour in high dim. Sticking to 64–512 dim feature spaces is sensible; the paper's R64 choice is not arbitrary.
- **What's the right audio feature space?** The paper sidesteps this by recommending random embeddings. For our project, the practical answer is probably: report D&C under both CLAP/MERT *and* a random 1D-conv embedding, treat divergences between the two as informative (CLAP says coverage is good but random-CNN says it isn't → the model is exploiting CLAP-perceptible features).
- **Sample-size scaling for short clips.** 10k clips of 10s each is 100k s ≈ 27 h of audio — fine. 10k clips of 0.1s is 1000s ≈ 17 min — also fine. No issue for this project's regime.
- **No analysis of temporal coherence.** D&C in a fixed feature space gives you no signal on whether generated audio is *temporally coherent* (no clicks, no phase discontinuities at chunk boundaries) — those failure modes need additional diagnostics.
