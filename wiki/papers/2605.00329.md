# AudioDEAR — energy-scoring + representation distillation for 1-step T2A

## TL;DR (project lens)

A one-step generator that **bypasses** trajectory/score distillation by replacing the diffusion head's regression loss with the **energy-distance scoring rule** (Szekely 2003). The "head" is a small MLP-with-adaLN that maps `(contextual_token h^i, gaussian_noise n)` directly to an audio latent `x^i` — no iterative denoising. Quality is closed to the multi-step teacher (IMPACT, 100-step) via **representation-level distillation**: MSE between final-layer transformer hidden states of student (trained with energy-distance) and a frozen multi-step teacher (trained with diffusion). Both share the same backbone architecture (IMPACT-Base, 24L, D=768, ~191 M).

**For us (raw sample space, CPU):** the work is *latent* (1024×64 mel → AudioLDM VAE → 256 tokens of dim 128, MAR-style iterative parallel decoding over those tokens). The result is **not** strictly 1-NFE end-to-end — it's 1-NFE *per token-generation iteration*, with `T=64` iterations of the transformer backbone in the reported config. The paper's "one-step" refers only to replacing the inner diffusion head's `n=100` denoising loop with a single energy-scoring forward pass. The headline 8.5× speedup is over IMPACT (the 100-step teacher), latency 2.61 s on V100 for 8 × 10 s clips. There is **no CPU benchmark**.

What's portable to raw sample-space audio:
1. **The energy-distance training objective itself** — a true scoring rule that turns a noise-to-target sampler into one forward pass, no ODE/SDE, no teacher needed during training. Distillation-free in the literal sense; we already track this family under `methods.md`.
2. **The auxiliary representation-distillation loss** — MSE between student and teacher final-layer hidden states, weight λ=1000 to balance against the energy term. Effective at narrowing the one-step / multi-step gap *without* needing the teacher's output predictions during sampling.
3. **Noise-as-input config for the sampling head** — they ablate "noise as condition vs noise as input" for the small head and find **noise-as-input + h^i-as-adaLN-condition** substantially better (FD 22.09 vs 28.32). Direct guidance for any conditional one-step head we build.

## The problem it addresses

IMPACT and similar MAR-style T2A systems do **two nested loops at inference**:
- `r` iterations of the MAR transformer (each progressively unmasks more latent positions),
- inside each iteration, `n` denoising / flow-matching steps to generate the unmasked positions.

Total cost: `r × n` forward passes. IMPACT uses `r=64, n=100` → 6400 head-forwards plus 64 backbone-forwards. Prior one-step approaches inside this framework (ConsistencyTTA, SoundCTM, AudioLCM, AudioTurbo) cut `n` but lose objective and subjective quality. The paper attacks the `n=1` setting with a stronger objective + auxiliary loss.

The reduction is to `r=64, n=1` — so backbone cost is unchanged, but the per-iteration head cost collapses by 100×. Latency: IMPACT 22.34 s → AudioDEAR 2.61 s for the same batch (8 × 10 s clips), on V100.

## Method

### Energy-distance training objective

For probability measures `P`, `Q` on `R^d`, the energy distance (Szekely 2003, recapped in the paper's Appendix A with full proof):

```
E(P, Q) = 2·E[‖X−Y‖] − E[‖X−X'‖] − E[‖Y−Y'‖],   X,X' ~ P,  Y,Y' ~ Q.
```

This is a strictly proper scoring rule: `E(P,Q) ≥ 0` with equality iff `P=Q`. The `E[‖Y−Y'‖]` term is constant in θ (data-only) so the actual loss with `m=2` model samples per training point is:

```
L_energy = ‖x_1 − y‖ + ‖x_2 − y‖ − ‖x_1 − x_2‖,
```

where `y` is a ground-truth latent at a masked position, and `x_1 = F_θ(h^i, n_1)`, `x_2 = F_θ(h^i, n_2)` are two model samples from independent noise vectors `n_1, n_2 ~ N(0, I)` conditioned on the contextual representation `h^i`. The third term is *maximised* — it penalises mode collapse. Section 5.4 ablates `m`: increasing m=2→3→4 slightly improves FD/FAD but degrades KL and CLAP. They settle on **m=2**.

### Energy-scoring head

A small MLP-residual-block stack `F_θ : (n, h) → x`, where `n ~ N(0, I)` is the **main input** to the residual blocks and `h^i` (the transformer's contextual representation for position `i`) is the **adaLN condition** (scale/shift/gate). They ablate the inverse (h as input, n as condition) and it's substantially worse (FD 28.32 vs 22.09).

### Auxiliary representation distillation

Student backbone is initialised from IMPACT and *kept trainable*. Teacher is the same IMPACT, frozen. Per training example, both forward the same masked latent + text embeddings; student head is the energy-scoring head, teacher head is the (frozen) diffusion head — but the distillation loss is at the *backbone* level:

```
L_distill = (1/L) · Σ_i ‖h_S^i − h_T^i‖²
L_total   = L_energy + λ · L_distill,    λ = 1000.
```

λ ablation (Table 2) is informative: freeze backbone → worst (FD 22.79); λ=0 → 22.09; λ=50 → 20.52; λ=1000 → **18.67**; λ=5000 → 19.88 (regression). The aux loss is doing real work — it propagates the multi-step teacher's strong text-conditioning into the one-step student's backbone, *without* the student ever seeing the teacher's outputs.

### Inference

For each MAR iteration `t ∈ {1, …, 64}`: forward the partially-unmasked latent sequence through the transformer to get `{h^i}`; for each newly-selected masked position, draw `n ~ N(0, I)`, compute `x^i = F_θ(h^i, n)` once, write back. After 64 iterations all positions are filled; VAE decoder produces the mel; (a vocoder, not discussed in the paper, produces the waveform).

**CFG is applied at representation-level**: `h^i = CFG · h^i_cond + (1 − CFG) · h^i_uncond`, scale 4.0. This is *only* viable because the head is one-step (no error accumulation through iterative denoising). The paper shows in Appendix F that representation-level CFG **diverges catastrophically** on multi-step diffusion teachers (FD 115.96 at CFG=4.0 for IMPACT), but is stable on AudioDEAR because no recursion.

## Architecture

- **Backbone**: IMPACT-Base transformer, 24 layers, hidden D=768, ~191 M params, attention over 256 audio-latent tokens + 78 text-embedding tokens (Flan-T5 + CLAP, concatenated).
- **Audio latent representation**: Mel(1024×64) → AudioLDM VAE → 256 latent tokens of dim 128 (patch size 4 over 16×8 spatial layout, flattened).
- **Energy-scoring head**: small ResBlock-MLP stack, noise as primary input, `h^i` injected via adaLN (scale/shift/gate per block). Tiny relative to the backbone.
- **Teacher**: IMPACT same backbone, frozen, diffusion head.

Training data: AudioCaps + WavCaps + AudioSet (~1700 h). Batch 2048, lr 1e-3, masking ratio sampled from [70, 100]% per step. Single Nvidia GPU class not stated; teacher training data is the same.

Same backbone (~193 M), same MAR setup, only the inner head/loss differs. At `steps=1`:

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|

This is a clean controlled comparison: every popular "few-step" loss collapses at n=1 under the MAR framework; energy-scoring is the only one that works. The toy Swiss-roll plots (Appendix H) corroborate — energy-scoring covers the spiral, MeanFlow under-covers, Shortcut/flow-matching collapse to the mean.

### CFG sensitivity

CFG=1→6 sweep (Table 3) on AudioDEAR: optimum at CFG=4.0; degrades gently. Compare IMPACT-with-rep-level-CFG (Appendix F), where representation-level CFG at scale 4.0 gives FD 115.96 (catastrophic). **The error-free 1-step setting is what makes representation-level CFG safe** — a recurring property of 1-NFE generators we should remember when designing CFG schedules.

## Latent vs raw-sample-space — honest accounting

This is unambiguously a **latent-space** system. Generation produces 256 latent tokens of dim 128, then AudioLDM's VAE decoder produces a mel spectrogram, then a vocoder (not described — presumably HiFi-GAN-class) produces the waveform. The paper's "one-step" claim sits inside the inner sampling head only; the backbone still runs 64 times for `T=64` MAR iterations, and there's a 2-stage decoder downstream. The 8.5× speedup over IMPACT is real but compared against a 64×100 = 6400-NFE baseline; the absolute latency is 2.61 s for 80 s of generated audio on a V100, i.e. ~30× realtime on GPU, **completely unbenchmarked on CPU**.

If we strip away the MAR/VAE machinery and ask "what would energy-scoring buy us in raw sample space?":

- The **energy-distance loss is dimension-agnostic** — it's just three pairwise L2 norms on whatever the model outputs. Swap `y, x_1, x_2` to be raw-waveform tensors of shape `(B, 16000)` for 1 s @ 16 kHz, and the loss is identical. Variance of the Monte-Carlo estimate scales with `d` (latent dim 128 vs waveform dim 16000), so we'd expect noisier gradients at high dimension — and the paper's `m=2` may need to grow (or batch size to grow) to compensate. Worth empirically probing on a toy waveform task.
- The **representation-distillation trick requires a multi-step waveform teacher** to distil from. Today the only public option in this repo is CRASH (raw drum diffusion, ~400 NFE). The recipe of (a) initialise student from teacher, (b) MSE on final-layer hidden states, (c) λ ≈ 1000 is straightforwardly portable — and it's a more honest distillation route than trajectory/score distillation because the student is free to learn its own output map; only its internal representations get aligned.
- The **noise-as-input + condition-as-adaLN** choice for the sampling head is a useful design data point for any one-step head we build that maps a noise vector to a waveform chunk conditioned on context.

What does **not** port:
- The MAR iterative-parallel-decoding framework presumes a tokenised representation. There is no obvious "audio token" in raw sample space below the codec. If we generate the entire waveform in a single forward pass (no autoregression), the inner-head one-stepness is the entire generation budget — much more attractive, but also much harder; the paper's argument that "reducing `r` hurts more than reducing `n`" is a warning here.
- All the absolute quality numbers are mediated by a frozen VAE + vocoder we are explicitly avoiding.

## Strengths

- **Cleanly motivated objective.** Energy distance is a proper scoring rule with a sample-based estimator that needs **no density**, no score, no ODE — fits one-step generation natively. The toy ablation (Sec H) is the most informative one-page evidence we've seen that this family is qualitatively different from MeanFlow/Shortcut at `n=1`.
- **The auxiliary-distillation trick is a small idea with a large effect.** λ ablation shows it accounts for the bulk of the quality gain. Generalisable to *any* one-step student / multi-step teacher pair that share a backbone — useful template.
- **Representation-level CFG only works because the head is one-step.** A nice example of one-step systems unlocking new inference-time tricks closed off to iterative samplers.

## Limitations

- **Latent-space only; relies on AudioLDM VAE + downstream vocoder.** All quality numbers inherit those codec floors.
- **r=64 MAR iterations.** The transformer backbone still runs 64×; the only saving is in the inner head. End-to-end latency 2.61 s / 80 s of audio on a V100 is fine but ~10–100× away from a CPU-realtime target.
- **No CPU benchmark.** Inherits the audio-T2A literature pattern.
- **MC estimate of energy-distance is noisy.** m=2 is the smallest possible setting; the paper notes the trade-off (m=4 improves FD by 0.5 but hurts CLAP). At high output dimension the estimator may need rethinking.
- **Distillation is symmetric to the backbone.** Student must match the teacher's hidden-state shape; this prevents using distillation to *shrink* the model, which is what we'd want for CPU inference.

## Open questions for this repo

1. **Does the energy-distance loss work in raw waveform space?** Smallest experiment: a 1D toy (mixture of damped sinusoids or short EnCodec-class waveforms), small conv-1D net `F_θ(n, c) → x_waveform`, train with `L_energy`. Compare with FM and MeanFlow at n=1. The Swiss-roll plot in Appendix H is roughly the right shape of evidence to look for.
2. **Does Monte-Carlo variance of the energy estimator blow up at audio dimensionality?** d=16000 vs d=128. Run the same toy at progressively higher d and watch gradient noise / required batch size.
3. **Can we combine energy-scoring with a *non-MAR* generator** — i.e. produce the entire short waveform in one head-forward pass conditioned on text? That would be 1-NFE end-to-end (vs the 64-NFE backbone here), and is what our project actually needs.
4. **The representation-distillation recipe (init student from teacher; MSE final-layer; λ ≈ 1000) is a template** worth keeping in the back pocket for *any* one-step waveform student / multi-step teacher pair.
5. **Compare to Drifting** (2602.04770) — Drifting uses a contrastive sample-level *V*-field in φ-space; energy-scoring uses three pairwise distances in output space and needs no φ. Both are scoring-rule / kernel-method one-step recipes; both ablate identically against MeanFlow/Shortcut on toy data; the comparison axis is "does the loss need a learned φ?" — energy-scoring says no, Drifting says yes. For raw audio with no pre-existing φ, energy-scoring is the lower-friction option.
