# ARC — Adversarial Relativistic-Contrastive post-training for fast text-to-audio

## TL;DR (project lens)

The **canonical audio adversarial post-training paper**. ARC turns a pretrained latent rectified-flow text-to-audio model (Stable Audio Open) into an 8-NFE generator using only an adversarial objective on real data — **no distillation, no teacher trajectory, no CFG at inference**. The adversarial objective has two parts: (1) a **relativistic** loss that compares each generated sample against its *paired* real sample sharing the same text prompt, and (2) a **contrastive** loss where the same discriminator must score correctly-prompted reals above shuffled-prompted reals — replacing the role CFG normally plays for prompt adherence. Sampling is **ping-pong** (denoise-then-renoise) at 8 steps. Released as **Stable Audio Open Small (497 M params total: 156 M autoencoder + 109 M T5 + 232 M DiT after shrinking the DiT from 1.06 B → 0.34 B)**, generating ~12 s of 44.1 kHz stereo audio in **~75 ms on H100** and **~7 s on a Vivo X200 Pro mobile CPU** (Octa-core Arm, Int8 weight + dynamic-Int8 activation quant via Arm KleidiAI / LiteRT / XNNPACK). No desktop-CPU number is reported. For this repo this is the strongest existing recipe for "post-train a pretrained flow model into a few-step on-device audio generator without distillation"; the *contrastive* discriminator is the trick that lets you drop CFG without losing prompt adherence, and ping-pong is what lets a small-step generator escape error accumulation.

## Why this matters for raw-audio CPU generation

- **Distillation-free.** ARC does not need a teacher network in memory during training, does not need cached trajectory pairs, does not need a TrigFlow conversion. The training loop is the original RF generator + a discriminator + real (audio, text) pairs. Cheap to set up on top of any pretrained RF/flow audio model — including a future raw-waveform one.
- **CFG-free at inference.** The contrastive loss `L_C` is what replaces CFG. CFG was the main thing preventing real one-/few-step latency reductions in audio (it doubles every NFE). At 8 NFE × no CFG, the effective number of forward passes is 8, not 16. On-device this halves VRAM/RAM peak (4.06 GB vs 4.20 GB pre-trained RF; one-CFG-pass model would be ~2× that for the same model size).
- **Real edge numbers on a 12 GB Arm phone.** ~7 s for ~12 s of stereo 44.1 kHz audio (RTF > 1.7) on the phone, with Int8 dynamic quant applied post-training (no quantization-aware training). H100 RTF is 156.42 (75 ms for 12 s); consumer 3090 is 187 ms (RTF ≈ 64). Mobile is the headline; **there is no CPU desktop benchmark in the paper.** For our project this means we know the algorithmic approach scales to mobile-class inference, but we don't have a direct laptop-CPU translation number.
- **Pretrained-model surgery is part of the recipe.** Before ARC starts, they shrink the DiT — **width 1536 → 1024, depth 24 → 16, remove "seconds-start" embedding** — 1.06 B → 0.34 B params. They also add QK-LayerNorm and `torch.compile` the base DiT. Without this surgery, the ARC numbers wouldn't land. This is a useful pattern: post-training is the place to remove architectural fat that the pretrained model didn't strictly need.
- **Ping-pong sampling, not Euler.** The few-step generator predicts clean `x_0` (not velocity) and is unrolled via alternating denoise-then-renoise rather than ODE integration. This pairs naturally with the adversarial objective (which gives well-defined gradients on x_0).
- **Diversity is explicitly measured and improves.** Their proposed CCDS (CLAP Conditional Diversity Score) shows ARC actively *increases* conditional diversity vs the 8-step base RF and vs Presto (the distillation baseline) — relevant for any creative-tool use case.

## Problem and positioning

Pretrained text-to-audio flow models (Stable Audio Open, MusicGen-style, AudioLDM) need 50–250 NFE for good quality and depend on CFG, doubling per-step cost. Two dominant acceleration paths exist:

1. **Distillation** — Presto (Novack et al. 2024, [18]) is the SOTA audio example: it minimises a reverse KL against a teacher + a GAN loss. Quality is high, but distillation has documented cost (storing/running teacher and student concurrently; trajectory storage; CFG must be baked in).
2. **Adversarial post-training without distillation** — UFOGen [24], APT [25] in the image domain. Reported gains have historically been *limited* (UFOGen) or required *distillation-based initialisation* (APT). The community's working assumption was that adversarial-only acceleration just doesn't match distillation.

ARC's claim is that this assumption is wrong if you (a) use a **relativistic** rather than absolute adversarial loss and (b) add a **contrastive** discriminator term for prompt adherence. The framing connects neatly to language-model post-training as preference learning: the relativistic loss is exactly the Bradley-Terry pairwise reward log-likelihood with the discriminator as reward model and `(x_real, x_gen)` as the preferred/dispreferred pair given prompt c (§2.6 in the paper).

## Method

### Pretraining setup (inherited from SAO)

- **Forward process** (rectified flow, [6, 20]): `x_t = (1−t) x_0 + t ε`, with `ε ∼ N(0, I)` and `t ∈ [0, 1]`.
- **Velocity** `v_θ(x_t, t, c) = ε − x_0` learned by FM loss `‖v − v_θ‖²`.
- **Latent space**: stereo waveform → 64-channel 21.5 Hz latent via a 156 M-param pretrained autoencoder. Prompt encoded by a 109 M T5.
- **DiT generator** `G_φ`, parameterised as `G_φ(x_t, c, t) = x_t − t · v_φ(x_t, t, c)` (predicts clean `x_0`). 0.34 B params after surgery.

### The relativistic loss `L_R` (the R in ARC)

For each real (`x_0`, `c`) pair, draw a fresh `t ∼ p_gen(t)` (shifted logit-normal in log-SNR [-6, 2]), corrupt `x_0` to `x_t`, generate `x̂_0 = G_φ(x_t, t, c)`. Pick a **discriminator noise level** `s ∼ p_disc(s)` (a different, shifted-logit-normal distribution that puts more weight in mid-to-high SNR). Add noise at level `s` to both real and generated samples, producing `x_s` and `x̂_s`. The discriminator `D_ψ` evaluates both:

```
Δ_real(x_0, s, c) = D_ψ( q(x_s | x_0), s, c )
Δ_gen (x_0, t, s, c) = D_ψ( q(x̂_s | x̂_0 = G_φ(x_t, t, c)), s, c )

L_R(φ, ψ) = E[ f( Δ_gen − Δ_real ) ],  f(x) = −log(1 + e^{−x})
```

with `min_φ max_ψ` over `L_R`. Two non-trivial choices:

- **Disentangled generator/discriminator noise levels.** `p_gen` and `p_disc` are *different* distributions, sampled independently per pair (cf. Presto [18]). Past work used the same `s = t`; decoupling improves stability and lets `p_disc` be focused where the discriminator has high signal.
- **Pairing.** The relativistic loss is computed on `(x_real, x_gen)` *pairs that share the same prompt c*. Past relativistic GAN work [30] doesn't specify pair structure; ARC's pairing-by-prompt provides a stronger gradient signal than random pairing.

Concretely: the generator wants every generated sample to be "more real than its paired real", and the discriminator wants every real to be "more real than its paired generated". This is the Bradley-Terry pairwise objective.

### The contrastive loss `L_C` (the C in ARC)

`L_R` alone fixes realism but *not* prompt adherence. ARC reuses the same discriminator (which is natively text-conditional via the inherited prompt-embedding layers) for a second relativistic term, now between **real (audio, correct prompt)** and **real (audio, shuffled prompt)** pairs:

```
L_C(ψ) = E[ f( Δ_real(x_0, s, P[c]) − Δ_real(x_0, s, c) ) ]
```

where `P[·]` is a random intra-batch permutation of prompts. Only `ψ` is updated by `L_C` (no generator term). The discriminator learns to push down audio-text mismatches; via `L_R` this propagates into the generator's prompt adherence. **This is the explicit replacement for CFG.** Loss-wise it's a contrastive [34] / noise-contrastive [34] estimator over correct vs incorrect audio-text pairs.

A nice side-effect: because `L_C` adds a semantic-alignment job to `D_ψ`, the discriminator can no longer "win" by focusing on high-frequency artifacts — it must use semantic features. The paper explicitly notes this pushes the relativistic loss toward improving prompt adherence rather than over-fitting easy frequency cues. CFG's known drawbacks (low diversity, over-saturation [23]) are also avoided.

### Combined ARC objective

```
min_φ max_ψ  L_ARC(φ, ψ) = L_R(φ, ψ) + λ · L_C(ψ),   λ = 1
```

Trained with alternating updates (one step on `G_φ` minimising `L_R`; one step on `D_ψ` maximising `L_R + L_C`).

### Discriminator architecture

- **Initialised from the pretrained RF DiT** (the same one being post-trained). Specifically: the input embedding layers + **75 % of the DiT blocks** of the pretrained RF.
- A **randomly-initialised lightweight discriminator head** on top: 4 blocks of 1-D convolutions interleaved with GroupNorm + SiLU.
- **~274 M params total.**

This re-uses RF features for the discriminator backbone, which is becoming a standard adversarial-post-training pattern (image GAN-distillation: SD3-Turbo, SiD^a). For raw audio the same trick should port: 1-D backbone instead of DiT, last few blocks fine-tuned, conv head on top.

### Ping-pong sampling

Because `G_φ` predicts `x_0` from any noise level (not a velocity to integrate), they avoid Euler/ODE solvers and use **ping-pong**:

```
x̂_0 = G_φ(x_τ_i, τ_i, c)             # denoise to clean estimate
x_{τ_{i−1}} = (1 − τ_{i−1}) x̂_0 + τ_{i−1} ε,  ε ∼ N(0, I)   # re-noise to a smaller τ
```

8 steps over a fixed `τ_0 > τ_1 > … > τ_7 = 0` schedule. Re-injected noise gives the sampler a chance to correct earlier mistakes — closer in spirit to consistency-model multi-step sampling than to Euler. **No CFG → no double pass → half the VRAM** vs CFG-enabled 8-step sampling (4.06 GB vs ~4.20 GB peak on H100; critical for the mobile target).

### Training details

- Data: Stable Audio Open data minus long-form FMA music — 6.33 kh / 472 k audios from Freesound under CC0/CC-BY/CC-Sampling+ (focus: sound effects and loops; up to 11.89 s, with timing control).
- Pretrained RF for **670 k iters**; ARC post-training for **100 k iters**, batch size 256 across 8 H100s, AdamW, LR `5×10^{−7}` for both `G_φ` and `D_ψ`. Discriminator weights and learning rate matter — RF-initialised D + matched LR was the recipe that worked.

## Results

Test set: AudioCaps subset (979 audios, 881 generations). Headline: **8-step ARC matches or beats 100-step SAO on FD/CCDS, is 100× faster on RTF, has the best diversity MOS, and has the lowest VRAM. Presto is sharper-quality (4.0 MOS) but its CCDS is 2.7 / 0.26 — a documented diversity collapse, the classic CFG-baked-in failure mode.**

### Ablations (Table 1)

- **`L_R` alone** (no `L_C`): CLAP collapses to 0.20 — the generator becomes unconditional, the realism loss dominates and the model just makes plausible audio ignoring the prompt. (CCDS goes up to 0.57 because unconditional generation is "diverse" in the wrong sense.) This isolates `L_C` as the prompt-adherence mechanism.
- **`L_LS + L_C`** (replace relativistic with standard least-squares GAN): FD 98 vs 84 — relativistic > absolute even with `L_C` present.
- **NFE sweep**: 8 steps is the sweet spot. 1-step is significantly worse (FD 100 vs 84); 4-step is close (FD 90). Aligns with [51] (Consistency Models Made Easy) on accelerated models needing more steps than larger counterparts.
- The qualitative MOS-quality gap vs the 50-step pre-trained RF is small but non-zero (3.5 vs 3.7), traded for a 10× speedup, +0.07 CCDS, +1.3 Div MOS, and CFG-free inference.

### Edge-device numbers (§3.7)

- **Vivo X200 Pro**, Octa-core Arm CPU (1× Cortex-X925 + 3× Cortex-X4 + 4× Cortex-A720, 12 GB RAM).
- Arm **KleidiAI** ops via LiteRT runtime + XNNPACK.
- **Dynamic Int8 quantization** applied **post-training, selectively** to certain layers — weights quantized ahead of time, activations dynamically at runtime. **No quantization-aware training**.
- Inference time: **15.3 s (F32 original) → 6.6 s** for 12 s audio.
- Peak runtime RAM: **6.5 GB → 3.6 GB**.
- H100: 75 ms. Consumer 3090: 187 ms. **No CPU desktop benchmark.**

### Diversity metric (CCDS)

Proposed in this paper: average CLAP cosine distance between same-prompt generations within a batch, averaged across all batches. Designed to catch *conditional* diversity collapse (CFG-style mode pinning) which FD doesn't surface. **Listening test confirms CCDS ranking correlates with human "diversity" MOS** (paper's qualitative claim). Useful for any future on-device audio gen evaluation in this repo.

## Translation to raw audio (sample-space)

ARC is already audio-domain — but it operates on a **64-ch 21.5 Hz latent**, not raw waveform. What carries directly to a hypothetical raw-waveform flow model:

- **Loss form is representation-agnostic.** `L_R + L_C` only requires a generator that maps `x_t → x̂_0` and a discriminator that ingests noised audio + text + noise-level. A 1-D conv backbone for `G_φ` and a 1-D conv discriminator (or a Mel-domain discriminator like HiFi-GAN's multi-scale) would replace the DiT pair.
- **Discriminator features must be semantic.** Re-using a pretrained generator backbone as the discriminator is the trick. For raw waveforms we don't yet have a strong pretrained semantic backbone — candidates: a frozen audio-φ (PaSST, openl3, CLAP-text-aligned audio encoder) feeding the head. Risk: without prompt-aware features, `L_C` is weaker.
- **Ping-pong sampling on raw waveforms.** Same mechanic; just operates in sample space. Compute per step is `O(seq_len)` for a 1-D conv U-Net; at 16 kHz × 1 s = 16k samples × 8 steps, the prefactor depends entirely on the chosen 1-D backbone.
- **CFG-free is a free win.** Audio raw-waveform CFG (when used at all, e.g. CRASH) is rare; many raw-audio flow papers haven't even considered CFG yet. Adopting `L_C` from the start avoids the need to ever bake it in.
- **Caveat: ARC is a *post-training* method.** Its claims rest on the existence of a strong pretrained RF model. For a from-scratch raw-waveform recipe we'd either (a) pretrain RF first then ARC-post-train, doubling the training pipeline, or (b) integrate `L_R + L_C` into joint training — which the paper doesn't try and which has known instability risks in joint GAN/diffusion training.
- **Stereo / sample rate scaling.** ARC handles stereo 44.1 kHz at 21.5 Hz latent rate. At raw waveform we'd be operating at 44.1 kHz × 2 ch — 2000× higher temporal resolution per latent. The DiT is the wrong backbone; a 1-D conv U-Net or hybrid is needed. ARC says nothing about that architectural choice.

## Strengths

- First successful **distillation-free** acceleration of a strong T2A model. Solves a real practical problem (training pipeline complexity).
- Drops CFG cleanly via a principled mechanism (the contrastive loss), with documented diversity gain rather than loss.
- The relativistic-on-prompt-paired-data formulation is elegant: stronger gradients than random pairing, and a literal Bradley-Terry preference interpretation. Connects to RLHF / DPO conceptually.
- Excellent measurement discipline: H100 RTF + 3090 RTF + mobile latency + VRAM all reported. CCDS proposed and validated against MOS.
- Strong ablations: `L_R` alone, `L_LS + L_C`, NFE sweep, model surgery. Each component shown to matter.

## Limitations / open questions

- **No desktop-CPU number.** Mobile (Vivo X200 Pro, Arm) and H100/3090 GPU only. We don't know what an Intel/AMD laptop core would do.
- **Quality MOS still trails Presto** (3.5 vs 4.0) and the 50-step pre-trained RF (3.5 vs 3.7) — ARC trades sharpness for diversity. For some creative use cases this is a feature; for others it's a regression.
- **8 steps, not 1.** 1-step ARC degrades significantly (FD 100 vs 84). The "few-step" lower bound is genuinely few, not one.
- **No teacher-free joint-training experiment.** Everything ARC does is post-training a strong RF. The recipe might not bootstrap from scratch.
- **No gradient penalty / R1 reported.** The paper does not mention an R1 (Mescheder) or zero-centered gradient penalty — somewhat surprising for a relativistic GAN at 100 k iters; either they didn't need one (relativistic + contrastive may be self-stabilising) or didn't report. Worth checking the code release.
- **Discriminator memory cost.** D is 274 M params, comparable to G (340 M). Doubles training memory.
- **Mobile latency is still > real-time** (~7 s for 12 s — that's RTF ≈ 1.7, but with substantial setup/warmup). Truly real-time on-device generation isn't quite there yet.
- **CCDS uses CLAP cosine** — inherits CLAP's biases; doesn't capture low-level timbral diversity.

## Things to test in this repo

1. **Port `L_R + L_C` to a 1-D conv U-Net raw-waveform RF teacher.** Use a small CRASH-style backbone + the same ping-pong 8-step inference. Measure CPU desktop latency vs an Euler-50 baseline.
2. **`L_C`-only post-training of an existing CFG-using audio flow model.** Just to test whether the contrastive trick suffices to *remove CFG from an existing model* without ARC's full adversarial machinery — separating "no CFG at inference" from "few-step".
3. **Replace the DiT discriminator with an audio-φ backbone** (PaSST features → small conv head). Tests whether ARC needs the matched-backbone trick or whether any semantic feature stack works.
4. **Run CCDS on our own raw-audio generators** (CRASH, future ARC port) and see whether the metric ranks them sensibly.
5. **Try ARC-style post-training of a smaller 1-D model end-to-end on CPU** to see whether the recipe collapses when the discriminator is much weaker than the H100/DiT-based one used here.
