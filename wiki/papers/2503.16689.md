# WaveFM (NAACL 2025)

## TL;DR (project lens)

The **FM+CM half of the FM+GAN/FM+CM dichotomy** in audio post-training-to-1-step recipes — the closest published vocoder analog to Flow2GAN's FM+GAN. Mel-conditioned **reparameterized flow matching** vocoder (predicts `x₁` directly, not the velocity / "random derivative"; same predict-`x₁` trick Flow2GAN later identifies as the audio-FM fundamental), trained on a **mel-conditioned diagonal-Gaussian prior** (variance derived from `√(Σ mel-bands)/√(mel-bands·32768)`, clamped at 10⁻³) that aligns the source distribution to the per-utterance energy envelope before the flow even starts. Adds a refined multi-resolution **STFT loss** that replaces spectral convergence with an `atan2`-wrapped **phase-angle loss** plus gradient + Laplacian operators on the magnitude — explicitly attacks phase reconstruction, the place where Flow2GAN's spectral-energy-inverse weighting *doesn't*. Then **tailored consistency distillation** for **1-NFE inference**: targets are clean waveforms at `t=1`, student predicts `x₁` at smaller `t`, truncated-Gaussian `t ~ Ñ(0, 0.33²)` on `[0, 0.99]`, EMA teacher, distillation runs only 25 k steps after the 1 M-step FM pretrain. Architecture: **19.5 M-parameter asymmetric HiFi-GAN-style 1-D U-Net** with multi-receptive-field ResBlocks, **snake-beta activations from BigVGAN**, dilated convs, FiLM-injected sinusoidal-time embeddings — operates on waveform at sample rate, the **opposite architectural choice from Vocos / RFWave / Flow2GAN's STFT-domain ConvNeXt**. The **headline CPU number is `xRT 0.64` at 1-NFE on Intel Xeon Platinum 8457C, batch 16, 1 s @ 24 kHz, 19.5 M params** (reported in Flow2GAN's Tab. 7 re-benchmark, not in the WaveFM paper itself — WaveFM's own RTF table is 4090 only) — **sub-real-time on CPU**, sitting alongside RFWave (0.37) and PeriodWave-Turbo (0.12) as the sample-rate-waveform vocoder family that *all loses to STFT-domain Flow2GAN (4.85)* on CPU. WaveFM's CPU loss is **not the consistency distillation** (1-NFE is the cheapest possible inference) but the **waveform-domain backbone** — the same axis Flow2GAN's CPU benchmark identified as dominant.

## Why this paper matters for the project

1. **Direct 1-NFE waveform-output vocoder benchmark.** One of three audio vocoders in the wiki with a published 1-NFE number (HiFi-GAN, Flow2GAN, WaveFM). Operates in raw sample space — no STFT-domain compute.
2. **FM+CM as a separate recipe from FM+GAN.** Same FM pretrain → 1-step finetune skeleton as Flow2GAN, but the finishing move is **consistency distillation against an EMA teacher**, not adversarial post-training. The two papers form the FM+post-training axis: distillation (this) vs adversarial (Flow2GAN, PeriodWave-Turbo).
3. **Reparameterized FM is a deliberate design choice that ports.** WaveFM derives the predict-`x₁` reparameterization from the perspective that the original `dx_t/dt` regression is a "random derivative" (`E[dx_t/dt|x_t=x]`) — derivatives of stochastic processes are problematic, and predicting `x₁` directly lets you stack auxiliary losses (STFT-loss, mel-L1) that don't make sense on a velocity prediction. Flow2GAN independently arrives at the same conclusion. **Strong cross-paper evidence that x₁-prediction is the right audio-FM parameterisation**, and the ablation here (Tab. 4: no-reparameterization drops SMOS 4.11 → 3.88) makes that quantitative.
4. **Mel-conditioned prior is the most refined audio-prior recipe in the wiki.** Lee 2021 (PriorGrad) introduced data-dependent Gaussian priors with clamping `σ ∈ [0.1, 1]` to keep training stable. WaveFM's reparameterized FM **only samples** the prior (Theorem 1 only requires marginals, not analytical PDFs), so it can use a much tighter clamp — minimum σ ~10⁻³. Ablation (Tab. 4: w/o conditioned prior SMOS 4.11 → 3.79) is the single largest non-reparameterization drop. **The mel-conditioned prior is load-bearing for 1-step quality**, not just decoration.
5. **Consistency distillation at vocoder scale, on waveform domain, with publicly available code.** Github `luotianze666/WaveFM` (Apache-2.0 per page 1 footnote). One of very few published audio CM recipes with everything — pretrain code, distill code, mel-prior implementation, multi-resolution STFT-with-phase loss, snake-beta activations — in one repo. **Sets the architectural and training-loop baseline that any future FM+CM audio experiment should diff against**, the same way Flow2GAN sets the FM+GAN baseline.
6. **Conditional, not unconditional — the same caveat as Flow2GAN.** Mel conditioning pins down the marginal and provides energy structure to the prior. Whether the FM+CM recipe survives without mel conditioning is the open question for the project's unconditional target — *same* open question as Flow2GAN.

## Problem framing

Mel-conditioned vocoders split:

- **GAN-only** (HiFi-GAN, BigVGAN, Vocos) — 1-NFE at inference, unstable training, audio quality plateau without scale.
- **Diffusion / Flow-Matching** (DiffWave, PriorGrad, FreGrad, FastDiff, WaveGrad, RFWave) — stable training, strong quality, but multi-step inference (typically 6–50+ NFE).

WaveFM's framing matches Flow2GAN's: get diffusion-grade quality at GAN-grade inference cost. WaveFM picks **consistency distillation** as the 1-step recipe; Flow2GAN picks **adversarial post-training**. Both papers report the recipes work, with quality near top of class.

## Method

### Reparameterized flow matching (the predict-x₁ trick, derived from "random derivatives are bad")

Standard FM: linear interpolant `x_t = t·x₁ + (1−t)·x₀`, learn `v(x_t, t) = E[dx_t/dt | x_t = x] = E[x₁ − x₀ | x_t]`. WaveFM frames the problem: this is a **random-derivative estimation problem** — and **derivatives of stochastic processes are problematic for periodic-inductive-bias networks** (snake activations, MRF ResBlocks) because the noise in the random derivative `dx_t/dt = x₁ − x₀ + noise` disrupts periodic patterns. Furthermore, you cannot stack auxiliary losses (mel-L1, STFT loss) on a velocity prediction in a meaningful way.

**Reparameterize to predict `x₁` directly.** Algebra (Eq. 16–17):

```
x₁ − x₀ = (x₁ − x_t)/(1−t),  so  v(x_t, t) = (E[x₁|x_t] − x_t)/(1−t)
```

Train the network as `v'(x_t, t)` predicting `x₁` (Eq. 17):

```
min_v'  E ‖ x₁ − v'(x_t, t) ‖² / (1−t)²,   t ∈ [0,1).
```

Then auxiliary losses on `v'` are auxiliary losses on `x̂₁` — interpretable. To prevent the `1/(1−t)²` from blowing up near `t=1`, the coefficient is clamped to `1/min(0.1, 1−t)` (i.e. capped at 10 for `t ∈ [0.9, 1)`). Algorithm 1 puts the full training loss together:

```
L = ‖x₁ − v₁‖² / min(0.1, 1−t)
  + λ₀ · STFTLoss(x₁, v₁)
  + (λ₁/M) · ‖mel(x₁) − mel(v₁)‖₁,    λ₀ = λ₁ = 0.02.
```

Identical motivation to Flow2GAN's predict-`x₁`-not-`v` ablation (PESQ jump 2.35 → 2.81). Independent rederivation; WaveFM names the underlying reason ("random derivative is hard"), Flow2GAN names the symptom ("silent regions force `v ≈ −x₀`"). **Two papers, same conclusion: predict `x₁` on audio FM.**

### Mel-conditioned prior `N(0, Σ_mel)`

Standard FM picks `x₀ ~ N(0, I)`. WaveFM picks `x₀ ~ N(0, Σ_mel)` where `Σ_mel` is a *diagonal* matrix per sample. Construction (paper §3.1):

1. Take the log-mel spectrogram of the conditioning signal.
2. Reshape: `σ_per_frame = √(Σ over mel-bands) / √(mel-bands × 32768)`. (32 768 = 2¹⁵, scaling 16-bit-PCM range to `[0, 1]`.)
3. Linearly interpolate from frame-rate (one σ per mel-frame) to sample-rate.
4. Clamp at min 10⁻³.

The mel-spectrogram captures audio energy; the prior variance tracks audio energy point-by-point in time. In near-silent regions, `σ` is ~10⁻³ — the prior is **already extremely close to the data**, transportation cost in the FM flow is small. Lee 2021 (PriorGrad) had to clamp `σ ∈ [0.1, 1]` to stabilise DDPM training; **reparameterized FM only requires sampling from the prior**, not knowing its analytical PDF (Theorem 1, App. A), so WaveFM's clamp can be much looser → much tighter alignment of prior to data.

Ablation (Tab. 4 LibriTTS-Test): w/o conditioned prior SMOS drops 4.11 → 3.79 (1-NFE, eight comparable models in the table) — **the second-largest ablation drop**, only behind reparameterization itself. The reparameterization + mel-prior pair are **co-load-bearing** for 1-step quality.

### Multi-resolution STFT loss with phase-angle + Laplacian (the audio-perceptual loss)

Standard multi-resolution STFT loss (Parallel WaveGAN; FFT/hop/win at (1024, 120, 600), (2048, 240, 1200), (512, 50, 240)):

```
L_sc  = ‖|STFT(x)| − |STFT(x̂)|‖_F / ‖|STFT(x)|‖_F          (spectral convergence)
L_mag = (1/N) ‖log|STFT(x)| − log|STFT(x̂)|‖₁                 (log magnitude)
```

WaveFM redesigns it for the predict-`x₁` regime:

1. **Adjust FFT/hop/win** to `(1024, 128, 512)`, `(2048, 256, 1024)`, `(512, 64, 256)` — more uniform window sizes.

2. **Replace `L_sc` with a phase-angle loss.** Since the network now outputs an actual waveform (`x̂₁`), the STFT of that waveform has a phase that can be compared. Phase difference is `ΔP = Phase(STFT(x)) − Phase(STFT(x̂))`, wrapped to `(−π, π]` via `atan2`:

   ```
   L_pha = (1/N) ‖atan2(sin(ΔP), cos(ΔP))‖₁                  (Eq. 22–23)
   ```
   
   Skip cells where the squared magnitude is < 10⁻⁶ (phase is uninformative).

3. **Add temporal-gradient, frequency-gradient, and Laplacian filters** on the *magnitude* spectrogram, with MSE loss against the corresponding clean-audio filtered magnitudes, weighted (4, 4, 2):

   ```
   T-grad:    [-1  1; -2  2; -1  1]/4    (vertical kernel, time direction)
   F-grad:    [-1 -2 -1; 1 2 1]/4         (horizontal kernel, frequency direction)
   Laplacian: [-1 -1 -1; -1 8 -1; -1 -1 -1]/8
   ```

   These act as edge / structure detectors on the spectrogram — the network is asked to match not only the magnitude itself but its gradients and second-derivatives, which encode the *regularities* of the mel pattern.

Ablation (Tab. 4): with-original-STFT-loss (i.e. swap WaveFM's redesigned loss back to Parallel-WaveGAN's `L_sc + L_mag`) SMOS drops 4.11 → 4.05. **Modest but real win**; Fig. 1 shows the redesigned loss produces sharper spectrograms at 1-step inference. Also: w/o auxiliary losses entirely SMOS 4.11 → 3.97 — auxiliary losses are load-bearing on reparameterized FM (consistent with Flow2GAN's spectral-energy-inverse weighting being load-bearing there).

### Tailored consistency distillation for 1-NFE inference

Standard CM (Song & Dhariwal 2023): student `f_θ(x_t, t)` predicts `x_ε` (the endpoint near `t=ε` on the PF-ODE) for any `(x_t, t)` along the trajectory, trained against an EMA teacher's prediction on a slightly-noised version of the input. CM is for **SDEs/score-matching**, not FM.

WaveFM's tailored consistency distillation (Algorithm 2):

- **Initialize student** from the pretrained reparameterized FM model.
- **Targets are clean waveforms `x₁` at `t = 1`**, not the standard CM "consistency function evaluated at `t = ε`". *This is a significant deviation* — standard CM parameterises a continuously differentiable consistency function with `c_skip · x + c_out · F_θ`; WaveFM directly sets the target to be the clean audio. Their argument (page 5): "the quality of generated audio would severely degrade if we parameterized our model conventionally" — the standard CM parameterisation is incompatible with their reparameterization. So they just set `target := x₁` for `t + Δt > 0.99`, and `target := v_θ−(x_{t+Δt}, t+Δt, m)` for the recursive step (the teacher's prediction at the next time-step).
- **Sample `t ~ Ñ(0, 0.33²)` truncated to `[0, 0.99]`** rather than uniform — concentrates training near `t = 0` where the 1-step generation must work well from pure noise.
- **EMA teacher with decay μ = 0.999**, distance Δt = 0.01.
- **L₂ distance** between student and target, plus the four auxiliary losses (`‖x₁ − v₁‖²`, STFT loss, mel-L1 — same recipe as pretrain).
- Only **25 000 distillation steps** (initial LR 2×10⁻⁵), versus 1 M FM pretrain steps. **~2 hours on a single 4090.** Distillation is a cheap finishing move.

The mel-conditioned dynamic prior is compatible with this distillation: the student only needs to sample from `prior(m)`, not know its PDF.

## Architecture (the contrast with STFT-domain ConvNeXt)

**Asymmetric HiFi-GAN-style 1-D U-Net with multi-receptive-field ResBlocks at sample rate.** 19.5 M total. **No STFT-domain compute anywhere — this is a waveform-output, sample-rate-input architecture from start to finish.** Different family from Vocos / RFWave / Flow2GAN.

Layout (Fig. 2):

- **Input**: mel-spectrogram (frame-rate).
- **Downsampling chain** (left side of U-Net) — five strided 1-D convs taking the mel from frame-rate up to closer-to-sample-rate. ResBlock matrices are **4×1** here (only 4 ResLayers, kernel-3) — minimal capacity, just a controller for the upsampling side. ResLayer kernels and dilations: kernel-list `[3]`, dilation-list `[1, 3, 5]`-style.
- **Upsampling chain** (right side) — `ConvTranspose1d` strides `8, 8, 2, 2` (cumulative 256× = 24000-Hz sample rate / 93.75-frame rate, matching hop 256). Channel ladder `[512, 256, 128, 64, 32]` — identical strides and ladder to HiFi-GAN V1. Per-level `ResLayer([3, 7, 11], [1, 3, 5])` is a 3-kernel × 3-dilation MRF matrix of ResBlocks (the **multi-receptive-field block from HiFi-GAN**).
- **Asymmetric**: most capacity on the **upsampling side** (where the actual audio is being synthesized from the mel controller). Downsampling side is just an encoder of the mel conditioning.
- **Time embedding**: sinusoidal positional encoding of `100·t` (matching diffusion-scale magnitudes), 128-dim, then two Linear-SiLU expansions to 512-dim, then **added to hidden layers in the downsampling path** at each resolution. FiLM-style additive, single t embedding shared across all blocks at each resolution.
- **Activations**: **snake-beta from BigVGAN**: `snake(x) = x + (1/(eᵝ + ε))·sin²(eᵅ·x)`, channel-wise learnable `α, β`. Periodic inductive bias for audio. Ablation (Tab. 4): w/o snake activation SMOS drops 4.11 → 4.03 — small but consistent win.
- **Dilated convs** at larger kernel sizes on the upsampling side specifically (the paper emphasises this is where structural reconstruction happens).

**The waveform-domain choice is the load-bearing CPU difference vs Flow2GAN.** Sequence length per forward pass is the *sample-rate* sequence (24 000 per second of audio), not the *hop-rate* sequence (94 per second @ hop 256). At 19.5 M params this is the smallest of the FM-vocoder family by half, but it operates on sequences ~256× longer than Flow2GAN's per-branch sequences — and that's why the per-step CPU forward pass costs more even though the parameter count is smaller. **This is the same lesson Flow2GAN's CPU benchmark already taught**: operating sequence length dominates CPU cost, not param count or even NFE.

## Training (LibriTTS, ~1 000 hours, single 4090)

- **Data**: LibriTTS `train-clean-100 + train-clean-360 + train-other-500` — 350 k speech clips @ 24 kHz.
- **Mel features**: 100 bands, frequency range [0, 12 kHz], FFT 1024 / window 1024 / hop 256.
- **Pretrain**: 1 M steps, AdamW (β₁=0.9, β₂=0.99), initial LR 7.5×10⁻⁵, cosine annealing to 5×10⁻⁶, weight decay 5×10⁻⁴, batch 16. **~2 days on a single 4090.**
- **Distill**: 25 k steps, initial LR 2×10⁻⁵, weight decay 1×10⁻², β₁=0.8 / β₂=0.95, batch 16. **~2 hours on a single 4090.**
- **OOD evaluation**: MUSDB18-HQ music (vocal tracks only, since automatic evaluators are speech-tuned). Generalisation outside speech is reported as a deliberate claim.

## Inference cost (the headline this section)

WaveFM's own paper reports **only 4090 RTF, not CPU**. CPU numbers come from Flow2GAN's re-benchmark (Tab. 7 of `2512.23278`):

| Setting | NFE | Params | CPU xRT (Xeon 8457C, batch 16, 1 s, 24 kHz) | GPU xRT (H100) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| WaveFM-1 step | 1 | 19.5 M | **0.64** | 226.3 |
| HiFi-GAN V1 (reference, ~14 M @ 22 kHz, i7 laptop) | 1 | 13.9 M | 1.43 | 167.9 |
| Vocos (STFT-domain) | 1 | 13.5 M | **387.6** | 6440.8 |
| Flow2GAN-1 step (STFT-domain) | 1 | 78.9 M | **4.85** | 851.7 |

**WaveFM is sub-real-time on CPU at 1-NFE.** Same family as RFWave (0.37) and PeriodWave-Turbo (0.12) — **all the sample-rate-waveform vocoders lose to the STFT-domain vocoders on CPU by 1–3 orders of magnitude**, regardless of NFE or training recipe. WaveFM at 19.5 M params still loses to Flow2GAN at 78.9 M because Flow2GAN's per-step sequence length is ~256× shorter.

**The conclusion for the project: 1-NFE consistency distillation works as a recipe, but it doesn't overcome the architectural CPU bottleneck of sample-rate waveform convolutions.** If we want a CPU-fast unconditional generator, FM+CM in the WaveFM style still has to pair with a hop-rate / STFT-domain or otherwise short-sequence backbone — not a HiFi-GAN-style sample-rate U-Net.

GPU RTF (paper's own Tab. 3 on 4090): WaveFM-1 303× RT, WaveFM-6 50.2× RT, HiFi-GAN V1 325× RT, BigVGAN-base 90.3× RT, DiffWave-6 16.7× RT. **On GPU the consistency-distilled WaveFM is essentially HiFi-GAN-class speed.** The CPU gap is the laptop-vs-server hardware effect, not a fundamental property of the recipe.

## Comparison with the closest neighbours

### vs Flow2GAN (`2512.23278`) — FM+CM vs FM+GAN, waveform-domain vs STFT-domain

| Axis | WaveFM | Flow2GAN |
|---|---|---|
| Domain | Mel-conditioned vocoder | Mel- or Encodec-conditioned vocoder |
| Operating space | Raw waveform at sample rate (1-D U-Net) | STFT coefficients at hop rate (multi-branch ConvNeXt) |
| Stage 1 (pretrain) | Reparameterized FM (predict `x₁`), 1 M steps | Reparameterized FM (predict `x₁`) + spectral-energy-inverse weighting, ~880 k steps |
| Stage 2 (1-step recipe) | Consistency distillation against EMA teacher, 25 k steps | GAN finetune (MPD + MRD + multi-scale mel-L1), 11–110 k iters |
| Auxiliary losses | Multi-resolution STFT (with phase-angle + Laplacian) + mel-L1 | Multi-scale mel-L1 reconstruction (no raw-STFT phase loss — leaks electric noise per PeriodWave-Turbo) |
| Phase handling | Explicit `atan2`-wrapped phase-angle loss | Phase is implicit via iSTFT readout (network outputs complex STFT) |
| Params | 19.5 M | 78.9 M |
| 1-NFE CPU xRT (Xeon, batch 16, 1 s) | 0.64 | **4.85** |
| 1-NFE GPU xRT (4090 / H100) | 303× / 226× | 851× (H100 only) |
| 1-NFE PESQ (LibriTTS) | 3.514 | 4.19 |
| Code availability | Apache-2.0 (`luotianze666/WaveFM`) | Released with paper |

**They make opposite architectural and finishing choices, both reach competitive 1-NFE quality, but only the STFT-domain + GAN combination is faster than real time on CPU.** The dichotomy maps cleanly to:

- **Finishing move**: CM (deterministic teacher distillation, stable, easy to train) vs GAN (more capacity to recover sharpness, harder to stabilise).
- **Backbone choice**: sample-rate waveform U-Net (legacy HiFi-GAN family, slow on CPU) vs hop-rate STFT-domain ConvNeXt (Vocos family, fast on CPU).

The recipe-axis question for the project: **does FM + CM port to a hop-rate / STFT-domain backbone?** WaveFM doesn't test this. Flow2GAN didn't test FM + CM. The empty cell is **STFT-domain ConvNeXt + reparameterized FM + consistency distillation** — most natural unconditional waveform candidate.

### vs IMM-family consistency-from-scratch (`2503.07565`)

- WaveFM is **consistency post-training** with a pretrained FM teacher — needs the multi-step model first.
- IMM is **consistency from scratch** via inductive MMD bootstrap, no teacher.
- IMM Lemma 1: CM is a single-particle (M=1), first-moment (energy-kernel) special case of IMM. WaveFM's consistency distillation is exactly that corner: M=1, L2 distance (energy-kernel-equivalent), teacher's `x̂_{t_n}` as the bootstrap target. **The recipe IMM identifies as the worst point in its design space is what WaveFM uses**, but with a strong pretrained teacher carrying the load.
- If WaveFM-style 1-NFE distillation falls short of quality on raw waveform, **upgrading the kernel (Laplace, signature-on-paths) or particle count (M=2) is the natural next experiment** — both already enumerated in IMM / signature-kernel.

### vs WaveGrad / DiffWave / PriorGrad / FreGrad / FastDiff (the 6-NFE diffusion baselines this paper compares against)

WaveFM-1 step **beats them all on PESQ / MCD / V-UV F1 / SMOS** at 6× fewer NFE. This is the relevant audio data point: 1-step CM-distilled FM in the right parameterisation closes the quality gap to 6-step diffusion vocoders.

## How this translates to raw audio (unconditional)

The recipe parts that **port directly** to unconditional raw-waveform FM:

1. **Predict `x₁`, not `v`.** Independent rederivation of Flow2GAN's audio-FM-fundamental fix. Drop into any audio FM training loop. WaveFM's argument (random-derivative inductive bias incompatibility with snake/periodic activations) is a more general one than Flow2GAN's (silent regions force `v ≈ −x₀`); both reach the same place.
2. **Auxiliary losses on `x̂₁` only — never on `v`.** Both papers agree; this is the audio-FM design rule.
3. **Multi-resolution STFT loss with explicit phase-angle term.** Flow2GAN doesn't use raw-STFT loss (PeriodWave-Turbo found it leaks electric noise); WaveFM uses it but redesigns it: `atan2`-wrapped phase difference + magnitude gradients + Laplacian. **This is the one place the two papers diverge** — Flow2GAN bakes phase into the iSTFT readout (network predicts the complex spectrogram, phase is implicit), WaveFM keeps a waveform-output network and adds an explicit phase loss. For STFT-domain unconditional FM, Flow2GAN's implicit-phase route is probably the right one; WaveFM's explicit phase loss matters only if you keep a waveform-output backbone.
4. **Consistency distillation as the 1-step finishing move.** Stable, cheap (25 k steps), no discriminator to balance. Per IMM Lemma 1, this is the M=1 energy-kernel corner of inductive MMD — drop-in upgrades are M=2 + Laplace / signature kernel.
5. **Truncated-Gaussian `t` sampling near `t=0`** for the distillation phase. Concentrates supervision on the 1-step generation regime. Drop-in into any from-scratch consistency or distillation loop.
6. **Snake-beta activation + dilated MRF ResBlock structure** — only relevant if the backbone is waveform-domain. Skip if STFT-domain.

The recipe parts that **don't port** to unconditional:

1. **Mel-conditioned prior `N(0, Σ_mel)`.** Requires mel conditioning that captures audio energy. Without it, you're back to standard `N(0, I)` prior. Open question: does an unconditional analogue exist? (Constant data-side variance from training set?). This is the FM+CM analogue of the open question on Flow2GAN's spectral-energy-inverse weighting under unconditional generation.
2. **`x₁`-prediction with arbitrary `t` distribution.** Conditional FM has a tight pinning of the data distribution; the `x₁`-prediction error is well-bounded everywhere. Unconditional FM has no per-sample anchor, and the `1/(1−t)²` weight near `t=1` may explode in a less controllable way. WaveFM's 0.1-floor clamp is a partial fix; whether it survives unconditional training scale is untested.

## Open questions

- **FM + CM on an STFT-domain backbone.** The natural composition of WaveFM's recipe with Flow2GAN's architecture; untested anywhere. Would presumably outperform WaveFM on CPU (hop-rate sequences are cheaper) while keeping the stable CM training (vs Flow2GAN's GAN). If quality holds, this is the project's preferred FM+post-training audio recipe.
- **FM + CM vs FM + GAN at matched architecture.** WaveFM and Flow2GAN are not directly comparable (different backbones, different conditioning, different evaluation setups). The clean A/B would be: pick one backbone (STFT-domain ConvNeXt of Flow2GAN), pretrain reparameterized FM once, finetune two copies — CM vs GAN — at the same step count, compare PESQ / MOS / CPU xRT. **Single most informative cross-paper experiment** on the audio post-training axis.
- **Does WaveFM's mel-conditioned-prior trick port to unconditional raw audio?** The mel here is a per-utterance energy controller. Unconditional analogues: (a) constant data-side `Σ` from the training-set sample-rate energy; (b) global RMS-conditioned `Σ` (energy is a learned input). Untested.
- **WaveFM CM with M ≥ 2 (IMM upgrade).** IMM Lemma 1 says CM is M=1 energy-kernel inductive MMD. WaveFM is M=1. The upgrade to M=2 + Laplace / signature kernel is a one-line change. Could test on WaveFM directly.
- **WaveFM at smaller scale.** 19.5 M is the smallest FM-vocoder in the wiki; would WaveFM-style consistency distillation work at 5 M params (the project's CPU-budget target)? Currently no published data.
- **OOD generalisation gap.** WaveFM-1 step OOD music shows a 0.09 SMOS gap to WaveFM-6 steps, larger than the in-domain 0.08 gap. Consistency students are more brittle to distribution shift than their teachers. Question: does this gap grow on harder OOD (non-vocal music)?
- **Phase-angle loss outside vocoder regime.** The `atan2`-wrapped phase loss has clear semantics on speech (the phase is dominated by the vocal tract / glottal pulses). On unconditional music or drum-one-shot generation, the phase is far less structured; whether `L_pha` helps or hurts is unknown.
