# TL;DR — why this matters for raw-audio CPU gen

TiM is the **first from-scratch generative paradigm where a single trained network smoothly interpolates 1-NFE ↔ many-NFE with monotonic quality improvement**, derived from an *exact continuous-time finite-interval transition identity* on the PF-ODE rather than from local-velocity supervision (diffusion / FM) or fixed-endpoint supervision (consistency / shortcut / MeanFlow). For a CPU-bound deployment this is the right shape: bench at 1 NFE for interactive UX, fall back to 2–4 NFE on hard inputs **without retraining a separate model**, and use the *same* checkpoint as a "refinable integrator" when wall-clock allows. Every other 1-NFE method in the wiki (iMF, SoFlow, pMF, Drifting, W-Flow, EPG, energy-distance) either saturates or *degrades* with more steps — the "few-step ceiling" pathology. TiM's headline experiment shows an 865M model **beating SD3.5-Large (8B) and FLUX.1-Dev (12B) on GenEval at every NFE from 1 to 128** (Tab. 2: 1-NFE 0.67 vs SD3.5-Large 0.69@100; 8-NFE 0.76 vs FLUX.1-Schnell 0.68@1; 128-NFE 0.83 — monotonic). Two training-recipe contributions are independently portable: (i) **DDE** (Differential Derivative Equation) — a forward-only central finite-difference approximation `df_θ/dt ≈ (f(x_{t+ε},t+ε,r) − f(x_{t−ε},t−ε,r))/(2ε)` that replaces JVP, runs ~2× faster, and **works under FlashAttention + FSDP** (JVP doesn't); (ii) **decoupled (t, Δt) time embedding + interval-aware attention** — let the network reason about absolute time *and* the requested transition interval separately, with attention `q, k, v` projections that take `E_Δt` as an additive bias.

# What problem does it solve

Every from-scratch generative paradigm currently in the wiki commits to **one** training-time supervision granularity:

- **Diffusion / FM / score-matching** supervise the *local* PF-ODE: `f_θ(x_t, t)` learns instantaneous dynamics (score, velocity, or denoiser). High fidelity at high NFE; discretisation error dominates as NFE drops; **quality degrades or stagnates below ~50 NFE**.
- **Consistency / shortcut / MeanFlow / IMM** supervise a *fixed-endpoint* map `f_θ(x_t, t) → x_0` (or an average velocity that integrates to one). Strong few-step quality; but "by averaging the entire trajectory, they irrevocably discard the fine-grained local dynamics necessary for high-fidelity refinement. This leads to performance saturation — while effective for few-step generation, it offers no gains from additional sampling budget." (§1 of TiM). **Quality plateaus or *drops* with more steps** (Fig. 1 top: FLUX.1-Schnell, SDXL-Turbo, SD3.5-Turbo all degrade past 8 NFE).

TiM's thesis: neither choice is fundamental — both are byproducts of supervising at a *fixed* granularity. The right move is to make the network learn the **entire solution manifold** of the PF-ODE by parameterising `f_θ(x_t, t, r)` over *any* interval `Δt = t − r` and supervising with a single identity that holds exactly for *all* `(t, r)` simultaneously. With one such network, **NFE becomes a runtime choice on the same checkpoint**, and the paper demonstrates monotonic improvement with NFE — i.e. the few-step ceiling is gone.

This is a *different* axis from the WGF/Drifting/EqM moves: it doesn't change the inference primitive (still ODE-style Euler-like steps), it changes the *training supervision* so the same network is correct at *every* step size simultaneously.

# Method

## (1) The State Transition Identity (Eq. 8) — the exact finite-interval law

Forward process: `x_t = α_t x + σ_t ε` with `x ∼ p_data, ε ∼ N(0,I)`. The PF-ODE rewrites as `dx_t/dt = (α̇_t/α_t) x_t + (σ̇_t − (α̇_t/α_t)σ_t) ε`. Standard diffusion/FM parameterises a *local* operator `f_θ(x_t, t)`; TiM parameterises a *finite-interval transition* `f_θ(x_t, t, r) ≈ f(x_t, t, r)` such that running it once jumps `x_t → x_r` along the PF-ODE for *any* `r < t`.

Plug the network into the first-order PF-ODE relation `x_r = ((α_r σ̇_t − σ_r α̇_t) x_t + (σ_r α_t − α_r σ_t) f_θ(x_t, t, r)) / (σ̇_t α_t − α̇_t σ_t)` (Eq. 6, where `f_θ` is `x`-and-`ε`-blended via the transport's diffusion coefficients), differentiate both sides w.r.t. `t`, simplify (full derivation Appx. A.1, 11 lines). The result is the **State Transition Identity**

```
d/dt ( B_{t,r} · ( α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε − f_{θ,t,r} ) ) = 0
```

equivalently (expanded)

```
( α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε − f_{θ,t,r} ) · dB_{t,r}/dt  +  B_{t,r} · d/dt(α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε − f_{θ,t,r}) = 0
       └─────── PF-ODE supervision ───────┘                 └─── time-slope matching ───┘
```

with `α̂_t, σ̂_t` the time-derivative coefficients of the chosen transport (Tab. 7 lists `α_t, σ_t, α̂_t, σ̂_t` for OT-FM, TrigFlow, EDM, VP, VE — TiM unifies all of them under one objective). `B_{t,r}` is a transport-determined kernel; e.g. for OT-FM `B_{t,r} = r − t` linear in the interval, for EDM it's `(t−r)σ²_d √(t²+σ²_d) / ((t²+σ²_d)^{3/2} √(r²+σ²_d))`.

**Two structural properties the identity enforces simultaneously**:

- **Implicit trajectory consistency.** The product `B_{t,r} · h(t)` (with `h(t) = α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε − f_{θ,t,r}`) must be constant over `t` for fixed `r`. So the direct map `(t → r)` must equal any composition `(t → s) ∘ (s → r)` for `s ∈ (r, t)`. **This is the few-step ↔ many-step compositionality that makes monotonic NFE-quality work** — and the property "absent in standard consistency models" per the paper.
- **Time-slope matching.** Unpacking the product rule: not just `h(t) = 0` (the diffusion target) but `d h(t)/dt = 0` — the *temporal derivative* of the residual is also driven to zero. This higher-order supervision compels the network to learn a *smoother* solution manifold — the part that makes large-step sampling stable.

Solving Eq. 8 for the regression target gives the **TiM training objective** (Eq. 13):

```
L(θ) = E_{x,ε,t,r}[ w(t,r) · d( f_θ(x_t, t, r) , f̂(x, ε, t, r) ) ]
```

with target

```
f̂ = α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε + (B_{t,r} / (dB_{t,r}/dt)) · ( (dα̂_t/dt) x + (dσ̂_t/dt) ε − df_{θ−,t,r}/dt )
```

where `θ−` means the EMA-stop-grad parameters and `df_{θ−,t,r}/dt` is the time derivative of the network at fixed `r` — the load-bearing object that this paper makes practical.

**Reduces to diffusion at the boundary.** Take `r → t`: `B_{t,r}/dB_{t,r}/dt → 0`, the second bracket collapses, and `f̂ → α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε` — exactly the FM / diffusion target (Appx. B.1). So TiM is a strict superset of every transport's standard objective, only differing when `t ≠ r`.

## (2) DDE — replacing JVP with a 2-eval central finite difference

The training target needs `df_{θ−,t,r}/dt`. MeanFlow / iMF / SoFlow / sCM compute this via JVP, which (a) is compute-heavy and (b) is **incompatible with FlashAttention and FSDP** — a hard ceiling on practical billion-param training.

TiM's **Differential Derivative Equation** (Eq. 10) approximation:

```
df_{θ−,t,r}/dt  ≈  ( f_{θ−}(x_{t+ε}, t+ε, r) − f_{θ−}(x_{t−ε}, t−ε, r) ) / (2ε)
```

Two extra **forward-only** evaluations of the network at perturbed `(x_{t±ε}, t±ε)`. Properties (Tab. 1, TiM-B/4 on A100, batch 256, BF16):

|             | FLOPs (G) | Latency (ms) | Throughput (it/s) | Memory (GB) | 1-NFE FID | 8-NFE FID | 50-NFE FID |
|---           |---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **JVP**     | 48.29 | 213.14 | 1.80 | 14.89 | 49.75 | 26.22 | 18.11 |
| **DDE**     | **24.14** | **110.08** | **2.40** | **15.23** | 49.91 | 26.09 | **17.99** |

DDE is **~2× cheaper** at training time at matched (or slightly better) FID, *and* "natively compatible with FSDP" — JVP's reliance on reverse-mode autograd of forward-mode primitives breaks both FlashAttention and FSDP. This is what makes the **865M T2I from-scratch run feasible at all** (16 A100s × 30 days).

The constant `ε` is set "small but not too small" — TiM does not give a sweep but Algorithm 1 treats it as a fixed hyperparameter.

## (3) Architecture — decoupled (t, Δt) embeddings + interval-aware attention

Vanilla DiT conditioning takes only `t` (or `(t, r)` summed). Two refinements (Tab. 4 ablation):

- **Decoupled time embedding (De-TE).** Two independent encoders `φ_t(t)` and `φ_Δt(Δt)` summed to form the conditioning vector `E_{t,Δt} = φ_t(t) + φ_Δt(Δt)`. Lets the model distinguish "where on the trajectory am I?" from "how far am I jumping?". Ablation (Tab. 4 d→e, B/4 ImageNet 256²): 56.22 → 49.91 1-NFE FID, 28.75 → 26.09 8-NFE FID, 20.37 → 17.99 50-NFE FID.

- **Interval-aware attention (IA-Attn).** Standard self-attention is interval-agnostic. A large `Δt` should drive *global, coarse-grained* attention; a small `Δt` should drive *local, fine refinement*. TiM injects `E_Δt` into Q/K/V via additive interval-conditioned projections:

  ```
  q = z W_q + b_q + E_Δt W'_q
  k = z W_k + b_k + E_Δt W'_k
  v = z W_v + b_v + E_Δt W'_v
  ```

  Each token's q/k/v gets a `Δt`-conditioned shift before attention. Marginal gain alone (Tab. 4 d→f: 48.38 1-NFE FID); **combined with De-TE (g)** the gains compound to 48.30 / 25.05 / 17.43.

- **Time-warped interval weighting (Eq. 11–12).** Short intervals dominate training (sampled more often, less noise in target). Loss weighting `w(t,r) = (σ_data + tan(t) − tan(r))^{−1/2}` (tangent-space warping). Tab. 4 g→h: 47.46 / 24.62 / 17.10 — best run.

- **Text-conditioning split.** For T2I, time conditioning `E_{t,Δt}` modulates AdaLN only; text features go through dedicated cross-attention. For class-conditional, class embedding is added to the time embedding pre-AdaLN.

## Connections to prior fastforward methods (Appx. B.2)

The paper formally connects TiM to:

- **Continuous-time CMs (sCM, etc.).** TiM with `r = 0` and `d = ‖·‖²` gives a gradient (Eq. 39) that differs from the continuous-time CM gradient (Eq. 46) by exactly the multiplicative `B_{t,0} · dB_{t,0}/dt` term — bridgeable via the weighting function. **sCM is a fixed-`r=0` slice of TiM.**
- **Consistency Trajectory Models (CTM), Phased Consistency Models, Shortcut Models.** All learn *discrete-interval* transitions between specific (t, r) pairs. TiM generalises to the *continuous* `(t, r)` domain via the identity Eq. 8.
- **MeanFlow / iMF / SoFlow.** These learn an average velocity `u(z_t, t, r) = (1/(t−r)) ∫_r^t v dτ`. The MeanFlow identity `v = u + (t−r)·du/dt` is a *specific instance* of TiM's identity for OT-FM transport. **MeanFlow is a transport-specific special case of TiM**; SoFlow's solution-flow parameterisation `f_θ(x_t, t, s)` is essentially equivalent to TiM's `f_θ(x_t, t, r)` but trained with a different identity (Taylor finite-difference SCM vs TiM's exact state-transition identity).

## Architecture & scale

Backbone: DiT [52] with the three modifications above. Two scales reported:
- **Class-conditional ImageNet 256² ablations**: TiM-B/4 (131M), 80 epochs.
- **T2I headline**: TiM 865M, ~33M images (Megalith-10M, jackyhate-text-to-image-2M, LAION-400M Recaps, BLIP3o-60K-GPT-4o, ShareGPT-4V, MJHQ-30K, etc.). DC-AE [13] tokenizer (vs SD-VAE for ablations). Gemma3-1B-it text encoder. 16× A100, ~30 days, BF16. Native-resolution training with resolution-dependent timestep shifting (SD3-style).

# Inference cost — the headline for this project

This is the load-bearing property: **same checkpoint, every NFE, monotonic quality**.

- **1 NFE**: GenEval 0.67 — beats SDXL-Turbo (0.40), Hyper-SDXL (0.46), SD3.5-Turbo (0.50), and **FLUX.1-Schnell at 1-NFE** (0.68, with 12B params vs TiM's 865M). MJHQ-30K FID 6.68 — *better than every multi-step baseline in Tab. 3* (PixArt-α 6.14, PixArt-Σ 6.15, SDXL 6.63, SD3.5-Large 11.92, FLUX.1-dev 9.19 — TiM at 1-NFE matches the *best* of them with one forward pass).
- **8 NFE**: GenEval 0.76 — beats SD3.5-Turbo (8 NFE, 8B params, 0.69), Hyper-SDXL (0.46), and FLUX.1-Schnell at 1-NFE (0.68). MJHQ-30K FID 5.28.
- **32 NFE**: GenEval 0.80, MJHQ-30K FID 5.65.
- **128 NFE**: GenEval 0.83 — *new SOTA across all of Tab. 2*, beating SD3.5-Large@128 (0.69), FLUX.1-Dev@128 (0.65), SANA-1.5@40 (0.81). Monotonic — every doubling of NFE buys quality.

This monotonicity is the entire point. **Compare to FLUX.1-Schnell**: 0.68 GenEval at 1-NFE → 0.67 at 2 → 0.62 at 32 → 0.58 at 128 — *strictly degrades* with more compute. SDXL-Turbo, SD3.5-Turbo: same pattern. TiM is the first from-scratch model in the literature where this pathology is *absent*.

**Resolution & aspect-ratio robustness** (Tab. 6, Fig. 1 middle/bottom). TiM trained native-resolution generalises to 1024×4096 (39 GenEval at 8-NFE) where SD3.5-Turbo / FLUX.1-Schnell fail (✗ — GenEval < 0.10). Aspect ratios 2:5 to 5:2 all stay ≥ 0.55. This is downstream of the native-resolution training strategy plus the interval-aware attention; not specific to TiM's objective per se but a useful property to inherit if we port the architecture.

**Per-step cost is the standard DiT cost.** TiM doesn't change per-NFE FLOPs; it just makes the NFE-quality curve well-behaved. So "1-NFE FID 6.68 on MJHQ-30K at 865M params" sits in the same compute ballpark as iMF-XL/2 (610M, FID-ImageNet 1.72 at 1-NFE) — different benchmarks so not directly comparable, but the per-step CPU cost should be in the same order of magnitude.

No CPU benchmark in the paper. No GFLOPs/sample reported. The 865M / 16-A100×30-day training cost is reported, the inference latency is not.

# How this translates to raw audio

The objective and DDE machinery are dimension-agnostic. The architecture pieces are most useful if the audio backbone is Transformer-shaped; some adapt to 1-D conv with substitutions.

- **TiM objective is dimension-agnostic.** Replace image patches with 1-D conv / 1-D Transformer tokens. The State Transition Identity holds for any PF-ODE, so it ports as-is to raw-waveform FM / diffusion training. No image-specific assumptions. The transport coefficients `(α_t, σ_t, α̂_t, σ̂_t)` and `B_{t,r}` are read off Tab. 7 for the audio team's chosen transport (OT-FM is the simplest).
- **DDE is the load-bearing portable trick.** For a 1-D conv U-Net (CRASH-style) where JVP through `torch.func.jvp` is in principle supported but slow at our memory budget, DDE is **strictly cheaper at training time** (~2× throughput) and is the only viable option if we want FSDP / FlashAttention for a 1-D Transformer-on-waveform variant. *This is the single most directly useful trick in this paper for our codebase.*
- **Decoupled (t, Δt) conditioning.** On a 1-D conv backbone, the analogue is **dual FiLM**: separate `φ_t(t)` and `φ_Δt(Δt)` heads, summed FiLM-style into each conv block, rather than the usual single `σ`-FiLM. Cheap edit. The ablation 56.22 → 49.91 (B/4, 1-NFE) suggests this alone is ~11% relative FID improvement at 1-NFE — large enough to be worth testing.
- **Interval-aware attention** translates to **interval-conditioned conv kernels** if we have a Transformer-on-waveform; on a pure 1-D conv U-Net there's no attention to modify, so this piece becomes "use a Transformer bottleneck conditioned on `E_Δt`" or skip it. The paper's ablation shows IA-Attn alone (f) gives a smaller boost than De-TE alone (e): 48.38 vs 49.91 — but the *combined* g→h is where the win compounds (48.30 → 47.46). On a hybrid 1-D conv + bottleneck-Transformer (likely architecture for waveform), IA-Attn at the bottleneck is the right shape.
- **Variable-NFE on CPU is the headline property.** For a deployment that has to be 1-NFE on a laptop but allow opt-in "refine" mode for hard inputs, TiM is the *only* method in the wiki that supports this on one checkpoint with monotonic quality. The interactive UX implication: ship a single waveform model, default to 1-NFE preview, expose a "high-quality" slider that runs 8/32/128 NFE — and quality *improves* every time.
- **Audio CPU concern.** TiM's per-step cost is full DiT-865M; for 1 s @ 22 kHz waveform we'd need a much smaller backbone. The recipe is what we adopt; the checkpoint isn't useful.
- **Monotonic-quality property as a regulariser, not just a UX win.** The implicit-trajectory-consistency constraint (composition `(t→s)∘(s→r) = (t→r)`) acts like a strong path-consistency regulariser on the learned manifold. For audio — where mode collapse and timbral over-smoothing are the typical failure modes of one-step methods — this kind of structural constraint is the right shape, *not* the GAN-style discriminator fix.
- **Phase/waveform fidelity.** Same caveat as iMF: the loss is plain `‖f_θ − f̂‖²` on raw samples, no phase-aware reweighting. Pair with Flow2GAN's spectral-energy-inverse weighting if needed.
- **No SSL pre-training required.** Like iMF / SoFlow, TiM is from-scratch with no encoder bootstrap. Compares favourably to EPG (which needs an SSL stage) for the audio bootstrap path.

# Strengths

- **Only known from-scratch method with monotonic quality vs NFE.** Resolves the few-step ceiling pathology that *every* other 1-NFE method in the wiki exhibits.
- **Single network, runtime-variable NFE.** Same checkpoint serves 1-NFE preview and 128-NFE high-fidelity. The deployment-flexibility analogue of EqM but on the standard ODE-step axis (so existing schedule/sampler infrastructure carries over).
- **DDE is a strict practical improvement over JVP.** ~2× training throughput, matched FID, FlashAttention/FSDP compatible. Plug-in replacement for any JVP-based fastforward training loop (MeanFlow, iMF, SoFlow training pipelines could all drop DDE in).
- **865M beats 8B and 12B on GenEval at all NFE.** Compute-efficiency claim is solid.
- **Generalises every transport.** Unifies OT-FM, TrigFlow, EDM, VP, VE under one objective (Tab. 7).
- **Native-resolution + interval-aware attention generalises to 1024×4096** where teacher-distilled models fail at >2048. Useful structural property; transfers to variable-length audio if we go Transformer.

# Limitations (project-relevant)

- **No CPU benchmark.** Standard image-paper omission. We have no idea what 865M TiM costs per forward pass on CPU; for our purposes the recipe transfers, not the checkpoint.
- **The 128-NFE wins are still wall-clock-expensive on CPU.** Monotonic quality with NFE is great in principle but doesn't change per-step cost. For 1 s of audio at our target (>1× RT on CPU), we still need 1-NFE to dominate; the value of monotonic improvement is "graceful refinement when budget allows", not "guaranteed real-time at every NFE".
- **DDE has a hyperparameter `ε`** with no sweep reported. Discretisation error of the finite difference depends on `ε`; too large biases the target, too small amplifies noise from network stochasticity (BF16 numerics, dropout). Audio replication needs an empirical sweep.
- **Class-conditional ablations are at TiM-B/4 (131M) for 80 epochs only.** The 865M T2I is the headline; not an apples-to-apples comparison to other 1-NFE methods (iMF-XL/2 at 610M, 240 epochs, ImageNet-256 FID 1.72). TiM-B/4's 47.46 1-NFE FID on ImageNet at 80 epochs is not directly comparable to iMF's published number — different schedule, different training length. The paper doesn't run TiM at iMF-XL/2 scale on the standard ImageNet-256 1-NFE FID protocol; **we cannot directly read off a head-to-head 1-NFE FID number** between TiM and iMF/pMF/SoFlow/Drifting/W-Flow.
- **Generates degraded content "occasionally at high resolutions (3072×4096) attributable to biases in the underlying autoencoder"** (paper's own limitations). Inherits DC-AE artefacts.
- **No raw-pixel variant tested.** TiM is run on SD-VAE latents (ImageNet-256) and DC-AE latents (T2I). Whether the objective survives at raw waveform without a frozen encoder is the open audio question.
- **Loss-weighting choice (tangent warping) isn't ablated against alternatives.** Appendix Tab. 12 likely contains alternatives — paper text only gives the chosen form. Audio replication may need its own warping schedule (audio data distributions are heavier-tailed than ImageNet in time, equal in frequency).

# Connection to other ingested papers

- **iMF (2512.02012), SoFlow (2512.15657), pMF (2601.22158), MeanFlow.** TiM **strictly subsumes** the MeanFlow family at the objective level: TiM's identity reduces to MeanFlow's average-velocity identity for OT-FM transport. iMF / SoFlow / pMF are points in TiM's design space at fixed transport + specific JVP-or-Taylor strategies. **DDE is also a strict alternative to iMF's stop-grad'd JVP and to SoFlow's Taylor finite difference** — same train-time cost as SoFlow, same backward properties as iMF (target is network-independent), no JVP, FSDP/FlashAttn compatible. The unexplored hybrid "iMF v-loss + DDE + flexible-CFG conditioning" is straightforwardly constructable.
- **Consistency Models (iCT, EPG-CT branch, IMM 2503.07565).** TiM with `r = 0` and `d = ‖·‖²` gives a gradient that differs from continuous-time CM only by a `B_{t,0}·dB_{t,0}/dt` weighting (Appx. B.2). **TiM is a strict generalisation of continuous-time CM**: CMs supervise only `(t → 0)`, TiM supervises *all* `(t → r)` simultaneously. The few-step monotonic-quality property is the visible payoff for this strictly more demanding supervision.
- **CTM / PCM / Shortcut Models.** Discrete-interval predecessors of TiM. Same direction (learn `(t → r)` transitions), discretised to a fixed grid. TiM takes them to continuous `(r, t)`.
- **EqM (2510.02300).** Different axis: EqM also gives a runtime-variable inference budget but via *sampling-as-optimization* on a time-independent energy field — completely throws out PF-ODE dynamics. TiM keeps the PF-ODE; just supervises it over all intervals. **Different families of variable-NFE story**: EqM's knob is "optimizer step-count on a static field"; TiM's knob is "number of finite-interval transitions along the trajectory". Both could compose into a "TiM-as-coarse-scaffold then EqM-as-local-refinement" two-stage sampler, untested.
- **FD-loss (2604.28190).** Post-training Fréchet-distance polish; the TiM paper does not test this composition. **TiM-base + FD-loss post-train at 1-NFE** is the natural stacked pipeline (analogous to iMF + FD-loss = 0.72 FID).
- **W-Flow (2605.11755).** Different family (one-step compression of a training-time Wasserstein gradient flow). W-Flow currently holds 1-NFE SOTA on ImageNet 256² (FID 1.29). Whether TiM at iMF/W-Flow scale on the same benchmark beats it is not in the paper. **The structural difference matters**: W-Flow is strictly 1-NFE (multi-step inference not supported by design); TiM is 1-NFE + arbitrary-NFE on one checkpoint. For project use, TiM's flexibility is the more valuable property even if W-Flow has a better 1-NFE FID head-to-head.
- **CRASH (2106.07431).** CRASH provides the audio backbone template (1-D U-Net, σ-FiLM); TiM training on a CRASH backbone (replace σ-FiLM with dual-FiLM `(t, Δt)`; use DDE; switch loss to TiM target) is the obvious audio replication setup.

# Open questions for the repo

1. **DDE on a 1-D conv backbone — `ε` sweep.** The image paper uses a fixed `ε` (value not in main text). On waveform inputs at 22 kHz, the natural `ε` scale is unclear; sweep `ε ∈ {1e-4, 1e-3, 1e-2, 1e-1}` × `(t, x_t)` perturbation and report DDE-vs-JVP correlation at matched compute.
2. **TiM on the CRASH 1-D U-Net.** Smallest experiment: TiM objective + DDE + dual `(t, Δt)` FiLM on CRASH-size backbone (~30 M params) on the drum dataset. Compare 1-NFE / 8-NFE / 50-NFE FAD against vanilla CRASH (50-NFE) and against a from-scratch iMF on the same backbone.
3. **Does monotonic quality vs NFE survive on small backbones / small datasets?** TiM's headline is a 865M T2I run on 33M images. Small-data regime: at 30k drum clips with a 30 M model, does the NFE-quality curve stay monotone, or do we hit overfitting that breaks the implicit-consistency property?
4. **TiM at iMF/W-Flow scale on the same ImageNet-256 1-NFE FID protocol.** The paper doesn't run this; the 47.46 FID at TiM-B/4 / 80 epochs is not comparable to iMF-XL/2 / 240 epochs / 1.72 FID. **The unanswered question for the wiki's 1-NFE leaderboard is whether TiM-XL/2 at 240+ epochs beats iMF, pMF, W-Flow at strict 1-NFE on ImageNet-256.**
5. **DDE as drop-in replacement for iMF / SoFlow JVP / Taylor SCM.** Both could be retrained with DDE in place of their current target-time-derivative computation. Does iMF + DDE match iMF-with-JVP at half the training cost? If yes, the "iMF v-loss + flexible CFG + DDE" hybrid is a strict superset of both iMF and SoFlow's training advantages.
6. **TiM + FD-loss post-train.** The natural extension to push 1-NFE quality below TiM-alone. Untested.
7. **Variable-length audio via TiM + ELIT.** ELIT (2603.12245) gives runtime-variable per-step FLOPs; TiM gives runtime-variable NFE. Stacking gives a two-axis runtime budget knob on one checkpoint. Untested; clean composition.
8. **Audio CPU cost of the recipe at small backbone.** Run TiM-style training on a ~5 M 1-D conv U-Net, measure CPU forward-pass cost (batch=1, laptop CPU) at 1-NFE for 1 s @ 22 kHz. Critical missing data.
9. **Loss-weighting `w(t, r)` choice for audio.** TiM uses tangent-space warping. Audio data has different temporal/spectral concentration; the right warping may not be `(σ_data + tan(t) − tan(r))^{−1/2}`. Likely a couple of FAD points on the table — worth ablating.
