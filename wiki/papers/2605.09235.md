# TL;DR ‚ÄĒ why this matters for raw-audio CPU gen

The theoretical paper the MeanFlow audio program has been missing. Proves that the conditional velocity field `v_cond = x_1 - x_0` plays **two statistically distinct roles** inside the original MeanFlow loss ‚ÄĒ *regression target* (where it is an unbiased estimator of the marginal velocity `v(x_t, t)`) and *Monte-Carlo control variate inside the JVP tangent* (where the original objective gives it coefficient `ő≤ = 0`, which is structurally wrong). Derives the **closed-form optimal control-variate coefficient** `ő≤* = őļ/(őļ+1) ¬∑ ŌÉ¬≤d/(ŌÉ¬≤d + ‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ¬≤)` (Theorem 4) ‚ÄĒ a product of a noise-cancellation factor `őļ/(őļ+1)` and a James-Stein-style shrinkage. The gradient variance under the original `ő≤ = 0` scales unboundedly with `‚ÄĖJ‚ÄĖ¬≤` where `J = (t-r)‚ąā_{x_t} u_őł ‚ąí I_d` is the **Jacobi factor**; **the stop-gradient hides this amplification from the optimizer** ("semi-gradient gap", Theorem 3) ‚ÄĒ the formal mechanism behind MeanFlow's empirical "non-decreasing loss with high-variance gradient" pathology. **Table 1 unifies every concurrent "fix MeanFlow" paper as a different practical realization of the same `ő≤ ‚Üí 1` (or `J ‚Üí 0`) corner**: iMF (separate v-head), AlphaFlow (őĪ-curriculum avoiding high-őļ regimes), Re-MeanFlow (rectified-flow preprocessing reduces `‚ÄĖJ‚ÄĖ¬≤`), TVM (terminal-time differentiation removes spatial `J`), Modular MF, Decoupled MF, Kim et al.'s staged schedule. The wiki's MeanFlow lineage now has a **single statistical-efficiency lens** to read all of them through, rather than seven competing identity-level / target-level / curriculum-level tricks. For raw audio specifically: **(a)** the analysis is dimension-agnostic and the variance amplification grows with the spatial Jacobian's spectral magnitude ‚ÄĒ waveform's much higher d (`d ‚Čą 22k` at 1 s / 22 kHz vs ImageNet latent `d ‚Čą 4k`) makes the variance pathology *worse*, not better, and the `ő≤ ‚Üí 1` corner correspondingly more important; **(b)** the **EMA-tangent + FM-anchor recipe** (Algorithm 1: replace JVP tangent with `u_őłŐĄ(x_t, t, t)` via Polyak average, anchor with small FM loss at `r = t ‚ąí őī`) is the cheapest practical realization ‚ÄĒ one extra EMA forward + one anchor forward per step, no separate v-head, no curriculum, no identity change, **drops straight into any existing MeanFlow waveform training loop**; **(c)** the FID-MSE landscape mismatch (the `ő≤*` that minimizes gradient MSE sits at `‚Čą 0.94` interior, but the `ő≤*` that minimizes FID sits at `0` ‚ÄĒ the unbiased conditional-velocity corner) is a separate empirical-protocol caveat to remember when running audio FID/FAD experiments. **No CPU benchmark, no 1-D experiments, no audio.** Sets the **theory floor** under all the existing MeanFlow audio prior art ([SplitMeanFlow](2507.16884.md), [MeanFlowSE](2509.23299.md)) and under any from-scratch raw-waveform MeanFlow we end up running.

# What problem does it solve

The MeanFlow loss (Geng 2025, NeurIPS) is

```
L_MF(őł) = E_{r,t,x_0,x_1} [‚ÄĖu_őł + (t‚ąír)¬∑sg(JVP(u_őł; (x_t, r, t), (v_cond, 0, 1))) ‚ąí v_cond‚ÄĖ¬≤]
```

‚ÄĒ with `v_cond = x_1 ‚ąí x_0`. The training pathology that every concurrent paper attacks: **loss is empirically non-decreasing during training** (climbs while sampling quality improves), **gradient variance is unboundedly high** as the trained backbone's spatial Jacobian `‚ąā_{x_t} u_őł` moves away from the flat `(1/(t‚ąír))¬∑I_d` regime. AlphaFlow, iMF, Re-MeanFlow, TVM, Functional Mean Flows, Modular MF, Decoupled MF ‚ÄĒ each empirically works in isolation, no theory explains *why* the original objective is unstable or *what* the fixes have in common.

This paper's contribution is to answer that "why" in a single sentence:

> *The conditional velocity `v_cond` plays two statistical roles inside MeanFlow's loss; the original objective assigns the wrong coefficient to one of them.*

# Method ‚ÄĒ the theoretical derivation

## (1) Two roles of `v_cond`

Reynolds decomposition: `v_cond = v + v'` where `v = v(x, t)` is the (deterministic) marginal velocity and `v'` is the zero-mean conditional fluctuation `Cov_{x_0|x_t}[v'] = ő£_{v'}`.

Substituting into the MeanFlow loss expands the per-sample loss (Eq. 5) as

```
E_{x_0|x_t}[‚Ąď_MF(őł)] = ‚ÄĖr_őł^sg‚ÄĖ¬≤ + sg[Tr(J ő£_{v'} J^T)]
```

where
- `J = (t‚ąír) ‚ąā_{x_t} u_őł ‚ąí I_d` is the **Jacobi factor** (the spatial-derivative-of-the-network term in the JVP linearization),
- `r_őł^sg = u_őł + (t‚ąír)¬∑sg[‚ąā_{x_t} u_őł ¬∑ v + ‚ąā_t u_őł] ‚ąí v` is the **deterministic mean-field residual** ‚ÄĒ the quantity we actually want to minimize per Eq. 3 of the paper (the exact MeanFlow identity).

The trace term `Tr(J ő£_{v'} J^T)` is **non-decreasing** in `‚ÄĖJ‚ÄĖ¬≤` and **only vanishes when `J ‚Üí 0`** (i.e. `‚ąā_{x_t} u_őł ‚Üí (1/(t‚ąír))¬∑I_d`, the trivial-flow corner). The stop-gradient on the JVP outcome hides this term from the optimizer.

**Role split:**
- **Unbiased target.** As regression target, `v_cond = v + v'` injects `v'` *linearly* into the residual ‚ÄĒ contributes only the *irreducible* per-sample noise floor `Tr(ő£_{v'})`. Independent of őł; can serve as an unbiased target (zero-mean property of `v'`). This is the well-understood FM identity (Lemma 1).
- **Variance amplifier.** As JVP tangent, `v_cond` injects `v'` *multiplicatively* through `J` ‚ÄĒ contributes the amplified term `Tr(J ő£_{v'} J^T)`. **This is the dominant variance source.**

## (2) Theorem 2 (Jacobian Variance Amplification)

Let `g = ‚ąá_őł u_őł(x_t, r, t)` be the parameter-Jacobian of the network output, and `G_őł = g g^T` the parameter-space Gram matrix. The total gradient variance satisfies

```
Tr(Cov[‚ąá_őł ‚Ąď_MF | x_t]) ‚ąĚ Tr(g^T J ő£_{v'} J^T g) .
```

For fixed `g` and `ő£_{v'}`, **grows with `‚ÄĖJ‚ÄĖ¬≤` unboundedly** as `J ‚Üí ‚ąě`. Bounded only at the irreducible-noise floor `J ‚Üí 0`.

## (3) Theorem 3 (Semi-Gradient Gap)

The gradient of `L_MF` *with* and *without* stop-gradient differ by

```
2(t‚ąír) E[(‚ąá_őł(‚ąā_{x_t} u_őł ¬∑ v + ‚ąā_t u_őł))^T r_őł]   +   E[‚ąá_őł Tr(J ő£_{v'} J^T)]
       ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ mean-field gradient diff ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ       ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ variance-driven gradient diff ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ
```

At convergence `r_őł ‚Üí 0`, the mean-field-difference term vanishes ‚ÄĒ **the gap is dominated by the variance-driven term** `‚ąá_őł Tr(J ő£_{v'} J^T)`. The stop-gradient operator hides this term from őł-updates, so the optimizer cannot drive `J ‚Üí 0` even when doing so would reduce variance. **This is the formal mechanism of MeanFlow's instability** ‚ÄĒ the stop-grad is structurally tied to the variance pathology.

Without stop-grad, the optimizer could in principle reduce variance via the Jacobian-spectrum-shrinking path; but as the paper notes, removing the stop-grad introduces higher-order gradients w.r.t. őł that make optimization harder in practice. The semi-gradient gap is the *cost* paid for using stop-grad to stabilize the JVP backprop.

## (4) Theorem 4 ‚ÄĒ closed-form optimal coefficient

Define a **tangent-mixing coefficient** `ő≤ ‚ąą [0, 1]`:

```
ŠĻĹ_tang(ő≤) = (1‚ąíő≤) v_cond + ő≤ vŐā = v + (1‚ąíő≤) v' + ő≤ b ,
```

where `vŐā` is *any deterministic proxy* for the marginal velocity `v` (e.g. an EMA-tracked copy of `u_őł`, a separate v-head, or a learned velocity network) and `b = vŐā ‚ąí v` is its bias. `ő≤ = 0` recovers the vanilla MeanFlow (`v_cond` everywhere); `ő≤ = 1` replaces the JVP tangent entirely with the deterministic proxy.

Substituting `ŠĻĹ_tang(ő≤)` into the JVP tangent and writing out the per-sample MSE gives a quadratic in `ő≤`. Under the scalar-isotropic approximation `ő£_{v'} ‚Čą ŌÉ¬≤ I_d`, `J ‚Čą őļ I_d`, and parameter-isotropy `g g^T ‚ąĚ I_d` (which reduces M to per-component MSE):

```
M(ő≤)  ‚ąĚ  ő≤¬≤(őļ+1)¬≤ ‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ¬≤  +  ŌÉ¬≤d ((1‚ąíő≤)őļ ‚ąí ő≤)¬≤
```

with unique minimizer (Eq. 12)

```
ő≤*  =  [ őļ / (őļ+1) ]  ¬∑  [ ŌÉ¬≤d / (ŌÉ¬≤d + ‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ¬≤) ]
       ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ noise-cancellation ‚ĒÄ    ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ shrinkage ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ‚ĒÄ
```

**Two factors with distinct roles:**
- `őļ/(őļ+1)` cancels the Jacobian-amplified noise via destructive interference between `J v'` (in the original term) and `I v'` (introduced by `ő≤ ¬∑ b`). At `J ‚Čą I_d` (flat manifold, `őļ = 0`): `ő≤* ‚Üí 0` ‚ÄĒ no cancellation needed, use the conditional. At large `őļ`: `ő≤* ‚Üí 1` ‚ÄĒ full cancellation needed.
- `ŌÉ¬≤d / (ŌÉ¬≤d + ‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ¬≤)` is the classical **James-Stein shrinkage** pulling `ő≤*` toward 0 when the proxy bias is large. When `‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ ‚Üí 0`: `ő≤* ‚Üí őļ/(őļ+1)`. When `‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ ‚Üí ‚ąě`: `ő≤* ‚Üí 0` (regardless of `J`).

**Original MeanFlow's `ő≤ = 0` is the right choice only when `őļ = 0` (trivial manifold) or `‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ = ‚ąě` (no usable proxy). Both fail in practice** ‚ÄĒ trained backbones produce `J` matrices spectrally far from `(1/(t‚ąír)) I_d`, and there are multiple viable proxies for `v`.

## (5) Table 1 ‚ÄĒ concurrent works unified

The paper's central unification claim. Every concurrent "fix MeanFlow" paper realizes the same optimum at a different practical edge:

| Concurrent work | Mechanism in this framework | Practical realization |
|---|---|---|
| **AlphaFlow** [2510.20771](2510.20771.md) | Avoid high-őļ regimes | őĪ-curriculum schedule |
| **Improved MeanFlow (iMF)** [2512.02012](2512.02012.md) | Deterministic tangent (ő≤ ‚Üí 1) | Separate velocity head + sg |
| Modular MF (You et al. 2025) | Deterministic tangent (ő≤ ‚Üí 1) | Gradient-modulated loss family |
| Kim et al. 2025 | Deterministic tangent (ő≤ ‚Üí 1) | Staged training schedule |
| TVM ‚ÄĒ Terminal Velocity Matching (Zhou et al. 2026) | Remove the spatial Jacobian (J ‚Üí 0) | Differentiate w.r.t. terminal time only |
| Re-MeanFlow (Zhang et al. 2026) | Reduce ‚ÄĖJ‚ÄĖ¬≤ | Rectified-flow preprocessing (straighten trajectories) |
| Decoupled MF (Lee et al. 2025) | Bypass JVP construction altogether | Flow-map conditioning on later t |

**The frame.** Each row corresponds to a different angle on the same optimum: either drive `ő≤ ‚Üí 1` (use a deterministic proxy for the tangent), reduce `‚ÄĖJ‚ÄĖ¬≤` directly (straighter trajectories or skip the spatial Jacobian), or sidestep the construction (Decoupled MF). The paper does **not** subsume these ‚ÄĒ they all have additional motivations and ablations of their own ‚ÄĒ but it provides the **single statistical-efficiency lens** that explains why each works.

Notable: **SplitMeanFlow** ([2507.16884](2507.16884.md)) and **SoFlow** ([2512.15657](2512.15657.md)) are not in Table 1. Both eliminate the JVP entirely via different identities (algebraic interval-splitting / Taylor finite-difference) ‚ÄĒ they bypass the variance-reduction problem rather than solving it. **TiM (2509.04394) DDE** is the closest analogue to "sidestep the JVP construction" but isn't cited.

## (6) Algorithm 1 ‚ÄĒ the practical ő≤ = 1 corner instantiation

Two design choices at near-zero compute overhead:

- **EMA proxy.** Take `vŐā ‚Üź u_őłŐĄ(x_t, t, t)` where `őłŐĄ = őľ¬∑őłŐĄ + (1‚ąíőľ)¬∑őł` is a Polyak EMA. The MeanFlow boundary condition `u_őł(x, t, t) ‚Č° v(x, t)` makes `u_őłŐĄ(x, t, t)` a natural marginal-velocity estimator. The variance loss replaces only the *tangent* with the EMA proxy, **keeps the regression target as `v_cond`** (per the role asymmetry in Sec 3.1, Proposition 3):

  ```
  L_MF^EMA(őł) = E_{r,t,x_0,x_1} [‚ÄĖu_őł + (t‚ąír)¬∑sg(JVP(u_őł; (x_t,r,t), (u_őłŐĄ,t, 0, 1))) ‚ąí v_cond‚ÄĖ¬≤]
  ```

- **Flow-matching anchor.** Proxy bias `b = u_őłŐĄ(x_t, t, t) ‚ąí v(x_t, t)` must be controlled or it propagates through `ő≤(J+I)b`. Supervise the boundary `u_őł(x_t, t, t) = v(x_t, t)` directly with a small FM loss at `r = t ‚ąí őī` (small interior offset, `őī ‚ąą [őī_min, őī_max]` with `őī_max ‚Č™ 1`):

  ```
  L_FM(őł) = E_{t, x_0, x_1} [‚ÄĖu_őł(x_t, t ‚ąí őī, t) ‚ąí v_cond‚ÄĖ¬≤]
  ```

  Evaluated at a small interior `őī` rather than the boundary `őī = 0` for two reasons: (i) same `u(x_t, t‚ąíőī, t)` is what the EMA tangent eventually converges to, so anchor and JVP target share the same network-evaluation pattern; (ii) probing a thin neighbourhood of the boundary is more stable than a single point.

Full loss: `L_{ő≤=1}(őł) = L_MF^EMA(őł) + őĽ L_FM(őł)`. Hyperparameters: `(őī_min, őī_max, őĽ) = (10‚ĀĽ‚Āī, 10‚ĀĽ¬≤, 0.5)` for 2-D toy / DGMM, `(0, 10‚ĀĽ¬≥, 0.1)` for DiT-B/4 on ImageNet-256 at ő≤ = 1. **One extra EMA forward + one extra FM-anchor forward per training step.** ~22% wall-clock overhead on TPU v4 (2.20 step/s vs 2.65 baseline); the interior `ő≤ ‚ąą {0.25, 0.5}` runs incur only the EMA forward (~10% overhead).

Magnitude tracks the spectral magnitude `‚ÄĖJ‚ÄĖ` of the trained backbone ‚ÄĒ high-curvature datasets (eight_gaussians, checkerboard) see the largest reductions.

## DGMM (dense Gaussian mixtures, varying d)

Tab. 2 / Tab. 5: at `d ‚ąą {2, 4, 8, 16, 32}`, SW‚āĀ is essentially ő≤-insensitive (the noise-cancellation factor `őļ/(őļ+1)` saturates near 1 over the entire ő≤ range when the EMA proxy yields small `‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ¬≤`). At `d = 64`, an **interior minimum** appears at `ő≤ = 0.4` with ~9 % SW‚āĀ reduction over `ő≤ = 0` ‚ÄĒ `őļ` comes off saturation with increasing data dimension. **Direct implication for audio:** waveform `d ‚Čą 22k` is well past `d = 64`, and is *expected* to show stronger interior-`ő≤` preference than ImageNet latents at `d ‚Čą 4k`.

**Two complementary measurements:**

Directly validates Theorem 2 ‚ÄĒ the Jacobi-factor amplification is real at DiT scale and grows with `t`.

2. **FID-vs-ő≤ at converged training** (Fig. 4 right, Tab. 6):

   | ő≤    | FID at step 295k | őĒ vs ő≤=0 |
   |------|------------------|----------|
   | 0    | **11.37**        | ‚ÄĒ        |

   Empirical ordering `FID(ő≤=0) < FID(ő≤=0.25) < FID(ő≤=0.5) < FID(ő≤=1)` at *every* 5k-aligned matched-step checkpoint after the early-training transient (54 consecutive datapoints).

**FID-MSE landscape mismatch.** The matrix-form optimal `ő≤*_matrix ‚Čą 0.94` (computed from direct measurement of `‚ÄĖb‚ÄĖ¬≤` and `ŌÉ¬≤d` on the DiT checkpoints) ‚ÄĒ close to the deterministic-tangent corner. The empirical FID-optimal `ő≤` is `0` ‚ÄĒ the unbiased corner. The FID landscape is **super-linear in the MSE-axis bias** (~2.6√ó amplification along the őĒFID axis): FID over-penalises bias relative to gradient MSE, shifting the FID-optimal past the gradient-MSE interior minimizer all the way to `ő≤ = 0`. So:

- **If gradient stability is the primary concern** ‚Üí `ő≤ ‚Üí őļ/(őļ+1)` via EMA proxy + FM anchor.
- **If sample quality is the primary concern** ‚Üí `ő≤ ‚Üí 0` (vanilla MeanFlow) or anneal ő≤ toward 0 over training.
- **A metric-aware ő≤ schedule** that interpolates between these regimes is the paper's headline open direction.

# How this translates to raw audio

The analysis is dimension-agnostic and the variance pathology becomes *more* relevant at audio dimensionality, not less:

- **Higher d strengthens the case for non-zero ő≤.** ImageNet latent `d ‚Čą 4k` (32√ó32√ó4) shows ő≤-insensitivity until `d = 64` in the DGMM sweep; raw 1 s waveform `d ‚Čą 22k` at 22 kHz is **5√ó past the latent and 343√ó past `d = 64`**. The DGMM trajectory at `d = 64 ‚Üí ő≤* = 0.4` suggests the audio interior-ő≤ preference will be *more* pronounced than ImageNet's. **Open empirical question: at waveform d, does the FID-MSE mismatch invert, narrow, or hold?** The paper does not test past `d = 64` on synthetic data or past ImageNet latents on real.
- **The EMA-proxy + FM-anchor recipe is the cheapest practical port.** No separate v-head (vs iMF), no curriculum schedule (vs AlphaFlow), no identity change (vs SplitMeanFlow / SoFlow / TVM), no rectified-flow preprocessing (vs Re-MeanFlow). Drop into any existing MeanFlow waveform training loop with: (a) Polyak EMA of all parameters with `őľ = 0.9999`; (b) one extra forward call `u_őłŐĄ(x_t, t, t)` to compute the tangent; (c) one extra forward call `u_őł(x_t, t ‚ąí őī, t)` for the FM anchor; (d) loss = `L_MF^EMA + őĽ L_FM` with `őĽ ‚Čą 0.1`, `őī ‚ąą [0, 10‚ĀĽ¬≥]`. **~22% wall-clock overhead per step** (TPU v4 number; expect similar on consumer GPUs at 1-D conv backbones).
- **JVP/FlashAttention conflict is not the relevant axis for 1-D conv backbones.** On a CRASH-style 1-D U-Net there's no FlashAttention to break, so the SoFlow/SplitMeanFlow/TiM-DDE motivation does not apply directly ‚ÄĒ the variance-reduction framework is the more relevant lens for waveform training. The relevant axis is *training stability and gradient variance at high d*, exactly what Theorem 2 quantifies. **For Transformer-on-waveform backbones the JVP-elimination methods are still preferable** (FlashAttn compatibility), but the variance-reduction recipe can be combined with DDE inside the EMA-tangent corner (replace the JVP through EMA copy with a central finite difference through EMA copy ‚ÄĒ both are deterministic tangents that drive `ő≤ ‚Üí 1`).
- **The role asymmetry (Proposition 3) is the load-bearing structural insight.** *Replacing the JVP tangent* with a deterministic proxy carries a multiplicative `(t‚ąír)` factor on the bias term ‚ÄĒ pinned to zero at `r = t` by the FM anchor. *Replacing the regression target* with the proxy carries no such factor and produces stationary error equal to the proxy bias `b` independent of `(t, r)`. **For any audio MeanFlow port: keep `v_cond` as the regression target, only replace the tangent.** This is the cleanest "what should I not screw up" instruction the paper provides.
- **No CPU benchmark, no audio.** Both standard image-paper omissions.
- **Composes with the AudioMAE / waveform-MAE direction.** The EMA proxy could be replaced by *any* deterministic marginal-velocity estimator ‚ÄĒ including a frozen audio-FM teacher's velocity field if one is available. Distills variance reduction *and* teacher knowledge in one step.

# Strengths

- **The first theoretical treatment of MeanFlow's empirical pathology** ‚ÄĒ non-decreasing loss and high gradient variance get a single formal explanation (Jacobi-factor amplification + semi-gradient gap from stop-grad).
- **Closed-form `ő≤*`** with two interpretable factors (Stein shrinkage + noise cancellation) ‚ÄĒ replaces empirical őĪ-curriculum tuning with a principled choice.
- **Unification of seven concurrent fixes** under one statistical lens (Table 1). The wiki's MeanFlow family ‚ÄĒ iMF, AlphaFlow, Re-MeanFlow, TVM, Modular MF, Decoupled MF, Kim et al., plus SplitMeanFlow/SoFlow on a separate axis ‚ÄĒ now reads as practical realizations of a single optimization problem.
- **Algorithm 1 is hardware-trivial.** EMA + one extra forward call + a small FM anchor loss. No new identity to verify, no curriculum to tune, no parameterisation change, no architecture change.
- **Direct measurement of the per-step loss variance at DiT scale** (Fig. 4 left, 71√ó gap at `t = 0.9`) ‚ÄĒ the empirical signature is unambiguous. Easy to instrument on any new MeanFlow training run as a stability diagnostic.
- **FID-MSE mismatch is itself a useful contribution.** Most theory papers stop at "we minimize gradient MSE"; this one shows that gradient-MSE optimum and FID optimum can diverge by more than `őĒ FID = 12`, which is a project-relevant warning for any "I tuned by training loss" claim.

# Limitations

- **Scalar-isotropic approximation `J ‚Čą őļ I_d`, `ő£_{v'} ‚Čą ŌÉ¬≤ I_d` for the closed-form `ő≤*`.** Direction-dependent optimum would be `ő≤_i = J_ii / (J_ii + 1) ¬∑ ŌÉ_i¬≤ / (ŌÉ_i¬≤ + b_i¬≤)` per axis; the paper measures a matrix-form `ő≤*_matrix ‚Čą 0.94` that *is* larger than the scalar approximation's `ő≤* ‚ąą [0.46, 0.50]` on DiT ‚ÄĒ so the isotropy is an actual gap, not a cosmetic one. May fail for anisotropic data (waveform has highly anisotropic frequency-band variances).
- **FID-aware loss design is left open.** The paper measures the FID-MSE mismatch but does not derive an FID-aware ő≤ schedule. The "metric-aware ő≤ schedule" they suggest as future work is the most useful follow-up direction.
- **No 1-D experiments, no audio, no CPU benchmark.** ImageNet latent only (Tab. 6) plus 2-D toys.
- **No comparison against the JVP-elimination family** (SplitMeanFlow / SoFlow / TVM not benchmarked on the same DiT-B/4 protocol). Whether `ő≤ = 1` EMA + FM-anchor or `ő≤ = 0` SplitMeanFlow is the better practical fix at audio scale is an empirical open question.
- **Euclidean-only.** Manifold MeanFlow extension (Riemannian MeanFlow [43] in the paper's references) is "straightforward at the gradient level but requires care in defining `ő£_{v'}` on tangent spaces" ‚ÄĒ not derived.
- **DiT-B/4 only at ImageNet scale; 300k steps.** No XL/2 scale, no longer training. Scaling behavior of the FID-MSE mismatch unknown.
- **`őī` hyperparameter is fixed**, not swept. The FM-anchor offset trades off the "thin-neighbourhood vs boundary" stability concern; behavior at `őī = 0` (pure boundary anchor) vs `őī = 10‚ĀĽ¬≥` (interior) not ablated.

# Connection to other ingested papers

- **iMF ([2512.02012](2512.02012.md))** is a Table-1 row: iMF's `V_őł = u_őł + (t‚ąír)¬∑JVP_sg(u_őł; v_őł)` with `v_őł` from a separate velocity head implements `ő≤ ‚Üí 1` via a deterministic v-head proxy. This paper's contribution beyond iMF: (i) **derive `ő≤*` in closed form** and prove it's a unique minimizer; (ii) show the EMA proxy is essentially equivalent to iMF's separate-v-head proxy at near-zero compute cost; (iii) **separate the gradient-MSE-optimal `ő≤` from the FID-optimal `ő≤`** ‚ÄĒ iMF's experiments don't disentangle these, and at converged training their `ő≤ ‚Üí 1` choice trades a higher FID for stability. The FID-MSE mismatch caveats iMF's 1.72 FID claim: that number is at the unbiased corner of one corner of the design space, achieved by stop-grad + adaptive weighting tricks that this paper's framework would relabel as approximate `ő≤ = 0` at low `őļ`.
- **AlphaFlow ([2510.20771](2510.20771.md))** is a Table-1 row: avoiding high-őļ regimes via őĪ-curriculum is the schedule-level realization of "stay in the low-őļ regime where `ő≤ = 0` is approximately optimal". AlphaFlow's gradient-conflict measurement `cos(‚ąáL_TFM, ‚ąáL_TC_c) ‚Čą ‚ąí0.4` is the *phenomenological* version of this paper's *mechanistic* explanation ‚ÄĒ the conflict is between the deterministic mean-field residual gradient (wants `J ‚Üí 0`) and the variance-driven gradient (hidden by stop-grad). The two papers are reading the same pathology from different angles.
- **SplitMeanFlow ([2507.16884](2507.16884.md))** and **SoFlow ([2512.15657](2512.15657.md))** are not in Table 1 ‚ÄĒ they sit on the JVP-elimination axis (algebraic interval-splitting / Taylor FD), which **bypasses rather than solves the variance-reduction problem**. The variance amplification through `J` only exists when the JVP is computed; SplitMeanFlow's three-forward-passes-plus-algebra and SoFlow's two-forward-passes-FD don't have `Tr(J ő£_{v'} J^T)` as a structural variance source. **Implication: SplitMeanFlow/SoFlow and the EMA+anchor recipe attack the same disease through different organ systems.** Direct head-to-head at matched protocol is the empirically-open question.
- **TiM ([2509.04394](2509.04394.md))** is also not in Table 1 ‚ÄĒ its DDE replaces the JVP with central finite difference of `f_őł‚ąí` (EMA copy), which is *both* a JVP-replacement *and* a `ő≤ ‚Üí 1` deterministic-tangent realization. The DDE can be seen as **simultaneously** in the JVP-elimination family (SplitMeanFlow/SoFlow axis) and in the deterministic-tangent family (iMF / Modular MF / Kim et al.). TiM's "2√ó cheaper training at matched FID" is consistent with this paper's variance-reduction prediction for the EMA-tangent corner.
- **MeanFlowSE ([2509.23299](2509.23299.md))** uses Geng's original MeanFlow loss (`ő≤ = 0`) on a small (40.7 M) DiT ‚ÄĒ directly exposed to the variance pathology this paper diagnoses. The reason it trains stably is *plausibly* that the strong WavLM SSL conditioning + frozen WaveVAE latent space jointly drive the backbone toward low-őļ behavior (clean phonetic representations + low-variance latent space). **The EMA-tangent + FM-anchor recipe is a drop-in upgrade to MeanFlowSE** at ~22% wall-clock overhead ‚ÄĒ should reduce gradient variance and potentially improve OVRL / WER at the same NFE.
- **SplitMeanFlow's `s ‚Üí t` limit proof** (algebraic identity becomes MeanFlow identity) and this paper's `ő≤ = 0` (vanilla MeanFlow as corner case) sit at the same boundary in *different* frames: SplitMeanFlow says MeanFlow is the limit of an algebraic identity; this paper says MeanFlow is a corner of a one-parameter variance-bias family. Two complementary "MeanFlow is a special case of X" claims for the wiki.
- **No connection to W-Flow / Drifting** ‚ÄĒ different family entirely (Wasserstein gradient flow rather than fastforward FM). The variance-reduction lens here is specific to JVP-containing objectives.

# Open audio questions

1. **EMA-tangent + FM-anchor on a CRASH-style 1-D conv backbone.** Train iMF-style MeanFlow on a small drum dataset at `ő≤ = 0` (baseline) and `ő≤ = 1` (Algorithm 1 with EMA `őľ = 0.9999`, `őĽ = 0.1`, `őī ‚ąą [0, 10‚ĀĽ¬≥]`). Measure (i) per-step gradient variance and per-step loss variance (the paper's headline diagnostics), (ii) FAD at converged training. **Smallest direct port of the paper's recipe to audio.**
2. **FID-MSE mismatch at waveform dimensionality.** Does FAD (audio analogue of FID) behave the same as FID ‚ÄĒ over-penalise bias relative to gradient MSE? Or does the higher d shift the FAD-optimal ő≤ toward the gradient-MSE-optimal ő≤? Direct measurement on the same `ő≤` sweep.
3. **Anisotropy of `ő£_{v'}` on waveform.** Waveform variance is highly frequency-band-dependent; the scalar-isotropic `ŌÉ¬≤ I_d` approximation is plausibly a worse fit than on ImageNet latents. Estimate `ő£_{v'}` empirically over training; check whether matrix-form `ő≤*_matrix` predicts the empirical optimum better than scalar.
4. **Direction-dependent ő≤.** The paper suggests `ő≤_i = J_ii/(J_ii+1) ¬∑ ŌÉ_i¬≤/(ŌÉ_i¬≤ + b_i¬≤)` per direction. For audio with highly anisotropic frequency content, a per-frequency-band ő≤ (computed in STFT domain) could outperform a scalar ő≤. Single most-natural audio-specific extension of the framework.
5. **Compose with SplitMeanFlow.** Use SplitMeanFlow's algebraic identity to eliminate the JVP, then **separately** apply the EMA-anchor mechanism at the boundary `r = t` to reduce variance in the anchor term. Test whether the two non-redundant variance-reduction mechanisms compose additively.
6. **Compose with AlphaFlow.** Algorithm 1's EMA+FM-anchor at `őĪ = 0` phase replaces the JVP+MeanFlow corner with the variance-reduced version. Combines AlphaFlow's gradient-conflict-aware curriculum with this paper's gradient-variance-reduction recipe.
7. **ő≤-vs-NFE.** Does the FID-MSE mismatch hold at 1-NFE inference, or does it appear only in the converged 1-NFE FID, with multi-NFE behavior different? Open since the paper doesn't test multi-step.
8. **Metric-aware ő≤ schedule.** The paper's headline open direction. For audio, anneal `ő≤` from `őļ/(őļ+1)` early (for stability) to `0` late (for FAD optimum). Empirical sweep: linear vs sigmoid vs cosine anneal, fraction of training to anneal.
