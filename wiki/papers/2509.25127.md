# Score Distillation of Flow Matching Models (SiD-DiT)

## TL;DR (project lens)

This paper closes a real conceptual gap that matters for us: it shows that **Score identity Distillation (SiD)**, a *score* distillation method developed for U-Net diffusion, transfers **out-of-the-box** to pretrained **flow-matching** models with DiT backbones (SANA, SD3-Medium, SD3.5-M/L, FLUX.1-dev), producing **4-step** generators with no teacher finetuning. For our project — distilling a pretrained raw-waveform flow-matching teacher into a 1-NFE student on CPU — the takeaway is:

1. We don't have to re-derive a distillation recipe per teacher parameterisation. The optimal solution under any of `x_0`/ε/v/FM prediction is the *same* conditional expectation `E[x_0 | x_t]`; only the **weight-normalized timestep distribution π(t) = w_t p(t) / ∫ w p** differs in practice.
2. The taxonomy SiD-DiT inherits — **trajectory distillation** (consistency-style, follow teacher's ODE path) vs **score distillation** (match teacher's score field via a Fisher-style divergence, can violate the trajectory) — is the right axis to navigate FM-distillation work. Score distillation is the more flexible side and currently delivers better few-step quality on T2I benchmarks.

It is data-free in its base form; an adversarial variant (`SiD^a_α`) pools DiT spatial features as a discriminator for extra FID gains. No CPU benchmarks given; the project page reports 4-step inference, which is in the right NFE range for our CPU target if we shrink the model.

## Problem and positioning

Flow matching (rectified flow, TrigFlow) was introduced as an alternative to diffusion with the *hope* of straighter ODE trajectories and fewer integration steps. In practice modern T2I FM teachers (SD3, FLUX) still need 20–40 NFE for good quality. The question this paper answers: do diffusion distillation techniques port to FM teachers, or does the FM "no probability density" framing make divergence-based score distillation unsound?

Prior FM distillation work (continuous-time consistency models, SANA-Sprint) **required converting rectified-flow checkpoints into TrigFlow**, then doing trajectory (consistency) distillation. This is invasive and not always applicable.

SiD-DiT: no teacher conversion, no architectural change, single shared codebase across 9 different DiT teachers, 0.6B–12B params, both rectified-flow and TrigFlow.

## Trajectory vs score distillation (the taxonomy)

Fan et al. 2025 (survey) divide pretrained-diffusion distillation into:

- **Trajectory distillation** — student is constrained to follow the teacher's ODE/PF-ODE path. Examples: progressive distillation, consistency models (CM, sCM, LCM), SANA-Sprint (TrigFlow + consistency).
  - Strength: theoretically grounded; student-teacher path identity.
  - Weakness: locks the student to teacher trajectory choices. Sensitive to noise schedule, network preconditioning, requires careful adaptation (e.g. converting RF→TrigFlow).
- **Score distillation** — student is only required to match the teacher's *score field* at sampled (x_t, t), via a Fisher-like divergence between student and teacher score predictions. Can take "shortcuts" through state space.
  - Examples: SiD, Diff-Instruct, DMD/DMD2, VSD/SwiftBrush, ProlificDreamer's VSD.
  - Strength: more flexible, consistently outperforms trajectory distillation on diffusion benchmarks (CIFAR-10, ImageNet).
  - Weakness: stability concerns (DMD's KL flavour was unstable for SANA-Sprint, motivating their switch to consistency); recurring doubt about whether divergence-based objectives make sense for FM, which "doesn't model density".

The paper shows the FM-density objection is misplaced: under Gaussian assumptions, FM has an implicit score `S_{φ*}(x_t, t) = -(x_t + (1-t) v^{FM}_{φ*}(x_t, t)) / t` derivable from the velocity, so Fisher-divergence score distillation is well-defined.

## Unified view of diffusion and FM (the load-bearing derivation)

The paper deliberately avoids SDE/ODE machinery. Instead:

- For Gaussian corruption `x_t = α_t x_0 + σ_t ε`, the score of the marginal is `∇_{x_t} log p(x_t) = -(x_t - α_t E[x_0 | x_t]) / σ_t²` (Tweedie).
- All training targets — `x_0`-prediction, ε-prediction, v-prediction, FM velocity-prediction, TrigFlow — are *linear transformations* of `E[x_0 | x_t]` given `x_t`. So the optimal predictor is the same; the losses differ only in weighting.

Concretely, every loss can be written as
```
L_φ = E_{t~p(t)} E_{x_t~p(x_t)} [ w_t · (α_t²/σ_t²) · L_φ(x_t) ]
    = C_π · E_{t~π(t)} [ (α_t²/σ_t²) · L_φ,t ]
```
where `L_φ(x_t) = E_{x_0|x_t} ‖f_φ(x_t,t) - x_0‖²` and `π(t) = w_t p(t) / ∫ w_t p(t) dt` is the **weight-normalized timestep distribution**. Any claim that one weighting `w_t` is superior without controlling for `p(t)` is misleading — it's the product `π(t)` that controls which timesteps dominate gradient updates.

This is the right mental model for porting an audio-FM distillation result across parameterisations: it doesn't matter whether the teacher predicts velocity, ε, or x_0 — what matters is the timestep distribution induced by `w_t × p(t)` at training and the analogous distribution used during distillation.

## Method (SiD-DiT)

Few-step (4-step) generator `G_θ` with shared parameters across steps:
```
x_g^(k) = G_θ((1 - t_k) sg(x_g^(k-1)) + t_k z_k, t_k, c),  t_k = (1 - (k-1)/4) T
```
with `k = 1..4`, `T = 1000`, `z_k ~ N(0, I)`, `sg` = stop-grad.

Two networks updated in alternation (data-free SiD recipe):
- **Generator `θ`**: minimises a weighted Fisher-divergence-style loss
  ```
  L_θ(x_t^(k)) = w_t · (f_φ(x_t^(k), t_k, c) - f_ψ(x_t^(k), t_k, c))^T (f_φ(x_t^(k), t_k, c) - x_g^(k))
  ```
  with `w_t = 1 - t` by default. `f_φ` is the **frozen teacher**'s implied `x_0`-prediction (linear from its velocity output via `f_φ = x_t - t · v^{FM}_φ`). `f_ψ` is a learned "fake" score / flow-matching network.
- **Fake network `ψ`**: trained with a plain flow-matching loss on the generator's own outputs `x_g^(k)`, providing an up-to-date approximation of the *student's* implied score field. Initialised from `f_φ`.

CFG is applied to both `f_φ` and `f_ψ` (scale 4.5, long-and-short guidance, Zhou et al. 2025b).

**Data-aided variant `SiD^a_α`**: adds a Diffusion-GAN adversarial loss using a discriminator built by pooling DiT features along the spatial dimension after the final norm but before unpatchify — no extra parameters. Initialised from a SiD-distilled checkpoint, then continued with the adversarial term. Pushes FID lower at the cost of needing curated images (MidJourney-v6-llava in their setup) and aligning the student's preferences with that dataset.

### What "out-of-the-box" actually means

- The same SiD code works on rectified-flow SANA (`x_t = (1-t) x_0 + t ε`) and TrigFlow SANA by *only rescaling the time variable t by 1000*.
- For SD3/SD3.5/FLUX (MMDiT backbones) only minimal hyperparameter adjustments needed. Training in BF16 (lr=1e-5, Adam eps=1e-4) is required for memory at SD3.5-Large / FLUX scale.
- For FLUX.1-dev: FLUX uses learned guidance embedding instead of explicit CFG; SiD-DiT keeps its own CFG and just *uses* FLUX as a teacher, which the authors call out as a slight mismatch (modest FID gap on FLUX).

### Architecture / cost

- Two networks live in memory (teacher `φ`, fake `ψ`); generator `θ` is one of them or a separate net (typically the fake net is the same architecture as the teacher).
- Training uses 8× A100/H100 80GB; SD3.5-Large/FLUX need BF16-only and CPU offloading.
- Inference: **4 NFE** for the distilled student (the headline). Each NFE is a full DiT pass at the teacher's resolution.

## Translation to raw audio

The point of reading this paper for us is the **2-step roadmap to 1-NFE-ish audio from a pretrained flow-matching audio teacher**:

1. **Pretrained FM audio teacher** (e.g. a future raw-waveform rectified-flow or stochastic-interpolant model — analogous to CRASH but FM-flavoured). Train normally at 20–50 NFE.
2. **SiD-style distillation in raw waveform**:
   - Implement the SiD-DiT alternating update with `f_φ` = frozen teacher implied `x_0`-prediction (via `f_φ = x_t - t · v^{FM}_φ` from rectified-flow velocity), `f_ψ` = a copy of the teacher trained on student-generated samples, `G_θ` = 4-step (or 1–2-step) generator sharing teacher's architecture.
   - The derivation is parameterisation-agnostic — works for whatever audio teacher we have, including v-prediction or velocity-prediction. We just need `α_t, σ_t` and the implied `x_0`.
   - Timestep distribution `π(t) = w_t p(t) / ∫ w p` is the audio-side knob to tune. The paper uses `Logit-N(ln 2, 1.6²)` for `p(t)` and `w_t = 1 - t`; for audio we may want to bias toward higher noise levels (mirrors EDM's `t < 0.8` restriction).
3. **Compress to 1 NFE**: SiD-DiT's 4-step recipe trivially specialises to 1 step by setting `k_max = 1`. Earlier SiD work on diffusion showed 1-NFE results on ImageNet; the authors choose 4 here for T2I quality, not because the method is structurally 4-step.

Concrete portability checks:

- **Backbone**: SiD-DiT relies on DiT-style transformers; for our 1-D conv-U-Net audio teacher (CRASH-flavoured), the adversarial variant's "pool spatial dimension before unpatchify" trick won't apply directly. We'd need a hook to extract a feature map at an intermediate U-Net stage. The **base** (data-free) SiD-DiT objective is architecture-agnostic — it only needs the teacher's `f_φ` and an evaluable fake-score `f_ψ`.
- **CFG**: audio rarely uses CFG-style classifier-free conditioning unless we have text/class conditioning. For unconditional drum / SFX generation, drop CFG. For text-to-audio, CFG ports as-is.
- **Stability**: the paper explicitly defends Fisher-divergence-based score distillation against the DMD instability concern. Worth re-confirming on a small audio teacher before trusting at scale.
- **Cost**: training requires holding two networks of teacher size. At raw audio scales we are anyway bandwidth-bound; if our teacher fits in 24GB on the 4090, training SiD-DiT-style distillation will need either gradient checkpointing or a smaller fake-network architecture. The paper notes the fake network can be the same architecture as the teacher — for audio we could try a slimmer fake network.

## Strengths

- **Unified theoretical framing.** The Tweedie + Bayes derivation cuts through the ODE/SDE noise. Useful as a teaching reference for the whole project.
- **No teacher finetuning.** SANA-Sprint requires RF→TrigFlow conversion before consistency distillation. SiD-DiT skips that — a real practical win for audio where we'd want to inherit a checkpoint as-is.
- **Single codebase across teachers.** Suggests the recipe is robust to architecture/parameterisation choices, which matters for the audio domain where the "right" parameterisation isn't settled.
- **Data-free option.** No need for paired (text, image) data beyond what the teacher already trained on — directly portable to audio settings where matched paired data is the bottleneck.

## Limitations / open questions

- No CPU benchmark. The 4-step DiT student is still 4 × DiT-forward-pass per sample, which at SD3.5-Large is far from real-time CPU.
- No 1-step ablation in the paper. We'd want to confirm SiD-DiT degrades gracefully to 1 step (prior SiD work on diffusion did, but DiT/FM may behave differently).
- The FLUX gap (FID 27.86) attributed to learned-guidance mismatch is unresolved; suggests SiD-DiT may need teacher-specific guidance handling for some FM models.
- π(t) tuning is acknowledged but not systematically studied. For audio we'd be doing this from scratch.
- The adversarial variant introduces data-curation issues (MidJourney-v6 stylistic bias); for audio the analogous issue is having a high-quality real-audio reference set whose distribution may not match user preferences.
