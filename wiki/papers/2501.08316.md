# Seaweed-APT ŌĆö Diffusion Adversarial Post-Training (ICML 2025)

## TL;DR (project lens)

The **paradigm-defining APT paper**. Starts from a pretrained MMDiT diffusion teacher (Seaweed, billions of params), **drops all distillation / score / trajectory objectives**, and continues training the generator with **pure non-saturating adversarial loss against real data**. Distillation only appears once, as a **deterministic warm-start** for the generator before adversarial training takes over. The thesis: after standard diffusion pretraining (which acts as a giant supervised pretrain), the right *finishing move* for 1-NFE generation is GAN training against real data ŌĆö not chasing the teacher's outputs. Three stability ingredients make this work at multi-billion-parameter scale: (i) **approximated R1 regularization** that sidesteps the second-derivative cost of true R1 and is shown to be critical (without it, training collapses within a few hundred steps); (ii) a **transformer-backbone discriminator initialized from the diffusion model**, with cross-attention logit heads tapped at multiple depths (layers 16, 26, 36) ŌĆö a *depth ensemble* across the discriminator; (iii) a **timestep ensemble** for the discriminator input ŌĆö `D(x,c) := E_{t ~ U(0,T) shifted} D╠é(x_t, c)`, so the disc sees real/fake at many noise levels (s=1 for images, s=12 for videos). Result: 1-NFE 1024 px images at quality comparable to SOTA few-step image distillation, and the **first published 1-NFE 1280├Ś720 24 fps 2-second video**, running in real-time on 8 H100 GPUs (~2 s wall-clock). For our project this is the **template for every "audio APT" follow-up**: pretrain a raw-waveform FM/diffusion teacher, then *finish* it with pure adversarial post-training against real audio ŌĆö no score-distillation loss in the mix. The design vocabulary (R1-approx, timestep-ensemble disc, deterministic warm-start, "post-training" framing) all originates here.

## Problem and positioning

Standard diffusion / FM distillation for few-step generation comes in two flavours:

- **Deterministic / trajectory distillation** (progressive distillation, consistency models, sCM, LCM, rectified flow, SANA-Sprint): student is regressed onto teacher outputs along the ODE path. Easy to train, but **bounded above by the teacher** and the regression target tends to give blurry few-step samples (MSE + reduced Lipschitz).
- **Distributional distillation** (DMD, DMD2, SiD, DiffInstruct, LADD): student is optimised so its score / distribution matches the teacher's. Stronger, but the teacher remains the conceptual ceiling and the gradient pathway still routes through a (frozen) teacher network.

APT's positioning: **forget the teacher as a target, treat the pretrained checkpoint purely as initialisation, and adversarially train against real data**. Conceptually identical to how supervised fine-tuning becomes "post-training" once you stop expecting the SFT teacher's outputs to be the optimum. Closest prior: UFOGen (Xu et al., 2023b) which also did adversarial + real data, but with a DiffusionGAN-corrupted discriminator input, sub-1B convolutional G/D, and the score-distillation term retained. APT removes the corruption, removes the score term, and scales to ~8B transformer G + 8B transformer D.

The contrast with distillation is made explicit in ┬¦1: distillation's quality is **inherently bounded by the teacher**, while APT can *surpass* the teacher on some criteria (the human-eval study in Table 2 shows APT 1-step preferred over its own 25-step diffusion baseline on visual fidelity, +37.2%).

## Method

### 3.1 Setup

Build on a pretrained text-to-video MMDiT (Esser et al., 2024 / Seaweed-7B, Seawead et al., 2025): 36 transformer blocks, ~8B params, flow-matching objective, mixed image+video training in latent space (`x, z Ōłł ŌäØ^{t'├Śh'├Św'├Śc'}`). Same backbone for both generator and discriminator after init.

Standard non-saturating GAN losses:

```
L_D = E_{x,c~T}  [ log Žā(ŌłÆD(x,c)) ]  +  E_{z~N, c~T} [ log Žā(D(G(z,c),c)) ]    (1)
L_G = E_{z~N, c~T} [ log Žā(ŌłÆD(G(z,c),c)) ]                                     (2)
```

### 3.2 Generator ŌĆö deterministic-distillation warm-start, then drop the distillation

Direct adversarial training on the raw diffusion checkpoint collapses. Fix: a **one-time consistency-distillation warm-start** to produce `─£`. Distill with MSE under fixed CFG=7.5 and a fixed negative prompt. `─£` produces blurry but globally coherent 1-step velocity predictions `v╠é = ─£(z, c, T)`, converted to a sample via `x╠é = z ŌłÆ v╠é` (Eq. 4). Initialise the APT generator at:

```
G(z, c) := z ŌłÆ ─£(z, c, T)         (5)
```

Critical detail: **after this init, the consistency / distillation objective is dropped entirely**. All subsequent gradient signal to G comes from the adversarial loss against real data. The distillation step is a warm-start, not a co-trained loss. The generator is also locked to always condition on the final timestep `T` ŌĆö one-step only.

### 3.3 Discriminator ŌĆö initialised from diffusion, transformer, multi-depth multi-timestep ensemble

Three load-bearing design choices:

1. **Backbone = full pretrained diffusion model** (all 36 layers, ~8B params), all params trainable. Half-depth or two-thirds-depth discriminators consistently produced worse quality (Fig. 6). Init from the *original* diffusion weights gives better results than init from the distilled `─£`.

2. **Multi-layer logit heads (depth ensemble).** Cross-attention-only transformer blocks inserted at layers **16, 26, and 36** of the backbone. Each head:
   - Uses a single learnable query token; visual tokens of the corresponding backbone layer act as K/V.
   - Produces a single per-head pooled token.
   - The three head outputs are channel-concatenated, normalised, projected to a scalar logit.
   - Ablation (Fig. 7): multi-layer logits give noticeably better structural integrity than a last-layer-only head.

3. **Timestep ensemble.** Real and fake inputs are *not* given to D at t=0 (which is uninformative because diffusion pretraining objective is degenerate at t=0; the discriminator collapses there). Instead, D's input is corrupted to a *shifted-uniform* noise level:
   ```
   D(x, c) := E_{t ~ shift(U(0,T), s)} D╠é(x_t, c)        (6)
   shift(t, s) := s┬Ęt / (1 + (sŌłÆ1)┬Ęt)                    (7)
   ```
   `s = 1` for images, `s = 12` for videos (heavier shift toward high noise for the latent video model). For training efficiency, only one t is sampled per training sample. This is *not* a DiffusionGAN-style corruption-as-input (the generated `x╠é` is fed cleanly into the network) ŌĆö the discriminator backbone is the diffusion network operating at one of its native input distributions, and the ensemble is over the *timestep parameter* of that network, not over data corruptions. Different stability story than UFOGen / DiffusionGAN.

### 3.4 Approximated R1 regularization (the load-bearing stability fix)

True R1 (Roth et al., 2017; Mescheder et al., 2018) penalises the squared norm of the discriminator gradient on real data:

```
L_R1 = ŌĆ¢Ōłć_x D(x, c)ŌĆ¢┬▓_2                                  (8)
```

This is the standard prescription for GAN convergence near Nash equilibrium. Problem: computing `Ōłć_x D` and then differentiating the R1 loss requires **higher-order gradient computation** ŌĆö and PyTorch FSDP, gradient checkpointing, FlashAttention, Apex fused ops *do not support* double-backward at the time of writing. R1 is therefore effectively unavailable for scaled transformer GANs in current ML stacks.

Paper proposes a **finite-difference R1 approximation**:

```
L_aR1 = ŌĆ¢ D(x, c) ŌłÆ D(N(x, ŽāI), c) ŌĆ¢┬▓_2                  (9)
```

i.e. **the discriminator should produce similar logits for clean real data and a slightly-Gaussian-perturbed version of it**. This is equivalent (in expectation, to leading order in Žā) to penalising `Žā┬▓ŌĆ¢Ōłć_x DŌĆ¢┬▓`, matching the original R1 objective but using *only* first-order grads ŌĆö works inside FSDP, FlashAttention, etc.

Final discriminator loss:

```
L_D = E[log Žā(ŌłÆD(x,c))] + E[log Žā(D(G(z,c),c))] + ╬╗ ┬Ę E[ŌĆ¢D(x,c) ŌłÆ D(N(x,ŽāI),c)ŌĆ¢┬▓]    (10)
```

with **╬╗ = 100, Žā = 0.01 for images, Žā = 0.1 for videos**. Applied at *every* discriminator step.

**Ablation (┬¦5.1, Fig. 5):** without `L_aR1`, the discriminator loss collapses to 0 within a few hundred steps and the generator outputs degenerate to colored plates (mode collapse). With `L_aR1`, the discriminator loss stays bounded above zero and training is stable. This is the most consequential ablation in the paper ŌĆö without R1-approx, the whole method collapses at scale.

### 3.5 Training schedule

Two-stage curriculum.

- **Stage 1 ŌĆö images only at 1024 px.** 128ŌĆō256 H100s, batch size **9062** (large batch is necessary; batch 256 mode-collapses on video, App. E). LR 5e-6 for both G and D. RMSProp (╬▒=0.9; equivalent to Adam ╬▓1=0, ╬▓2=0.9 with reduced memory). No weight decay, no grad clipping. BF16 mixed precision. EMA decay 0.995, EMA peaks at **350 G-updates** then degrades.
- **Stage 2 ŌĆö 1280├Ś720 24 fps videos, clipped to 2 s.** Generator initialised from the image EMA checkpoint; discriminator re-initialised from the diffusion weights. 1024 H100s, batch 2048 (gradient accumulation), LR lowered to 3e-6 for stability. **300 G-updates** total.

So the whole adversarial post-training run is on the order of a few hundred to a thousand generator updates after the warm-start ŌĆö small relative to diffusion pretraining, large relative to typical fine-tuning. Compute budget is dominated by hardware and batch size, not iteration count.

## Architecture summary

- Generator: 36-layer MMDiT, ~8B params, identical architecture to the diffusion teacher; warm-started from the consistency-distilled `─£`.
- Discriminator: 36-layer MMDiT backbone (separate weights, init from teacher), ~8B params, **plus** three cross-attention logit heads at layers 16/26/36. Final logit is a concatenation of pooled per-head outputs.
- **Total trainable parameters during APT Ōēł 16B** ŌĆö one of the largest GANs ever trained, and the largest with a transformer discriminator.
- Latent space (VAE in the loop is the diffusion model's existing VAE; not retrained). Text encoder also frozen.

## Inference cost

Reported only for H100 / GPU (App. C). For a one-step 1280├Ś720 24 fps 2-second video:

| # H100 | Text Encoder | DiT | VAE | Total (s) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0.28 | 2.65 | 3.10 | 6.03 |
| 4 | 0.28 | 0.73 | 1.19 | 2.20 |
| 8 | 0.28 | 0.50 | 1.19 | **1.97** |

So **~2 s wall-clock for 2 s of 720p24 video on 8├ŚH100 = real-time generation**. VAE decode dominates beyond 4 GPUs. On a single H100, 6 s wall-clock for 2 s of video (Ōēł 3├Ś real-time slower) ŌĆö the DiT forward pass is 2.65 s at 1 H100.

**No CPU benchmark.** The model size (8B at inference time) puts it far out of our CPU-real-time target by itself ŌĆö but this is irrelevant for our project; the *recipe* is what we'd port, not the checkpoint.

## Headline results

- **Images (1024 px, 1-NFE, human eval vs SOTA 1-step, Table 2)**: APT preferred over FLUX-Schnell (+35.7% visual fidelity), SD3.5-Large-Turbo (+97.8% VF), SDXL-Turbo (+68.9% VF), beats all 1-step baselines on visual fidelity. Slightly worse than baselines on text alignment (CFG-baked baselines win there). Average preference puts APT 2nd behind FLUX-Schnell absolute, 1st in *relative* preference (controlled for base-model strength).
- **Images, APT 1-NFE vs own 25-NFE diffusion baseline (Table 1)**: APT preferred on visual fidelity (+37.2%), slightly worse on structural integrity (ŌłÆ13.1%) and text align (ŌłÆ8.1%) ŌĆö i.e. APT **beats its own diffusion teacher on visual fidelity** but degrades on structure/alignment.
- **Video (1280├Ś720 24fps, Table 4)**: APT 2-step preferred on VF (+32.3%) vs 25-NFE diffusion, but loses substantially on structural integrity (ŌłÆ31.3%) and text alignment (ŌłÆ9.4%). APT 1-step: +10.4% VF, ŌłÆ38.5% structural integrity.
- **VBench (Table 7)**: APT 1-NFE total score 82.00 vs 25-NFE diffusion 82.15 ŌĆö essentially matched at 1/50 the NFE budget. Consistency-distillation 1-NFE baseline scores 67.05.

The pattern is consistent: **APT trades structural fidelity / text alignment for visual fidelity / realism**. The authors attribute the structural degradation to one-step capacity limits (Fig. 8 ŌĆö generator can't make sharp mode-switches in a single pass), and the text alignment drop to APT learning from the un-CFG'd real data distribution while the diffusion teacher had CFG=7.5 baked in.

## Critical observations

- **R1-approx is the load-bearing stability trick.** Removing it collapses training. Every audio APT follow-up that wants to scale even moderately needs this or an equivalent.
- **The discriminator-as-diffusion-network trick is what makes timestep ensembling natural** ŌĆö the disc backbone was *trained* to consume noised inputs across all t, so feeding noised real/fake at multiple t is in-distribution for the network. A from-scratch discriminator wouldn't get this for free.
- **The "post-training" framing is deliberate.** APT replaces the *fine-tuning* stage that usually does supervised demonstration-following (e.g. instruction tuning, RLHF) with adversarial training against real data. The mental model: diffusion pretraining is the supervised phase; APT is the preference/critic phase. This framing is the contribution that subsequent audio APT papers inherit wholesale.
- **Deterministic distillation appears only as init.** Crucially, this is *not* a distillation+adversarial hybrid in the LADD / DMD2 / SDXL-Lightning sense. Distillation gives a blurry-but-coherent start, then is removed. Subsequent works (DLO, audio APT variants) often re-introduce a distillation auxiliary; APT's clean thesis is that you don't need it.
- **Batch size matters.** Batch 256 mode-collapses on video; batch 1024 doesn't (Fig. 10). This is a real obstacle for small-scale replication.
- **Capacity ceiling shows up as structural degradation.** APT's worst failure mode is sharp-mode-switching scenes (Fig. 8); the authors call out under-capacitation as the root cause. Implication: at smaller scale (our audio runs), structural failures will likely be *worse* than the paper's image/video reports ŌĆö i.e. the per-NFE budget translates to a quality floor that depends on model size.

## How this translates to raw audio

The whole point of reading this paper for our project: **APT is the cleanest recipe for converting a pretrained raw-waveform diffusion / flow-matching teacher into a 1-NFE student**. Mapping:

1. **Teacher.** A pretrained raw-waveform FM (e.g. CRASH-flavoured or a future audio MMDiT-equivalent over STFT coefficients). FM velocity prediction or xŌéü-prediction both fit ŌĆö the APT generator only cares about a 1-step forward, and the post-training stage doesn't touch the FM loss.
2. **Generator warm-start.** A short consistency-distillation pass (or any deterministic distillation ŌĆö RFWave-style equal-straightness Euler with N=1 unrolled, or even a basic MSE-distill at fixed t=T) to get a non-random 1-step audio sample from any noise. The exact distillation doesn't matter ŌĆö its job is to land in a region where the discriminator can give useful gradient. Then drop the distillation loss.
3. **Discriminator initialised from the teacher.** Use the same audio backbone ŌĆö 1D U-Net, Vocos-style ConvNeXt-over-STFT branches, or a hybrid ŌĆö with logit heads tapped at multiple depths. In a Vocos-style multi-branch STFT model the natural depth-ensemble is across branches (low-res, mid-res, high-res STFT scales), which **already matches the audio convention of MPD + MRD** (multi-period + multi-resolution discriminators). The APT depth-ensemble is the latent-conv-tower analog of MPD/MRD.
4. **Timestep ensemble.** Direct port ŌĆö feed real and fake waveforms (or STFT coefficients) into the FM-pretrained discriminator at a noised version `x_t` with `t ~ shift(U(0,T), s)`. The audio s should probably sit somewhere between the image (s=1) and video (s=12) values ŌĆö higher s biases toward more-noised (easier-to-classify) inputs, which is needed when the latent dim is large. Raw audio at 24 kHz over 2 s is `~48k` samples = much higher than a 1024├Ś1024 latent, so a video-style high-s might be appropriate. **Empirical question for our experiments.**
5. **R1 approximation.** Worth implementing even at small scale ŌĆö the second-derivative limitation is *implementation*-level (FlashAttention / FSDP), not scale-level, and the finite-difference approximation is essentially free. Whether it's *critical* at our scale is open: at <500M params training collapse is less aggressive (most 1D U-Net audio diffusion models train without any GAN regularisation), but combining with a transformer-backbone discriminator may re-introduce the collapse mode. **Recommendation: implement L_aR1 by default; tune `╬╗` and `Žā` per experiment.** Cost is one extra discriminator forward pass per step on a perturbed real sample ŌĆö negligible.
6. **Two-stage curriculum.** APT's imageŌåÆvideo curriculum has no direct audio analog. Possible audio analogs: short-clip (0.5 s) ŌåÆ long-clip (2 s) curriculum, or mono ŌåÆ stereo. Not load-bearing for the core method.

**Is R1-approximation portable to our scale?** Yes, and probably useful but not critical for small (<500M) backbones with conv discriminators (HiFi-GAN, MelGAN, Vocos all train without R1). For **transformer discriminators** at any scale, expect collapse without it ŌĆö `L_aR1` is essentially the only first-order way to keep `ŌĆ¢Ōłć_x DŌĆ¢` bounded.

**Is the timestep-ensemble discriminator portable?** Audio already has the **MPD + MRD multi-projection convention** (period-based and resolution-based discriminator banks, c.f. Flow2GAN paper [2512.23278](2512.23278.md)). MPD/MRD ensemble *over data views* (different periods, different STFT resolutions); APT's timestep ensemble is *over noise levels*. They are **orthogonal axes** and likely stack:

- MPD/MRD = ensemble over the data's projection;
- APT timestep ensemble = ensemble over the data's noise level.

A natural audio-APT design is **MPD+MRD discriminators, each consuming a noised input at `t ~ shift(U(0,T), s)`**. Cheaper than running a transformer disc, keeps the convolutional audio-disc convention, and inherits APT's timestep-ensemble stability story.

**The single most important transfer**: APT proves that for a pretrained diffusion/FM checkpoint, **the right finishing move is GAN training against real data, not chasing the teacher**. Score-distillation losses (SiD-DiT, DMD2) are *not* an inevitable part of the few-step recipe; they can be dropped if you have a competent discriminator. For audio, where teachers will be smaller than image teachers and distillation ceilings will bite earlier, this argument is even stronger.

## Strengths

- Clean conceptual thesis: distillation is a warm-start, GAN-against-real is the loss.
- R1 approximation is a practical contribution beyond this paper ŌĆö any large-scale transformer GAN needs it.
- The timestep-ensemble discriminator design is elegant: re-use the diffusion model's natural input distribution rather than fight it.
- Beats own teacher on visual fidelity in human eval ŌĆö strong evidence the "distillation ceiling" is real and APT escapes it.
- First 1-NFE 720p24 video; sets a real bar for the few-step regime.

## Limitations / open questions

- Structural integrity and text alignment regress vs the 25-NFE teacher; APT is preferred on realism but not on correctness. The authors attribute this to one-step-capacity and to CFG-vs-real-data distribution gap. Open question for audio: will 1-NFE audio APT degrade on "structural correctness" (temporal coherence, harmonic structure) in a similar way?
- Compute scale is the main barrier to reproduction. 128ŌĆō1024 H100s, batch 9062 / 2048. Small-scale APT runs (single 4090) need to verify the recipe still works at, say, batch 64 with a 100M-param backbone; the paper's batch-256 mode-collapse result is a real warning sign.
- No CPU number, no FLOPs, no latency analysis ŌĆö H100-centric throughout.
- No discussion of audio at all (obviously) ŌĆö the audio translation is entirely our extrapolation.
- The "post-training" framing is *partly* rhetorical: in practice APT touches every parameter of a 16B-parameter model and trains for hundreds of steps. It is a *short* training run, not a *cheap* one.
- R1-approx requires picking `Žā`. For images Žā=0.01, videos Žā=0.1 ŌĆö i.e. Žā scales with latent dimensionality. For waveform-domain audio Žā will need fresh tuning.
