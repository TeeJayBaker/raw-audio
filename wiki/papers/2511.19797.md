# Terminal Velocity Matching (TVM)

**TL;DR for raw-audio CPU one-step gen.** The eighth published "fix-MeanFlow" recipe in the wiki and **structurally the closest published method to TiM** — both parameterise a two-time-conditioned displacement map `f_θ(x_t, t, s)` between arbitrary timesteps; both reduce to FM at `s = t`. Where they diverge: TiM supervises with an *all-`(t, r)` PF-ODE identity* and replaces the JVP with central finite differences (DDE); **TVM supervises with a *single-point terminal-time identity* and keeps the JVP, but takes the JVP only w.r.t. `s`** — which it argues is the variance-reduction sweet spot because the JVP is then *invariant to* `x_t` and `t`. **Headline image numbers: SOTA from-scratch 1-NFE on ImageNet (256: 3.29 FID; 512: 4.32 FID) on a 678 M DiT-XL/2** with no architecture tricks beyond Lipschitz control via RMSNorm-everywhere, no curriculum, no loss schedule, no multi-particle MMD, no teacher. **First wiki paper to prove an explicit upper bound on `W_2(p_0, model)` for a one-step generator** assuming model Lipschitz continuity — closes the "no distribution-level guarantee" gap that motivated IMM's multi-particle MMD, without the multi-particle cost. **Ships a fused FlashAttention kernel that supports backward through JVP** (the missing piece for JVP-based MeanFlow training on transformer backbones at scale) — `−65 %` peak memory + faster forward+JVP vs naive PyTorch SDPA. Audio relevance is the same as every other DiT-only ImageNet-256 fastforward paper (no audio, no 1-D, no CPU benchmark) — but the *terminal-time JVP-w.r.t.-s-only* construction is the only published JVP-formulation in the family that the variance-reduction paper (2605.09235) reads as **`J = 0` by construction** (the Jacobi factor exists at the regression-target boundary, not in the propagated gradient). This makes TVM the most natural drop-in for a 1-D conv waveform backbone where JVP works but variance amplification is a concern; it's also the only published JVP-based fastforward recipe that should compose with FlashAttention out of the box.

## Why this paper exists (the gap TVM fills)

The MeanFlow family at 2026-05 has six identity-level recipes (Shortcut, MeanFlow, SplitMeanFlow, SoFlow, AlphaFlow, IntMeanFlow), one variance-reduction analysis (2605.09235), one all-`(t, r)` generalisation (TiM), and one MMD-based distributional version (IMM). **None** simultaneously:

1. Provide a 2-Wasserstein bound on `W_2(p_0, model pushforward)`. IMM gives an MMD bound (multi-particle); MeanFlow / iMF / SoFlow / SplitMeanFlow / TiM provide a per-sample regression bound but nothing distribution-level.
2. Train with a single sample per step (vs IMM's `M ≥ 2`).
3. Avoid curriculum (AlphaFlow), loss-schedule (Geng's 75 % heuristic), and architectural tricks (iMF's in-context conditioning, TiM's interval-aware attention).
4. Bake in random-CFG-at-training without collapse.

TVM hits all four with one identity (terminal-velocity condition) + one architectural fix (Lipschitz control via RMSNorm-everywhere) + one optimizer change (Adam β₂ = 0.95) + one engineering deliverable (FlashAttention-JVP kernel with backward).

## Setup and identities

Standard FM: data `x_0 ~ p_0`, prior `x_1 ~ p_1`, linear interpolant `x_t = (1−t)·x_0 + t·x_1`, conditional velocity `v_t = x_1 − x_0`, marginal velocity field `u(x_t, t)`. Define the **flow map** (a.k.a. displacement map) `ψ(x_t, t, s) = x_t + ∫_t^s u(x_r, r) dr` — transports `x_t` along the PF-ODE from start time `t` to end time `s`.

Let `f(x_t, t, s) := ψ(x_t, t, s) − x_t` be the **net displacement**. Two facts (Eq. 3):

1. `f(x_t, t, s) = ∫_t^s u(x_r, r) dr` — by definition.
2. `d/ds f(x_t, t, s) |_{s=t} = u(x_t, t)` — by differentiating (1) w.r.t. `s` at `s = t`.

Parameterise the network as `f_θ(x_t, t, s) = (s − t)·F_θ(x_t, t, s)` (so `f_θ(x_t, t, t) = 0` automatically, like CTM and SoFlow). Define the **instantaneous velocity proxy** `u_θ(x_t, t) := d/ds f_θ(x_t, t, s)|_{s=t} = F_θ(x_t, t, t)`.

**The key identity (terminal velocity condition, Eq. 6 — TVM's signature move).** Differentiating fact (1) under the integral w.r.t. the *upper* limit `s` gives

```
d/ds f(x_t, t, s) = u(ψ(x_t, t, s), s)             (Eq. 6)
```

The left side is differentiation w.r.t. the *terminal* time `s`; the right side is the true marginal velocity *at the terminal point of the trajectory*. This is structurally distinct from every neighbour:

- **MeanFlow's identity** differentiates `d/dt u_θ = v − u_θ` w.r.t. the **start time `t`** of the interval — propagates a JVP through `x_t` and `t`.
- **TiM's State Transition Identity** differentiates the residual `d/dt (B_{t,r}·(α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε − f_{θ,t,r})) = 0` w.r.t. `t` — replaced with central FD over both `t±ε` and `x_{t±ε}`.
- **SplitMeanFlow's algebraic identity** is `(t−r)·u(z_t,r,t) = (s−r)·u(z_s,r,s) + (t−s)·u(z_t,s,t)` — no derivative anywhere.
- **TVM differentiates w.r.t. `s` only.** Because `f_θ`'s only `s`-dependence is through `(s − t)·F_θ(x_t, t, s)`, the JVP propagates *only* through the `s`-coordinate of the network. **`x_t` and `t` are *invariant* under this differentiation.**

This is the variance-reduction angle: the Jacobi factor `J = (t − r)·∂_{x_t} u_θ − I_d` from 2605.09235 lives on the `x_t`-axis. **TVM's JVP has no `x_t`-axis component** — `J = 0` by construction in the propagated gradient. (The paper's own framing in §5 is duality-with-MeanFlow: MeanFlow's `d/dt u = v − u` requires JVP through `u(x_t, t)`, which fluctuates wildly with random CFG; TVM's `df/ds = u(ψ, s)` requires JVP only w.r.t. `s` of `f_θ`, which is bounded.)

**The training loss (Eq. 9).** TVM regresses both the LHS and RHS of Eq. 6 against learned proxies, plus the FM boundary at `s = t`:

```
L^{t,s}_TVM(θ) = E_{x_t, x_s, v_s} [
    ‖d/ds f_θ(x_t, t, s) − u_θ(x_t + f_θ(x_t, t, s), s)‖²    ← satisfies Eq. 6
  + ‖u_θ(x_s, s) − v_s‖²                                      ← FM boundary (satisfies fact 2)
]
```

The first term enforces the terminal-velocity identity; the second is plain FM on the instantaneous-velocity proxy at the (independently sampled) anchor point `x_s`. Both `(x_t, x_s)` are drawn from the same `(x_0, x_1)` pair — only `t, s` differ.

**At training time the bracketed `u_θ` is stop-grad'd (EMA: `u_{θ̄}`)** to prevent collapse (Eq. 12, biased estimator). At `t = s` the first term automatically vanishes (because `f_θ(x_t, t, t) = 0` and the LHS reduces to `u_θ(x_t, t)`; cancels against the RHS), so the loss smoothly reduces to FM there.

## The 2-Wasserstein bound (Theorem 1)

**Given** Lipschitz-continuous `u_θ(·, s)` with constants `L(s)`, the TVM objective upper-bounds the 2-Wasserstein distance:

```
W_2²(f^θ_{t → 0} # p_t, p_0) ≤ ∫_0^t λ[L](s)·L^{t,s}_TVM(θ) ds + C
```

`f^θ_{t → 0} # p_t` is the model-pushforward distribution; `C` is a non-optimizable constant. This is **the only distribution-level guarantee in the MeanFlow family that doesn't require multi-particle MMD estimation** (IMM's bound needs `M ≥ 2` per step; TVM's bound holds with `M = 1`).

**The Lipschitz assumption is non-trivial for DiT.** §4 ("Semi-Lipschitz control") points out that *modern transformers with SDPA + LayerNorm are not Lipschitz continuous*. This is treated as a load-bearing architectural finding: it explains why MeanFlow / iMF / SoFlow training is unstable, not as a TVM-specific limitation but as a baseline pathology that the family has been working around with curricula / variance reduction / target rewrites.

## Semi-Lipschitz architectural fixes (load-bearing)

Three minimal edits make DiT-XL/2 *approximately* Lipschitz (Fig. 4 — without them, activations of the time-embedding layer explode at ~12 k steps):

1. **RMSNorm with learnable scale as QK-Norm** (coincides with Qi et al. 2023's `L_2`-QK-Norm) — replaces dot-product attention's unbounded `q^T k` with the bounded normalized version.
2. **RMSNorm⁻ (RMSNorm without learnable parameters) replacing all LayerNorm** — strictly Lipschitz.
3. **AdaLN rewrite** `AdaLN(x, t) = RMSNorm⁻(x) ⊙ RMSNorm⁻(a(t)) + RMSNorm⁻(b(t))` — applies RMSNorm⁻ to the modulation parameters `a(t), b(t)` themselves, otherwise the scale `a(t)` makes the layer's Lipschitz constant unbounded.

Net effect: **partial control of Lipschitzness is empirically sufficient** for training to stay stable. Cost: ~1 GB extra memory + small runtime overhead vs naive DiT (Fig. 10 — runtime 0.86 → 0.95 s/step on H100, memory 59.5 → 71.4 GB). **This is the most portable trick of the paper** — would stabilise any JVP-based MeanFlow training on a DiT-like backbone, independent of TVM's specific identity.

## Flash Attention JVP backward kernel

The training objective requires `d/ds f_θ = F_θ + (s − t)·∂_s F_θ`. **Computing `∂_s F_θ` is a JVP through SDPA, and is not supported by FlashAttention out of the box** — neither PyTorch's official SDPA nor open-source FlashAttn handles JVP backward efficiently. (This is the same hardware-compat problem that motivated SoFlow's "drop JVP", SplitMeanFlow's "drop JVP", and TiM-DDE's central FD.)

**TVM solves it directly with a fused kernel** (Appx. C) that (i) fuses JVP with the forward pass; (ii) uses significantly less memory than naive PyTorch (Fig. 10: TVM-with-improved-DiT 55.7 GB vs MeanFlow-with-naive-SDPA OOM; **up to 65 % memory reduction**); (iii) **supports backward pass on JVP results**. Detaching the JVP (no second-order grad) drops to 0.69 s/step at 55.7 GB — faster than MeanFlow-with-their-own-kernel (0.81 s) at less memory.

**This is the practical deliverable for the family.** Anyone doing transformer-scale JVP MeanFlow training (MeanFlow original, iMF, AlphaFlow's α=0 phase, EMA-tangent + JVP, TVM) gets FlashAttention compatibility back. **Detach-JVP mode** trades off the second-order gradient (introduces bias) for ~30 % training-time savings — a hyperparameter the paper doesn't ablate quality on.

## Other practical bits

- **Optimizer β₂ = 0.95** (vs default 0.999), per LLaMA practice. JVP introduces higher-order gradients with high fluctuation; faster second-moment update damps them. Fig. 5: terminal-velocity error fluctuation drops dramatically at β₂ = 0.95.
- **Scaled CFG parameterisation** `f_θ(x_t, t, s, c, w) = (s − t)·w·F_θ(x_t, t, s, c, w)` so `u_θ(x_s, s, c, w) = w·F_θ` scales linearly with CFG weight `w`. Combined with `1/w²` loss weighting (Eq. 15) — prevents gradient explosion when `w` is sampled randomly.
- **Random CFG sampling at training without collapse** is presented as a structural advantage over MeanFlow (Fig. 7): TVM's `‖u(x_t, t)‖` stays bounded under random CFG; MeanFlow's gradient norm fluctuates wildly. **Constant CFG `w = 2` works best for 1-NFE FID; `w = 1.75` works best for 4-NFE.** Random CFG gives slightly worse FID overall (limited model capacity), but trains stably without the curriculum tricks Geng's MeanFlow needs.
- **`p(s, t)` joint sampling.** Best scheme is **gap-sampling**: sample `g = t − s` from a logit-normal `(μ_g, σ_g) = (−0.8, 1.0)`, then sample `s` from a logit-normal `(μ_s, σ_s) = (−0.4, 1.0)` truncated to `[0, 1 − g]`. Variant `gap*` uses an independent `(μ_s, σ_s)` distribution for the FM loss term — slight FID improvement (3.44 → 3.36 at XL/2 200 K steps).
- **EMA target rate `γ = 0.99`** (sweet spot — `γ = 0` lags behind; `γ = 0.9999` is overly slow target update and is unstable). Algorithm uses Polyak-averaged stop-grad weights `θ̄^*_{sg}`.
- **No `t = s` sampling probability.** MeanFlow uses 75 % `t = s` (FM boundary); AlphaFlow reduces to 25 %; **TVM uses 0 % and finds higher fractions actively *degrade* 1-NFE FID** (Table 4d: 0 % → 3.72; 30 % → 3.97). The FM term in TVM is supplied by the second loss bracket at `(x_s, s)`, independent of `t`, so no `t = s` sampling is needed.

## Position vs TiM (the most important comparison)

Both TiM (2509.04394) and TVM parameterise a two-time-conditioned displacement/transition map `f_θ(x_t, t, s)` between arbitrary timesteps; both reduce to FM at `s = t`. Reading them as one family clarifies what each adds.

| Axis | TiM (2509.04394) | **TVM (this paper)** |
|---|---|---|
| Identity supervised | All-`(t, r)` State Transition Identity: `d/dt(B_{t,r}·(α̂_t x + σ̂_t ε − f_{θ,t,r})) = 0` (Eq. 8) | **Terminal-time identity**: `d/ds f(x_t, t, s) = u(ψ(x_t, t, s), s)` (Eq. 6) — single boundary, not all-interval |
| Differentiated w.r.t. | `t` (start time) | **`s` (terminal time) only** |
| JVP handling | **Replaced** with central FD over `(t±ε, x_{t±ε})` — DDE | **Kept**; FlashAttention-JVP kernel built to support it |
| Distribution-level guarantee | None proven | **2-Wasserstein upper bound** (Theorem 1) under Lipschitz model |
| Architectural support | Decoupled `(t, Δt)` time embedding + interval-aware attention | RMSNorm-everywhere + QK-Norm for Lipschitz control |
| Curriculum / loss schedule | None | None |
| Boundary handling | `r → t` smoothly reduces to FM | `t = s` smoothly reduces to FM; separate FM term at `(x_s, s)` provides the boundary anchor |
| Headline NFE behaviour | **Monotonic quality 1 → 128 NFE on one checkpoint** (the unique TiM property) | **Non-monotonic across NFE**; separate model/CFG per NFE for headline numbers |
| Headline 1-NFE ImageNet-256 FID | **No XL/2-scale number published** (ablation TiM-B/4, 80 ep: 47.46) | **3.29** (XL/2, ~600+ epochs) |
| Headline 4-NFE | 8-NFE T2I GenEval 0.76 → 128-NFE 0.83 | **1.99 FID ImageNet-256, beats 500-NFE diffusion** |
| FlashAttention/FSDP compat? | **Yes** (DDE has no JVP) | **Yes — via the paper's own custom JVP kernel** (now publicly described, not yet released) |
| Hardware fragility | DDE finite-difference step `ε` (not swept in paper) | RMSNorm-everywhere + custom JVP kernel + β₂ = 0.95 — multi-component |

**Conceptually:** TiM is the *finite-interval-everywhere* generalisation; TVM is the *terminal-point* specialisation. TiM supervises that the transition operator is *consistent across the whole interval*; TVM supervises that the operator *ends correctly* (matches `u` at the terminal time). The terminal-point view is what lets TVM prove the Wasserstein bound — a generator that ends in the right velocity field ends in the right distribution; a generator that's consistent across the interval doesn't immediately give a distribution-level statement.

**For raw audio:** TiM's *monotonic-NFE property* is the headline UX feature; TVM's *single-model 4-NFE beating 500-NFE diffusion* is the headline quality feature. They're on different axes — same parameterisation, different supervision philosophies.

## Position vs MeanFlow (the duality argument)

MeanFlow's identity (in this paper's notation, §5):

```
d/dt f_θ(x_t, t, s) = −u(x_t, t)        ← differentiate w.r.t. start time t
```

TVM's identity (Eq. 6):

```
d/ds f_θ(x_t, t, s) = u(ψ(x_t, t, s), s)  ← differentiate w.r.t. terminal time s
```

**These are formally dual** in `(t, s)`. But two practical differences:

1. **What enters the JVP.** MeanFlow's RHS contains `u(x_t, t)` — depends on `x_t, t`. JVP propagates through `x_t, t`. **Under random CFG, `u(x_t, t)` varies wildly in magnitude**, causing gradient norm fluctuation. TVM's RHS contains `u(ψ, s)` — but `ψ` is *computed by the network* and stop-grad'd in practice; the differentiation is purely w.r.t. `s` of `(s−t)·F_θ`. **Only `s` enters the JVP** — and `s` is one-dimensional and bounded.
2. **Loss bound.** MeanFlow's loss has no published distribution-level bound. TVM's loss bounds `W_2`.

The "JVP w.r.t. `s` only" is the variance-reduction sweet spot at the *identity-construction* level — equivalent to setting `J = 0` in the 2605.09235 framework not by control variate (`β = 1`) and not by elimination (SplitMeanFlow / SoFlow / TiM-DDE) but by **choosing which axis to differentiate**. This is the seventh independent answer to "what about the JVP" — and the only one that *keeps a JVP* while structurally avoiding the variance pathology.

## What TVM doesn't have

- **No CPU benchmark.** Per-step GPU memory + runtime only, on 8×H100 ImageNet-256, batch 256 (training-side, not inference).
- **No GFLOPs/sample at inference.** 1-NFE on DiT-XL/2 is one forward pass at 678 M params; FLOPs comparable to MeanFlow-XL/2.
- **No 1-D / waveform / audio experiments.**
- **No matched-protocol head-to-head against AlphaFlow, iMF, SoFlow, SplitMeanFlow.** Comparison is against MeanFlow (3.43 → 3.29 same protocol) + IMM (8.05 → 3.29) + Shortcut (10.6 → 3.29) + iCT/sCT + diffusion baselines. The 1.72 FID iMF achieves on the same dataset with `v-loss` + flexible-CFG is not in TVM's table; matched-recipe head-to-head is open.
- **Non-monotonic NFE behaviour.** Best 1-NFE model and best 4-NFE model have different CFG (`w = 2.0` vs `w = 1.75`). Headline numbers come from separately trained / retuned models — not one-checkpoint-many-NFE like TiM.
- **Code/weights not released as of arXiv v3.** Custom FlashAttention-JVP kernel implementation is described in Appx. C; no public repo yet linked from the paper.

## How this translates to raw audio

**Operating dimension.** 1 s @ 22 kHz mono is `d ≈ 22 k`; ImageNet-256 latent `d ≈ 4 k`. The Wasserstein bound holds with constants that depend on the Lipschitz constant `L(s)` of the model — does *not* scale obviously with `d` in the statement of Theorem 1. The bound is a per-batch *upper* bound on the squared 2-Wasserstein distance; whether it's *tight* on audio is the empirical question, identical to whether IMM's MMD bound is tight on audio.

**Architecture.** RMSNorm-everywhere + QK-Norm is the most portable trick of the paper — would stabilise *any* JVP-based fastforward training on a transformer audio backbone. On a 1-D conv U-Net (CRASH-style) there is no attention, so QK-Norm doesn't apply; RMSNorm-of-AdaLN-modulation still does and is a cheap edit. Whether 1-D conv backbones need this Lipschitz fix at all is open — the empirical pathology (Fig. 4 activation explosion) is reported on DiT, not 1-D conv.

**The FlashAttention-JVP kernel** is the only thing in the paper that *only* matters for transformer-on-waveform deployments (long sequences, FlashAttn essential for memory). For 1-D conv backbones it's irrelevant: `torch.func.jvp` already works on conv + standard activations.

**Identity port.** The TVM loss is dimension-agnostic. Drop `f_θ(x_t, t, s) = (s − t)·F_θ(x_t, t, s)` onto any waveform backbone; supervise with Eq. 12. **No JVP-hardware blocker on 1-D conv** ⇒ TVM is one of the cheapest MeanFlow-family recipes to test on raw waveform — comparable cost to vanilla MeanFlow (one extra EMA forward + one FM forward at `(x_s, s)`).

**Predict-`x_1` reparameterisation.** The Flow2GAN + WaveFM finding (`v ≈ −x_0` in silent regions; predict `x_1` instead) applies to TVM's `F_θ` output identically — needs the audio-FM reparameterisation. Convert `x_1 → u → F_θ` for the loss.

**CFG handling.** TVM's scaled parameterisation + `1/w²` weighting is conditional-only as stated (unconditional has no CFG to sweep). Drops for unconditional audio gen; matters only for text→audio or class-conditional drum synthesis where CFG matters at inference.

**The natural audio composition surface.** TVM is the **only published JVP-keeping fastforward recipe** that the variance-reduction lens reads as `J = 0` *by construction*. Composes with:

- **Predict-`x_1` + spectral-energy-inverse weighting** (Flow2GAN/WaveFM audio-FM lessons) — orthogonal output-parameterisation choice.
- **EMA-tangent** (2605.09235 Algorithm 1) — TVM already uses EMA targets; doesn't directly stack on the variance-reduction recipe because TVM's JVP doesn't have a tangent to mix.
- **DDE** (TiM) — TVM's JVP is on `s` only, which is one-dimensional; DDE would be a 2-point finite difference in `s` only. Should be even cheaper than TiM's DDE (which perturbs both `t` and `x_t`). Untested.
- **AlphaFlow curriculum** — interpolant from FM (the FM-anchor-only loss term in TVM) to TVM (with the terminal-velocity term active). Same curriculum shape; different α-family parameterisation. Constructable.

## Strengths and limitations

**Strengths:**
- **Distribution-level guarantee at single-sample-per-step cost** (Theorem 1) — closes IMM's MMD-vs-MeanFlow gap.
- **SOTA from-scratch 1-NFE FID** on ImageNet-256 and 512 at vanilla DiT-XL/2.
- **Cleanest matched-protocol head-to-head improvement over MeanFlow** in the family (3.43 → 3.29 at same model size, same architecture choice — no curriculum, no v-loss).
- **JVP variance-reduction by construction** (differentiate `s` only) — no control variate, no tangent replacement, no curriculum.
- **FlashAttention-JVP kernel** is a one-time-engineering contribution that unblocks every JVP-based fastforward at transformer scale, not just TVM. The fact that detach-JVP mode is *faster than MeanFlow-with-its-own-kernel* (0.69 s vs 0.81 s) suggests this kernel will get adopted across the family.
- **Random-CFG-at-training without collapse** — eliminates a hyperparameter (no CFG curriculum needed).

**Limitations / open issues:**
- **Lipschitz assumption requires architectural fixes** (RMSNorm-everywhere) that empirically work but are not theoretically guaranteed sufficient. The paper is honest that "partial control" works in practice.
- **Non-monotonic NFE quality.** Different CFG / model per NFE for headline numbers. Worse user-side property than TiM.
- **No matched-protocol comparison against AlphaFlow / iMF / SoFlow / SplitMeanFlow.** The closest neighbours in the family aren't in the table.
- **No CPU benchmark, no GFLOPs/sample, no 1-D / waveform / audio experiment.** Same as every DiT-only ImageNet paper.
- **Custom FlashAttention-JVP kernel not yet released.** The headline engineering deliverable is described but not publicly available; replication requires re-implementation.
- **Detach-JVP mode trades bias for ~30 % training-time savings**, but no quality ablation on this.

## Open questions for raw audio

- **Does the RMSNorm-everywhere Lipschitz fix matter on 1-D conv U-Net backbones?** The activation-explosion pathology (Fig. 4) is reported only on DiT; whether CRASH-style 1-D conv exhibits the same instability under JVP training is unmeasured. Cheap test.
- **Does the terminal-velocity identity (Eq. 6) converge on raw audio?** Same as the SplitMeanFlow / SoFlow / AlphaFlow audio-port questions. Smallest test: TVM-loss on CRASH-backbone, predict-`x_1`-reparameterised, no CFG conditioning, drum dataset, 1-NFE FAD vs vanilla MeanFlow at matched compute.
- **Does the 2-Wasserstein bound translate to a useful per-sample audio quality predictor?** The bound is global (full-batch); whether per-sample TVM-loss correlates with per-sample audio FAD is open.
- **`d/ds`-only-JVP variance behaviour at audio dimensionality.** The structural argument (`J = 0` by construction in the propagated gradient) doesn't depend on `d`. But the empirical gradient-norm advantage over MeanFlow at random CFG (Fig. 7) was measured on DiT-B/4. At `d ≈ 22 k` 1-D conv, the comparable metric is gradient variance across `t ∈ [0, 1]` for random-CFG training — should be flat for TVM, growing toward `t = 1` for MeanFlow per the 2605.09235 measurement.
- **Single-`s` vs multi-`s` Monte Carlo.** TVM samples one `(t, s)` per training step. SplitMeanFlow has the analogous question for its `λ ~ U(0, 1)` splitting point. Multi-`s` would reduce estimator variance at higher forward-pass cost; untested.
- **DDE-over-`s`-only composition.** TVM's JVP is one-dimensional (in `s`). A central finite difference `(f_θ(x_t, t, s+ε) − f_θ(x_t, t, s−ε))/(2ε)` would be 2 forward passes vs 1 JVP, and is FlashAttention-compatible without the custom kernel. Cheap to construct; untested. If this works, the custom JVP kernel becomes optional.
- **TVM + AlphaFlow curriculum.** Anneal from FM-anchor-only (the second loss bracket) to full TVM (both brackets active). Different curriculum interpretation than AlphaFlow's α-family but structurally similar. Untested.
