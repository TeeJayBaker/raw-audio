Adapting FD-loss to audio: why the naive port fails on drum one-shots, and what to change

2026-06-10 · grounded in runs/fd-finetune (PANN + AudioMAE + CLAP critics over the stft-b64-200k base) and the FD-loss paper (arXiv 2604.28190) · covers the three observed symptoms, the audio-specific theory, a revised recipe, and the capacity-at-fixed-latency question.

TL;DR

The FD-loss machinery in losses/dist.py is a faithful port (EMA-decoupled moments, self-normalised multi-critic aggregation, precomputed Σr½). What failed is the measurement: all three critics factor through log-mel magnitude, are blind above 14 kHz (8 kHz for AudioMAE), and pool a 100 ms–2 s event into one vector sized for 10 s clips. FD-loss optimises the generator into that joint null space — phase coherence is exactly where a 1-NFE STFT-domain model is weakest, so the 93.75 Hz frame-rate buzz is the predictable result, not a fluke. On top of that, the statistics were far off the paper's operating point (gradient batch 24 vs 1024, no warm-start, and — now fixed — 1,000 reference embeddings for a 2048-dim critic). The fix is not to abandon FD-loss; it is (1) a critic ensemble that spans waveform-domain features (EnCodec frames, MERT) plus one semantic mel critic (CLAP), (2) weak per-sample anchors so the marginal-matching loss cannot trade away identity or STFT consistency, and (3) paper-faithful statistics (warm-start, grad-accum, full-population reference).

SECTION 1Where the recipe stands

The base. fm-oneshots-mars-stft-b64-200k: rectified flow on raw waveform, x-prediction with v-space MSE, the backbone a d512 / 12-layer transformer (54.6 M) operating on complex-STFT frames (n_fft 2048, hop 512, win 2048 @ 48 kHz; real/imag channelised to 2 × 1025 = 2050 channels per frame, one token per frame). Noise is added in the waveform domain; since the STFT is linear this is equivalent to noising in STFT space with a correlated complex Gaussian, so the formulation is sound. The model returns a complex STFT and ISTFTs it inside forward, so the pretraining v-MSE was computed on waveforms — a detail that matters in §3.1. Plateaued around 100k steps; the 100k checkpoint is the FD init.

The post-train. FDTrainer reproduces the paper's shape: 1-NFE generation (one Euler step from t≈0, i.e. the model's x-prediction from pure noise), peak-norm, then Fréchet distance to precomputed real moments in each frozen critic space, with the FDi / sg(FDi+c) self-normalisation and EMA-decoupled generated moments. Conditioning (MATPAC) is kept; guidance is dropped from the base's 3.5 to 1.0, matching the paper's CFG-free path.

Observed. Perceptual quality degrades over FD post-training; some examples change identity entirely; a strong periodic artifact appears at 93.75 Hz — which is exactly 48,000 / 512, the frame rate of the generator's own STFT hop. And the original run estimated the real moments from only 1,000 clips (since fixed to the full ~122k corpus).

SECTION 2What the paper assumes that audio breaks

FD-loss works in images because the critic ensemble's null space — the set of generator changes the loss cannot see — happens to contain almost nothing perceptually salient. Six different networks consuming raw pixels have only learned invariances, and they disagree about them, so the intersection is small. The audio port violates this on four independent axes.

2.1 Phase invariance is structural, not learned

PANN, AudioMAE and CLAP-HTSAT all compute a log-mel magnitude spectrogram as their first layer. Phase is discarded before any weights are applied. This is not an invariance the ensemble happened to learn — it is hard-wired into all three critics identically, so ensembling does not shrink it. Everything that lives in per-bin phase is invisible: transient sharpness, inter-frame phase coherence (= STFT consistency, §3.1), comb/dispersion artifacts. For percussion, which is transient-dominated, the perceptually critical axis is almost entirely inside the joint null space. Images have no analogue: there is no canonical lossy transform that every image network applies before its first conv.

2.2 The ensemble is band-limited far below the generator

The generator works at 48 kHz (content to 24 kHz). The critics resample down and cut off their mel banks well below that: AudioMAE sees ≤8 kHz (16 kHz input), PANN and CLAP ≤14 kHz (fmax 14000). The 14–24 kHz octave — cymbal and hi-hat sizzle, attack air — is completely unconstrained by the FD objective, and 8–14 kHz is covered by only two of three critics. During pretraining the waveform-domain v-MSE constrained the full band; FD post-training removed that constraint and replaced it with nothing above 14 kHz.

2.3 Ten-second critics, tenth-of-a-second events

All three critics were trained on ~10 s AudioSet-style clips, and the wrappers make a one-shot fit that mould: AudioMAE zero-pads its fbank to 1024 frames (10.24 s) and mean-pools over all patch tokens — a 24,000-sample hit at 16 kHz occupies ~150 of 1024 frames, so ~85% of the pooled embedding is padding response; CLAP repeat-pads to 480,000 samples, i.e. tiles a kick into a 10 s loop; PANN handles native lengths (with a 10,560-sample floor) but global-pools over time internally. In every case the fine temporal structure that distinguishes a good transient from a smeared one is averaged into a few coordinates of a clip-level vector. The image analogue would be evaluating 32×32 thumbnails embedded in a 512×512 grey canvas — the metric mostly measures the canvas.

2.4 The statistics were off the paper's operating point

  • Reference side (was). 1,000 embeddings for a 2048-dim critic (PANN) gives a rank-deficient Σ̂r; FAD's small-sample bias scales like O(D/n) (the motivation for FAD∞, Gui et al. ICASSP 2024). The optimisation target itself was wrong. Fixed — moments now come from the full ~122k corpus.
  • Generated side. EMA with β=0.999 holds an effective population of roughly 1/(1−β) × B ≈ 24k exponentially-weighted samples — borderline for D=2048 — and it was initialised from the first batch of 24, so early FD values and gradients tracked a fantasy distribution for the first thousands of steps. The paper warm-starts from 50k pre-generated samples.
  • Gradient batch. 24 vs the paper's 1024. The gradient flows through the current batch's contribution to μ and Σ; at B=24 in 512–2048-dim spaces this is extremely noisy. A noisy distribution-level reward behaves like a drifting target — it pushes the generator around without pinning it anywhere, which compounds the identity drift of §3.2.
Gaussian assumption, noted for later The drum one-shot manifold is strongly multimodal in any semantic space (kicks / snares / hats / cymbals form separated clusters), and CLAP embeddings are L2-normalised onto a sphere. Matching first and second moments of a Gaussian is a crude summary of that geometry. The paper inherits the same assumption and works anyway, so this is not the first-order problem — but it is the reason KAD-style MMD (unbiased, distribution-free, small-batch-friendly; arXiv 2502.15602) is queued as an experiment rather than dismissed, and why per-class (bucketed) moments are a cheap option if folder labels are usable.

SECTION 3The three symptoms, mechanistically

3.1 The 93.75 Hz artifact is OLA inconsistency at the frame rate — and FD-loss unleashed it

The generator emits one complex frame per token and the frames overlap 4× (hop 512, win 2048). A set of overlapping complex frames is consistent — i.e. is actually the STFT of some waveform — only if the overlapping regions agree. Nothing in the architecture enforces that; torch.istft resolves disagreements by least-squares overlap-add. The residual disagreement shows up as hop-periodic amplitude modulation: a buzz at exactly 48,000/512 = 93.75 Hz, with sidebands at ±93.75 Hz around every partial. Calling it "aliasing" undersells how specific it is — it is the signature of inconsistent STFT frames, the same artifact family Griffin-Lim-style consistency projections exist to fix.

Why now and not during pretraining? Because pretraining computed its loss on the waveform after ISTFT — inconsistent frames produce waveform error, so consistency was implicitly trained in. FD post-training deleted that term and replaced it with critics that cannot see the buzz (magnitude front-ends, §2.1; temporal pooling, §2.3; and the sidebands sit partially inside mel-bin bandwidths). Worse, the 1-NFE operating point is precisely where phase commitment is weakest: the model's x-prediction from pure noise is a conditional mean, and averaging over phase is what conditional means do. FD-loss then sharpened the magnitude statistics the critics report while leaving phase free to satisfy them in the cheapest way — which is incoherently.

3.2 Identity changes: FD is a marginal loss, and the safety rails were removed

The Fréchet distance compares unpaired distributions. Nothing in the objective says "the sample generated for condi should sound like clipi" — only that the cloud of generated embeddings should match the real cloud. In the paper this doesn't bite because the base model's class adherence is strong and the FD gradient (at LR 1e-6–1e-5, B=1024) has no incentive to re-shuffle the conditional mapping. Here three things compound:

  • The base's conditional adherence is modest to begin with (the capacity-sweep probes put sampled identity at CLAP-own ≈ 0.6, retrieval ≈ 27% in the overfit regime).
  • The FD run generates and logs at guidance 1.0 while every base sample previously listened to was at guidance 3.5 — part of the perceived "examples completely changed" is the guidance swap, independent of FD. Any before/after comparison must hold guidance fixed.
  • At B=24 the moment gradient is noise-dominated (§2.4), and a noisy distribution-matching force applied to a weakly-pinned conditional mapping performs a random walk on identity.

3.3 Overall degradation: optimising into the null space, toward a (then-)wrong target

Combine §2 and the above: the loss rewarded moving mel-magnitude moments toward a reference that (until the fix) was itself biased rank-deficient junk, with gradients sampled at 1/43rd the paper's batch, while everything perceptually load-bearing for drums — transient phase, the top octave, fine temporal envelope — was free to degrade. The surprise would have been the run working.

SECTION 4FD-v2: the adapted recipe

4.1 Critic ensemble: span the null space, don't triple-cover one subspace

The paper's lesson is that the set of representations matters more than any single one. The audio translation is not "find three audio nets" but "find nets whose front-ends disagree". Proposed training set:

CriticFront-endBandFrames/sDimRole
CLAP (music HTSAT) — keeplog-mel magnitude50 Hz–14 kHz100512semantics / timbre classes (SigLIP-analog)
EnCodec-48k encoder — addraw waveform convs0–24 kHz150128phase, transients, full band, codec-grade detail
MERT-95M — addraw waveform convs @24 kHz0–12 kHz75768music-SSL mid-level structure with a phase-sensitive front-end
PANN, AudioMAE — demote to eval-onlylog-mel magnitude≤14k / ≤8k1002048/768held-out metrics (Inception/MAE analogs), §4.4

Two upgrades to how features become distributions:

  • Frame-level FD for EnCodec (and optionally MERT). Mean-pooling EnCodec's [B, 128, T′] latents to 128-d throws away exactly the temporal structure we added it for. Treat frames as the population instead: a batch of 24 one-second clips yields ~3,600 samples in 128-d — a far better-conditioned moment estimate than 24 samples in 2048-d, and it makes the loss sensitive to local texture (the buzz is flagrant at frame level). This is the audio analogue of computing FD over patch tokens. Cheap variant if memory bites: subsample frames per clip.
  • Two-moment pooling where clip-level vectors are kept (concat mean and std over time) so a steady low-level artifact at least perturbs the std coordinates instead of vanishing into the mean.
Why not MATPAC as a critic It is the conditioner and the identity-retrieval eval space. Training the generator to match moments in the same space we use to measure conditioning is Goodhart by construction — improvements would be unfalsifiable. Keep MATPAC for conditioning and eval only. (A paired MATPAC anchor is different — §4.2 — because it is per-sample supervision toward the given cond, not distribution matching in the eval space; report it as a training term, never as evidence.)

4.2 Anchors: weak per-sample rails so the marginal loss can't pay in identity or consistency

FD-loss stays the lead objective; the anchors below are small-weight insurance, each targeting one diagnosed failure. All are available in training_step already (the real batch and its conds are in scope):

  1. RF keep-alive (λ ≈ 0.05–0.2): keep computing the pretraining v-MSE on the same batch. This is the cheapest full-band, phase-sensitive, paired constraint — it is exactly the term whose removal let the buzz in — and the paper frames FD-loss as complementary to existing recipes. It does reintroduce a regression-to-mean force, hence the small weight: enough to forbid leaving the base's waveform manifold, not enough to fight FD's sharpening.
  2. MSS log-magnitude anchor (alternative or complement to 1): mr_stft_loss with log_weight > 0 between the 1-NFE output for condi and clipi. Log-magnitude, multi-resolution, full-band; per the project's own read of PeriodWave-Turbo, avoid raw complex/linear STFT L1 (phase-error gradients mislead) — the implemented spectral-convergence + log-mag form is the right one.
  3. Cond-cycle anchor (λ small): cosine(MATPAC(G(z, c)), c). Directly pins identity without referencing the paired waveform, so it tolerates legitimate within-cond variation better than (2). MATPAC's embed() is not wrapped in no_grad, so the graph is available.
  4. STFT-consistency penalty (targeted buzz killer): ‖S − STFT(ISTFT(S))‖ on the predicted complex frames — unsupervised, one extra STFT, zero inference cost. Necessary-not-sufficient (consistency ≠ quality), but it removes the §3.1 mechanism at the source rather than asking critics to detect its symptom.

Recommended starting combination: (1) or (2) + (3), with (4) held in reserve if the buzz survives the critic swap.

4.3 Statistics: run the paper's operating point, not a 1/43-scale sketch

  • Warm-start the generated moments: sample ~25–50k clips from the frozen init checkpoint once, initialise each MomentEstimator's m1/m2 from them (paper-faithful; removes the first-batch fantasy period).
  • Grad-accum ≥ 8. In EMA mode the population moments barely move within an optimiser step, so accumulated micro-batches approximate one large gradient batch — effective B ≈ 192–768 is reachable on the same 24 GB card. This is the single cheapest fidelity gain to the paper.
  • Critic round-robin if VRAM blocks batch growth (MATPAC + 3 critics co-resident is what capped the micro-batch at ~30): sample one or two critics per step. The self-normalised sum makes the expected gradient the same; each critic's EMA just updates on its own steps. Spend the freed memory on batch.
  • Reference moments: full corpus (done). For 2048-dim eval critics, prefer KAD for reporting regardless, since it is unbiased at small n.
  • Hold guidance fixed across comparisons (base vs FD, both at 1.0 or both at 3.5), or the listening test measures CFG, not FD.

4.4 Evaluation: held-out spaces or it didn't happen

Anything used as a training critic is disqualified as a metric. Dashboard:

  • KAD on held-out embedders (PANN, AudioMAE, VGGish) — unbiased, Gaussian-free.
  • MATPAC retrieval / own-cosine — the identity-drift detector.
  • Frame-rate line ratio — a purpose-built artifact meter: PSD of the energy envelope, energy in 93.75 ± 2 Hz over the neighbouring band. Cheap, quantitative, directly tracks the §3.1 mechanism through training. Lives in experiments/ first.
  • genRMS and per-band (0–8k / 8–14k / 14–24k) energy vs reals — catches both energy collapse and "free octave" garbage.
  • Listening AB at matched guidance — the only ceiling-free metric; FD-v6-style critic stacks still need human ears (the paper's own human study is the final word there too).

SECTION 5Capacity levers at fixed latency

The latency budget is defined by CPU single-shot inference (project headline; the wiki's calibrated points: Vocos-class hop-rate stacks run ≫ real-time on CPU, sample-rate stacks mostly don't). The backbone already sits in the right family — hop-rate tokens, 1-NFE endgame. Sequence lengths are tiny: ≈94 tokens per 48k-second (hop 512), 188 for the 2 s cap. Attention is a rounding error; GEMM width is everything. That shapes the levers:

Two structural observations worth keeping:

  • The token-width squeeze is real but borderline, and the probe agrees. Each frame is 2050 reals squeezed through a d512 projection. At 4× overlap, only ~512 new samples arrive per hop, so 512 dims roughly matches the information rate — which is why d512 works at all and why d768 helps without transforming: the model needs width beyond pass-through to carry signal + noise + computation simultaneously. Width (or frequency-banding, which attacks the same squeeze sideways) is the principled first lever, not depth.
  • The loss currently weights bins by linear power. Waveform/v-space MSE ≡ (Parseval) linear-spectral MSE: a kick fundamental at −6 dBFS outweighs a hi-hat's 12 kHz sizzle at −40 dBFS by three orders of magnitude, while perception is roughly log in both axes. Compressed-STFT data space and/or the MSS-log auxiliary both push supervision toward perceptual weighting; this is the likeliest cheap win for "dull hats" symptoms independent of FD work.

SECTION 6The 100k plateau in this light

The capacity sweep separated the plateau into: fitting (teacher-forced) — unsaturated, scales with capacity; and sampled quality — capacity-independent wall (energy collapse, identity ≈ 0.6 CLAP-own), i.e. a sampling-dynamics problem. The earlier conclusion was "next levers: CFG sweep, maybe reflow." The FD endgame reframes this: 1-NFE post-training operates at the deployment point and sidesteps the multi-step trajectory entirely — compounding ODE error and guidance scheduling stop being the binding constraint; distribution sharpening at t≈0 (exactly what FD-loss does, exactly what collapses the conditional-mean blur) becomes it. The paper's JiT-L result (1-step FID 291→0.77) is direct evidence that a weak 1-step base plus a correct distributional critic is sufficient.

So: deprioritise multi-step sampler surgery. Spend the base-model budget on what transfers to the 1-NFE endgame — width (d768), log-domain auxiliaries, possibly compressed STFT space — and spend the recipe budget on FD-v2. The 25-step path remains a useful diagnostic and a fallback product, nothing more.

SECTION 8Risks, fallbacks, open questions

  • Scale is the biggest unmatched factor. 122k clips and effective-B in the hundreds vs the paper's 1.28M and 1024. Distribution losses are statistics-hungry; frame-level FD partially compensates by multiplying the sample count, but if FD-v2 stalls, suspect the regime before the concept.
  • Audio has no DINOv2. EnCodec latents are compression-oriented (low-level), MERT is mid-level music structure, CLAP is coarse semantics — individually each is weaker than the image critics. The bet is that the union spans enough. If it doesn't, the proven audio alternative is paradigm-C post-training: Flow2GAN-style MPD/MRD adversarial fine-tune — per-sample discriminators are the artifact-killers vocoders rely on, and an MPD period set including the hop (512) would see the buzz directly. That is the wiki's recommended track anyway; FD-v2 and it are not mutually exclusive (FD for distribution, a light discriminator for texture).
  • Anchors vs sharpening tension. Every paired anchor reintroduces a regression-to-mean force. If outputs re-blur, the anchor weight is too high — the diagnostic is genRMS and the teacher-forced/sampled gap, not the FD value.
  • Goodhart, permanently. Each critic promoted into training deletes a metric. The held-out set (PANN, AudioMAE, VGGish, MATPAC-retrieval) must stay held out, and the final arbiter for "is it better" remains ears at matched guidance.
  • Open: does frame-level FD need a minimum frame count per clip to be stable for 100 ms hits (≈15 EnCodec frames)? Does the cond-cycle anchor alone fix identity without any waveform anchor? Is compressed-STFT flow space worth the retrain on its own, independent of FD?
Bottom line

FD-loss is the right idea applied through the wrong instruments at the wrong operating point. The 93.75 Hz buzz, the identity drift, and the overall degradation are all predictable consequences of three magnitude-mel critics + B=24 + (formerly) 1,000-sample reference moments. The concept deserves a second run on audio-native terms: phase-sensitive critics with frame-level statistics, weak per-sample rails, paper-faithful estimation — measured only in spaces the loss never touched.
