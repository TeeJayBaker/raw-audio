Backbone & protocol: why the STFT-frame transformer is the right shape, and where the recipe still leaks

2026-06-10 · the three candidates (WaveNeXt/Vocos ConvNeXt · raw-patch transformer · STFT-frame transformer) compared at the measured iso-latency point (≈30 ms per generated second, M1 CPU, batch 1) · plus an audit of the noise-in-waveform→STFT denoising protocol and the RF/MF question. Companion to the FD-loss adaptation report.

TL;DR

The STFT-frame transformer is the right choice, and not by accident. At fixed latency, token rate buys parameters: hop-rate tokens give you a ~54 M model where 64-sample patches give you ~7 M — and your own patch-512 run is the controlled experiment proving the win comes from the basis, not the token rate (identical geometry and FLOPs, retrieval 2/8 vs 7–8/8). The STFT basis is the correct inductive bias for a denoiser for three deep reasons: the shift theorem turns timing into smooth phase rotations, the fixed iSTFT oscillator bank absorbs the part of the task networks are worst at, and 4× overlap is free test-time ensembling. The vocoder-shape ConvNeXt is not wrong so much as mis-tasked: it excels when dense aligned conditioning (mel) pins the structure; with one global embedding the generation burden is global coordination, which its ±27-frame receptive field cannot do for a 188-frame clip and which attention does in one hop — at ≤400 tokens attention is essentially free, so the ConvNeXt's only edge (kernel simplicity) doesn't pay here. The noising protocol itself is sound — noising the waveform before the STFT is actually the better variant, keeping every model input on the consistent-spectrogram manifold. The genuine leaks are loss-weighting ones: linear-power (Parseval) weighting starves quiet high-frequency content, and the logit-normal(0,1) t-distribution under-trains the t≈0 region that is the only operating point of the 1-NFE endgame. Evolve within this family (width → frequency-banded tokens → hop 256 → compressed coefficients; MeanFlow as the protocol successor if the RF base's conditional-mean blur caps FD post-training) rather than switching families.

SECTION 1Iso-latency accounting: token rate buys parameters

For a transformer at these sequence lengths, FLOPs per generated second ≈ 2 · params · tokens/s (attention's quadratic term is <1% below ~400 tokens). Your measured anchor — d512/L12 (54.6 M) at ~94 tokens/s ≈ 10 GFLOP per generated second ≈ 30 ms on M1 — fixes the budget. At that budget the three candidates buy very different models:

SECTION 2Why the STFT basis is the right inductive bias for a denoiser

2.1 The shift theorem linearises timing

A denoiser conditioned on a global embedding must commit to when things happen while marginalising over micro-alignment under noise. In the STFT basis, delaying content by τ multiplies bin k by e−jωkτ — a smooth, low-dimensional, learnable transformation of the coefficients. In a raw-patch basis, a delay of even a few samples decorrelates every high-frequency coordinate of the patch — nearly a permutation. The raw-patch model must spend capacity building shift-equivariant machinery that the STFT provides analytically. This is the most economical explanation for the 2/8-vs-7/8 retrieval split at identical FLOPs.

2.2 The fixed oscillator bank absorbs what networks do worst

Neural networks have a documented low-frequency spectral bias; synthesising a clean 12 kHz carrier with an MLP/conv output layer is exactly the failure mode of learned-upsampler vocoders (buzz, aliasing — the reason BigVGAN needed anti-aliased nonlinearities and Vocos/WaveNeXt simply route output through an iSTFT). In the STFT-frame backbone the network only predicts coefficients that vary at the frame rate (93.75 Hz); the 24 kHz-bandwidth oscillation is manufactured by the fixed iSTFT basis. The network's function class is matched to envelopes, not carriers.

2.3 Overlap is free test-time ensembling (with a known tax)

At 75% overlap every output sample is the (least-squares) average of 4 frame predictions. Incoherent per-frame error components attenuate ~6 dB for free. The tax is the consistency failure mode analysed in the FD report: systematic disagreement between overlapping frames becomes hop-periodic modulation (93.75 Hz). The tax is manageable (waveform-domain anchors, consistency penalty, hop 256); the ensembling is structural.

2.4 Locally, drums are sparse in this basis

Within a 2048-sample window a one-shot is a handful of partials plus shaped noise — a compressible, quasi-stationary spectrum. MSE regression onto sparse structured targets is a far easier conditional-mean problem than regression onto dense oscillatory raw samples. The same property is why the conditioner (MATPAC) and every audio SSL model also start from a time-frequency front-end.

Known costs of the basis (all previously catalogued, none disqualifying): no weight sharing across frequency and a 2050→512 per-frame squeeze (fix: width, then frequency-banded tokens); linear-power loss weighting (§4.2); OLA consistency (§2.3).

SECTION 3The other two candidates, honestly

3.1 Raw-patch transformer (WavFlow shape): wrong side of the iso-latency trade, worst-case for drums

WavFlow's result — high-quality raw-audio FM with 64-sample frames — is real but lives at a model scale the latency budget excludes: at 750 tokens/s the 30 ms budget buys ~7 M parameters, an order of magnitude below your current backbone, and WavFlow's own guidance (frame size ≤200, quality from scale) points the same direction. Two structural problems compound at small scale:

  • Low-frequency resolution. A 64-tap learned filterbank (the patch embedding) cannot resolve below roughly 750 Hz within a token. A 50 Hz kick fundamental spans 960 samples — 15 consecutive tokens per cycle — so representing the single most important component of a kick drum requires coherent phase tracking across 15 tokens, paid out of attention/width budget. Drums are the worst-case content for this tokenisation; the STFT's 2048-sample window sees 2+ full cycles per token.
  • Alignment burden, as in §2.1, but at 8× the token count.

Verdict: not viable at this latency for this content. Would reconsider only if the latency budget relaxed ~10× or the content moved to broadband/HF-dominated material.

3.2 Vocoder-shape ConvNeXt (Vocos/WaveNeXt): mis-tasked, not broken — the conditioning-density argument

Your suspicion ("doesn't work as a conditional denoiser with simpler class/embedding conditioning?") is, I think, correct, and the mechanism is worth writing down because it predicts when the vocoder shape will and won't work:

Conditioning density determines the required mixing range

A mel-conditioned vocoder receives ~100 aligned coefficients per frame: every local decision (which partials, what envelope, where the onset is) is already made by the conditioning; the network performs local inversion, and a local conv receptive field suffices. A class/embedding-conditioned denoiser receives one 384-d vector per clip: the network itself must decide global structure — onset placement, decay law, spectral evolution — and keep those decisions coherent across the whole clip. That requires content-adaptive global communication. Attention does it in one hop at any position; a conv trunk must propagate it through stacked local kernels.

Concretely: the vocos-200k run's trunk (depth 8, k=7, hop 512) has a receptive field of 7 + 8·6 = 55 frames ≈ 0.59 s — about ±0.29 s around any output frame. A 2 s one-shot is 188 frames: the onset and the tail literally cannot see each other. AdaLN injects the same cond everywhere, which sets the global "what" but cannot coordinate the global "where/when". This is consistent with your own evidence: in the 8-clip overfit probe the ConvNeXt+iSTFT(realimag)+sep_norm recipe conditioned perfectly (8/8 retrieval — locally it's fine, and the conditioning-washout pathology was real but fixed), yet at 122k-clip scale it lost to the transformer. Flow2GAN — the reason the shape looked so promising — is mel-conditioned end to end; the wiki's open question ("does STFT-ConvNeXt survive unconditional generation?") flags exactly this gap in the literature.

And the supposed CPU advantage mostly evaporates in this regime: at 94–188 tokens, attention is <1% of FLOPs, so transformer-vs-ConvNeXt is a kernel-flavour choice, not a budget one. The ConvNeXt shape stays interesting for two futures only: streaming/chunked inference (where attention windows get awkward), and as a distillation target once a transformer teacher is good.

SECTION 4Protocol audit: noise → STFT → denoise → ISTFT

4.1 The construction itself is sound — and the better of the two variants

Because the STFT is linear, noising the waveform and then transforming is exactly RF in waveform space viewed through an invertible feature map: STFT(xt) = (1−t)·STFT(x0) + t·STFT(x1). Two non-obvious good properties follow. First, every input the model ever sees — training, any t, and pure noise at sampling — is the STFT of an actual waveform, i.e. lives on the consistent-spectrogram submanifold. The alternative (defining the flow directly on STFT coefficients with iid noise, RFWave-style) walks the model through inconsistent ambient space during sampling. Second, the loss-after-ISTFT means frame predictions are graded post-projection, so the model is never penalised for gauge (inconsistent frames that project to the right waveform). No flaw here; keep it.

4.2 The real leak №1: linear-power loss weighting

Waveform MSE = (Parseval) linear-spectral MSE. With peak-normed drums, the kick fundamental sits near 0 dB and the hat/cymbal detail at −30…−50 dB: a 10³–10⁵ weighting against the content whose crispness you can hear. Equivalently, per-bin SNR under a white source crosses 1 at t = 1/(1 + a) for a bin of amplitude a: a loud bin (a≈1) crosses at t=0.5, a −20 dB bin at t≈0.91, a −40 dB bin at t≈0.99 — quiet bins are effectively pure noise for almost the entire trajectory, and the logit-normal(0,1) t-distribution concentrates training exactly where they're invisible. Mitigations in ascending invasiveness: (a) MSS log-magnitude auxiliary (mr_stft_weight>0, log_weight>0 — implemented, currently off); (b) per-bin inverse-energy weighting of the spectral loss (the wiki's "spectral-energy-inverse weighting"); (c) move the flow's data space to compressed coefficients |X|αejφ, α≈0.3–0.5 (equalises dynamic range everywhere — model, loss, and noise addition — at zero inference cost; requires retraining).

4.3 The real leak №2: the t-distribution vs the 1-NFE endgame

At 1-NFE the model is evaluated at exactly one point: t≈0, x-prediction from pure noise. Logit-normal(0,1) training mass peaks at t=0.5 and thins toward both ends — the deployment point is in a tail. The v-space loss compounds this: it is the x-loss reweighted by 1/(1−t)², i.e. extra emphasis on the data side. That combination is literature-correct for multi-step samplers (and your probes confirmed t-uniform and x-space are no better at 25 steps), but nobody has checked it for the 1-NFE-via-FD path. Cheap, untested knob: shift logit_mean negative (toward the noise side) for the final stretch of pretraining, then FD post-train. Alternatively accept it — FD post-training is itself specialisation at t≈0 — but then the base's t≈0 quality is doing less work than assumed, which matters for §5.

4.4 Smaller items, for completeness

  • Scale mismatch / rms_lift: data RMS ~0.1–0.3 against a unit-variance source shifts every SNR crossing late (global version of §4.2). The lift fixes fitting (probe: teacher-forced corr 0.99–1.00) but hurt sampled identity, so it stays off pending the FD-v2 verdict — note that a logit-mean shift attacks the same imbalance from the schedule side without touching amplitudes.
  • x-prediction: correct for the 1-NFE endgame (JiT/pMF lineage); keep.
  • Gauge freedom: the model may emit inconsistent frames that ISTFT projects correctly — harmless ambiguity; the optional consistency penalty from the FD report removes it if it ever matters.
  • center=true edge frames: the first ~2 frames mix in zero-padding right where one-shot onsets live; the 8 ms start-pad augmentation already randomises this. Negligible.

SECTION 5RF vs MF: what the protocol should become

The RF base's 1-step output is a conditional mean — blur and energy collapse at t≈0 are intrinsic, not bugs — and the current plan (RF pretrain → FD post-train) asks FD-loss to do all of the un-collapsing. That is what the FD paper demonstrates (JiT-L, 291.6→0.77), so it is a legitimate plan, and it is already running; judge it first.

The principled successor if FD-v2 plateaus on base blur: a MeanFlow-family pretrain (pMF recipe) on the exact same STFT-frame backbone. MeanFlow learns the average velocity over finite intervals, so its 1-step output is trained to land on the data manifold rather than at its conditional mean — the t≈0 problem of §4.3 dissolves by construction, and FD-loss then polishes a sharp model instead of rescuing a blurry one. This is the wiki's canonical stack (iMF/pMF → FD polish), the codebase's method abstraction already anticipates it (build_method: "RF today, MF/others later"), and pMF is the cheapest published 1-NFE pixel recipe (33 GFLOPs, no learned φ) — the most audio-portable starting point. Decision rule: if E2/E3 of the FD ladder produce clean, identity-stable audio that still sounds soft, the base is the ceiling — port pMF. If they produce sharp audio, RF+FD suffices and MF is an optimisation, not a rescue.

SECTION 6Verdict and evolution path

Verdict

Keep the STFT-frame transformer. It won your empirical bake-off for identifiable theoretical reasons (basis, not luck), and its known weaknesses all have in-family fixes. Evolution order: (1) width d768 (probe-validated, ~2× FLOPs); (2) MSS-log auxiliary in pretraining (free at inference, attacks §4.2); (3) frequency-banded tokens (~2× FLOPs, removes the 2050→512 squeeze, adds cross-frequency sharing); (4) hop 256 and/or compressed coefficients if consistency artifacts or HF dullness persist; (5) pMF protocol port if the RF base's conditional-mean blur caps FD post-training. The ConvNeXt shape is shelved, not buried — revisit as a streaming variant or distillation target. The raw-patch transformer is excluded by arithmetic at this latency budget.
